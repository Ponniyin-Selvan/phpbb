Message No 188 from archives reproduced:

Veera pandiyan MAY NOT be Nandhini's father

Sumeetha and Pavitra have presented interesting insights into
Nandhini's
parentage. My full appreciation to their
efforts. What I'd like to present is an alternative.

* Pavitra says Serial novels (thodarkathai) suffer from some form of
inconsistency and that Kalki did'nt plan to make Veera pandiyan -
nandini's
father and this was a later decision. Unfortunately, this is not
true !
Nandhini mentions very clearly that Veera pandiyan is her lover -not
only in the
first part (Pudhu vellam)
(Nandhiniyin Kaadhalan) but even also in the fourth part !
Just before the murder of Aditya, The head of Pandiyan appears
before
nandhini -
and she exclaims and talks in
hallucination like this " Oh Dear ! Have you come ? Welcome !....and
so on ...".
In fact if you carefully read
the novel, you'll find that whenever Nandhini talks about Veera
Pandiyan she
always says that he is her lover -
Even at that critical moment when Pandiya is in great danger!

* There is only one place where there is a contradiction - when she
has her
final talk with Aditya. Even here,
Kalki does not provide this directly. He writes like this :
Nandhini said ..."My father is .....father is................."
Later Vandhiyathevan says to Kundavi that he overheard this
conversation and
heard that she was mentioning the
name of Veera pandiya here. Now, why the author does not provide
this fact
directly during Nandhini's final
dialogue and provides the info at a much later stage ? I think there
is a very
very definite purpose and
I'll try to answer this question in another mail (Kindly ref my last
lines in
this mail)

* Thus Kalki very deleberately presents a very consistent picture
throughout the
Novel that Veera pandiyan is
indeed Nandhini's Lover.

* The arguements that support the fact that VPandiyan is Nandhini's
father come
from Karuthiruman. But please
remember
- Karuthiruman is totally inconsistent in his story. At one point of
time he
says he fathered nandhini!
- This fellow has no interest in cholas and is very faithful to
pandiyas.
- There is no reason for him to reveal such a big secret to
Vandhiyathevan, that
too during a time pass chat !
Remember that he deserts vandhiyathevan instantly !
- Thus all of his statments are to be rejected. Infact this fellow
is more
dangerous that all others and there is more to him that what meets
the
eye ( I
plan to write about him in a separate mail - sometime in the future
).

* In the conclusion (mudivurai) he wrote for PS ( I don't know
whether
all of
you have read the conclusion. It is
very important for the novel as a whole), Kalki says :
"Just before her death, Nandhini reveals her birth secret to
Arulmozhi..."
If Kalki felt that the secret has already been revealed through
Vandhiyathevan
he need not have written these lines
at all. The fact is that Kalki deliberately confused the readers -
he
had many
more ideas about the characters them
selves and possibly had an idea of continuing the story even further
!

Now the question is : WHO COULD HAVE FATHERED NANDHINI, IF NOT VEERA
PANDIYAN
?????

I plan to take this topic in the near future.
Meanwhile your comments on this mail are most welcome.

Cheers !
Gokul

hi

A. Please refer to Karuthiruman Kathai chapter (part 5).
According to the statement made by him the following can be summarised:

1. That he saved Mandakini from lighthouse of Kodiakkari.

2. He met possibly Aniruddha and his men ( a statement corroborated by
Sundara Chola as he sent in his minister & friend to search for her).

3. He took her to a nearby island for money. She did not recognise even
her father as she was having amnesia. At the same time he also gives a
lift to man, who turned out to be the Pandyan king. What was he doing in
mid sea? Why he went to Kodiakkarai? If at he wanted to go to Lanka he
could have easily crossed the sea at Ramesvaram? Only Kalki can answer
this strange coincidence.

4. Karuthiruman leaves Veerapandya, the girl with amnesia (or mentally
unsound) and goes on to meet the Lankan king. Within few days
Veerapandya joins Karuthiruman at the court of Lankan king. Pandyan king
offers gold to him for the girl at Bhutathivu.

5. Karuthiruman meets her again within few days at Kodiakkari. Her
father had already died.

6. She regains her memory. She is found to be pregnant. She delivers the
twins - Nandhini and Old Madurantakan.

7. So when did she become pregnant? BY the Pandyan king when she was not
in her senses? If so, Veerapandyan got the deserving punishment at the
hands of Aditya.

8. She could not have become a mother due to Karuthiruman as he left
immediately after dropping her at Bhutativu.

B.In the chapter 62 Etti Painthathu

1. OId Mathurantakan enquires about his father, fearing that he may
claim the right. But Karuthiruman, informs him that he is a Rajakumaran
and he himself is the servant of his father - obviously Veerapandyan. he
was pleasantly surprised after having informed he is not the rightful
heir to Chola throne.

2. In the Chapter 64 Unnmaiyai Chol, Alwakadiyan extracts some
information from Karuthiruman.

C. In Chapter 90 Ponmazhai Pozhinthatu, in the long conversation between
VVD and Kundavai, she expresses a doubt over this issue - Veerapandyan
being the father of Nandini. VVD confirms that the name was uttered by
Nandini, hence Paluvettaraiyar got angry with her. Again Kundavai
express a doubt whether a lady could call her father a lover?. VVD
substantiates that it is the character of Nandhini and the situation
warranted as Aditya was so hot headed at that time.

What all this means?

1. Kalki wanted this issue to be live and unsettles one right through
like the secret about the actual assassin of Aditya. These two are the
core issues of the whole story and all the events leads to it until he
changes the track and pleased at the sacrifice of RR. Perhaps a later
thought.

2. Perhaps he introduced the character Karuthiruman only to confuse this
issue. He suggests that Mandakini became pregnant when she was not in
her senses. Both Veerapandya and Karuthiruman hardly spent few days with
her. How could they all be the father of her children?

3. In fact Kalki, being the master story teller, he wanted to highlight
the spoilt mind of Nandhini due to her lonely childhood and the insults
heaped at her. Nandhini must have developed a complex (can any
Psychologist name it), where she wanted to become a prince/queen. She
must have been afraid about her parentage so she tried to unknot the
secret. Perhaps by establishing her royal lineage she will be on
stronger ground to fight Kundavai. Therefore she repeatedly believed or
rather prayed Veerapandya was her father.

4. So Kalki wanted all of us to keep guessing. Even Nadhini was not so
sure, when she told what she believed to the truth to RRC.

As I said it is best kept secret of the novel. A prerogative of any
story teller is to leave loose ends. A tightly written story becomes
history, not a historical novel. Kalki is not writing history for that
matter. He drew a firm line as an historical novel writer and historian.
His post script proves it.
