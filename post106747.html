Hi Madan,
 
Take time to read the below articles:
 
http://www.thehindu.com/2006/08/24/stor ... 208100.htm
 
I guess in ancient India ( atleast in Tamilnadu ) the Gurukul system waswide open to all walks of people... See the list of Cankam poets who come from different backgrounds.
 
You are right in one way. Many Tamil-Brahmi inscriptions are written in slang when compared with the Tamil of the Cankam poetry. Mayilai Seeni Venkatasamy observes that this was a practice during those times. The other interpretation could be that these scribes had very bad language practice.
 
There was no other script in practice ( from the evidence so far ) during the Cankam period.  There is some or little evidence to trace the evolution of the Brahmi script form the late Harappan (Indus) script and there is lot of evidence to prove the evolution of current Tamil script from the Brahmi script ( see for example Mr. Vairam's recent postings onLord Murugan where he has posted this evolution as images on the top of the screen. )
 
Hope this helps!
