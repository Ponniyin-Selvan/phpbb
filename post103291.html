Vaanathi

by vidramki on Sat Oct 15, 2005 5:50 am

kamban used to describe "kaikeyi" in one word....
"NAADAGA-MAYIL"..
Wow, it means Drama Queen! Another new word to add to
my Tamil vocabulary. Obviously, it is quite lacking.

Very interesting how different languages use very
similar metaphors. If you had told me just the
word/phrase "NAADAGA-MAYIL", I would have taken it to
mean simply a dancing peacock. Thanks for giving
context around it, now I know what it really means.

BTW, the term Drama Queen is used frequently in the US
to describe young, teen girls. I find it interesting
that you'd use it for Vaanathi. Muthu Prakash wrote
some time back that her behavior was a reflection of
her young age, probably teenage.

Connecting the dots, from Kalki to Muthu Prakash to
Dhiwakar, I think I'm beginning to like Vaanathi more.
I know this was not the effect Dhiwakar hoped for when
he wrote that word, but once you put the words drama
queen and Vaanathi next to each other, all her actions
make so much more sense to me. I used to think that
she was an annoying character, always fainting etc.
But now I understand that Kalki was a superb observer
of human behavior.

Vidya

Dear Muthuprakash,

Vanathi's love very well summerised. Great !

Dear Vijay,

You have zeroed on the subject related to inscriptions.

AD 952 - Lankan War - Siriavelar demise.
AD 965 - Aditha Karikalan declared Yuvaraja (Ko-Parakesari)
AD 969 - AK Murdered
AD 969 - Madurantakan @ Uthama ascending. Arumozhi crowns him (as
reported in Thiruvalangadu copper plates)
AD 971 (or so) - Sundara dies (AD 973 says some Scholars)
AD 985 Arumozhi ascending. Becomes Rajaraja.
AD 988 Chera's Kandhalur Chalai destruction (c-in-c of this war was
Rajendra ).

Thirukkoilur inscription says : Vaanamadevi committed Sathi when
Sundara died (971 or 973 AD) - leaving behind a breast-fed child.
Our group discussed this in detail earlier).

Assume Rajendra was about 18 in 988. Then he was 970 born. Latest
Arumozhi married Vanathi @ Bhuvamadevi (Bhuvamadevi
thiruvayirudhitha Rajendra chozha thevar says Thiruvalanchuzhi
inscriptions) in 969 AD, just around when AK was murdered.

No grant of Vanathi is mentioned in any of RRC's inscritions.

In 988 AD, 3rd yr of RRC, as desired by Olgamadevi @ Danthi Sakthi
Vidangi, Crown Queen of RRC, at Olgapuram (near Marakkanam / Pondy)
a siva temple was constructed and named after her. Means she
ascended the throne along with RRC in 985 AD. She continued to be
identified in the entire 30 yrs span of RRC rule including
Thulabaram / Hiranyagarbam at Thiruvisalur inscriptions (29th yr of
RRC).

Let us look at these facts from Kalki's point of view :

Arumozhi married Vanathi after AK murder in about 969 AD.

Vanathi could not never imagined that her husband will be commencing
the Golden rule in about 15 years.

She could not have known that her son Rajendra shall spread the Rule
even overseas (Kalki does talk of this in one of the Chapters).

It was a Saamania Vaanathi fallen in love (or even forced to
consider) with Arumozhi without expecting any big Royal
accomplishments (since AK was Yuvaraja and Madurantakan was in the
queue).

And more likely she died prior to Sundara (within 2 / 3 yrs of
marriage) and it is Rajendra - who was breast fed by his
grandmother ! (as per Thirukkoilur inscriptions)

Kalki has characterised this VANATHI ..

and our Dear Muthukumar described HER LOVE in the appropriate words !

" Bhuvanamadevi Thiruvaryirudhitha Rajendra Chozha Thevar ... " her
accomplishment has gone into the Golden History of India (East -
Asia probably).

anbudan / sps
