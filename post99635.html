Writing Tamil History: Post National Perspectives

More than two millennia of documentary evidences show several undercurrents in Tamil historiography: (a) classical ideas; (b) Colonialism, Nationalism and Orientalism; (c) the Dravidian Movement and (d) post colonial competition of linguistic, religious, caste and class identities. The legacies of all of them operate in varying degrees in determining the Tamil psyche today. The contemporary world witnesses redefinition of nationalism, emergence of culture-identities, need for a global outlook and above all, a globally spread Tamil population which is aware of its identity. This paper attempts to kindle a debate on the historiographical needs of changed realities.
