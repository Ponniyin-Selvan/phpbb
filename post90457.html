The Bottomline is Kalkis Charectors are all human...They are heroes

I wonder which charector did he potray the best as the servant of the
empire?
Anbil Brahmarayar: The able administrator the trust worthy freind the
man with a steel heart

Periya Pazhuvettarayar:The greatest warrior in the story..mind clouded by
his new found love but when he realises his mistake makes up with a
greatest sacrifice
Poothi Vikrama Kesari The great general who sets aside his dream of his
brother's daughter's marraige to Arumozhi when she vows not to sit in the
throne but still fights for Arulmozhi to become king
Kalanthaga Kander:The thalapathi who scares the yamadarmaraja himself
when finding out the truth about his son in law goes to the extend of
fighting him to death to protect the kingdom.:
Malayamaan:The aging king who fights the sadness of his favorite grandson
to make way for the new king

Sembiyan Maadevi The Queen mother who puts the country before her own son
======================

Regarding characterisation by Kalki,

Periya Pazhuvettarayar plays major role and projected well;
Anbil - plays major role in the end;

Technically less historical evidences are avl on these in comparison with
Sembian Maadevi. It should have been difficult for Kalki to characterise
her .. Handled this tactfully considering the fact that she was the mother
of clouded Uthama.

Malayaman - Poothi Vikrama Sethupathy - Kalandagar are less known.

When Kalki was aware of Udyarkudi inscriptions - that Ravidasan was
responsible - why did he create clouds on Periya pazhuvettarayar!? Only for
story telling ? This keeps PP out;

Anbil and Sembian madevi remain in the race. Kalki chose to project a good -
humble Sembian Madevi - forgetting the facts that where from she got that
kind of power and wealth to perform so many Thiruppanis - inspite of her
not occupying any high position? Is this not in line with present day super-
power centres? Did Sundara allow S.Madevi to perform out of grace or did
he not have other choice?

That leaves with Anbil alone:

An able - true - patriotic Brahmadhirayar (may be comparable to Vallabai
Patel) Anbil Anirudha Brahmarayar.

I would place 1. Anbil 2. PP 3. SM 4.Poothi 5. Malayaman & 6. Kalandagar
(I am not sure historically :: but again these resemble Peria Marudhu and
Chinna Marudhu - Freedom fighters - Heros of South - who might have
provided inspiration to Kalki).

Kalki also would have derived inspirations from SG Kittappa - KB
Sundarambal - PS Veerappa & similar ones while characterising them....

Thanks Dear Sri, for taking us back to the eventful 10 century..

fondly, sps
