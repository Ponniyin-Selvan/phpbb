poykkavillai. annai thurggaiyin arul pooranamaaga irukkirathu.naangal
aatchi seytha urgalaiyellaam vanthu koodap paarththeergalaame?

madiyil kidaththi thelivikka vendum. poi varukiren. ennudan pesa
vendumenraal, pazaiyaarai aranmanaikku olai koduthu anuppavum.
pariseelanai seythuvittu santhikkiren.

ponghuzhali enge? avalaip paarkavendum. entha arasakumaaranai
kaappaatrik kondirukkiraalo??

poi varukiren,

kaiyoppam...

by dmaloo on Sun Mar 23, 2008 11:01 pm

Dear friends, I have joined today as a new member. I find a lot of
enriching, wonderful discussions going on and hope to learn and
share whatever little I know with all of you.

Just a little background about me - I was born and raised in Chennai
and Kovai. I was raised in a typical lower middle class tamil family
where the only form of entertainment in those days was open air
classical music concerts, movies in run down theatres, ilangai oli
parappu kootu thabanam and of course, Kalki and Ananda Vikatan. I
was not the ponniyin selvan generation, I was born in 70s. Being ill
I was not allowed to attend school very often and was home schooled.
This time allowed me to read the volumes of PS and other similar
works that were painstakingly bound from old magazines by my
grandmothers and aunts. I finished PS non stop during summer
holidays of 8th grade and fell in love since.

Several years later am in the states now and far away from
origins.My mother, grandmothers and all the fond women whom I shared
PS with have moved on to another world. Last year my father sent me
recently published PS, small books, no pictures, a far cry from the
beautiful illustrations of Maniyam in the original. But still
brought back a treasure of memories. My personal favorite is
Sivakamiyin Sabatham, far less characters and more depth in the
story, I felt, but PS is also a classic in its own right.

Please being here and hope to talk more soon,

Mala
