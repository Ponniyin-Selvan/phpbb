in the drawing is..Amazing..

Dear Raj, That is excellent description. Yesterday in my mail I do
not remember whether I mentioned your name and Prabakar's. Regret
omissions - inadvertant.

We should put this in the File Section for future reference.

TRIPURANTAKAR is a form of Siva, conquering the 3 Asuras - who had
flying Forts - Copper - Silver - Gold. Reminds us of the ALIENS in
the Independence Day movie. Enroute, Mr. Venkatesh was explaining us
the details when we crossed ACHARAPAKKAM. The Siva having failed to
worship Vinayaga - had a chariot Breakdown - ACHIRUTHA VINYAGAR -
removed his Vignam and Siva @ Tripurantaka was able to conquor the
Asuras.

The details of the Paintings, located in upper floor of the temple,
were explained to the Group in batches by some of ASI Staff and
varalaaru team members. After we came down, Dr. KB joined us. He was
describing the finer aspects of temples in General. When he was about
to conclude, a member requested him to mention something special on
the paintings. He replied: It may take hours to explain the details
of each painting. However to cite an example, he talked of
Tripurantaka painting.

In this painting, Tripurantaka - with his weapons - incl Bow and
Arrow - & soldiers was moving towards the Asuras. Saw some mild ego
in the faces of those accompanied him that the Lord needed their
Support. In the furious face of Tripurantaka, a smile was dawning.
He reversed his bow, kept back arrow and WITH THAT SMILE, THE ASURAS
WERE DESTROYED ! (I have seen the SMILE when the top of the face was
covered and the FURY when the bottom of the face was covered with our
palm).

As Raj rightly recollected, a famous European Art Critic, stood in
front of this panel for hours and observed : Thousands of Monalisas
will not be matching the smile of the Tripurantaka!

And Dr. KB concluded his narrations! Peruvudayar went inside
Palliyarai! We were moving out of the big temple spell bounded!

Thank you Meena.

Fresco Painting was done 10 centuries back on prepared wet plastered
surface using natural colours which got dried up in 3 - 4 days.
During Nayaks period overlapping paintings were done. ASI was lucky
enough to detect this and patiently removing those over lapped
paints. No restoration technique is adopted. What was left after
peeling off nayak painting was the form of original we got. They are
prone for damages.

hence ASI restricts the number of casual visitors. Reasearch
students / scholars / VIPs are only permitted. We come under the
first category and get permission from ASI with the CONDITION THAT
NONE OF THE PAINTINGS IS TO BE PHOTOGRAPHED. There is another
Copyright issue also. Government of India has published some photos
in foreign countries through INTAG.

Regret No photos are available / supposed to be available.

However ASI is organising an exhibition of these photos shortly.
Shall let the Group when arranged.
