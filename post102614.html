Flow of tourists to Thanjavur on the rise

G. Srinivasan

Augmentation of facilities being carried out: official

---------------------------------------------------------------------
-----------



THANJAVUR: Tourist flow to Thanjavur has been on the increase over a
period of years, if statistics available with the tourism department
are any indication.

During 2005, a total of 25,000 foreign tourists visited Thanjavur
while domestic tourist flow was three lakh. It increased to 27,000
and 3.75 lakh respectively during 2006. During 2007, the flow was
30,000 and 4.10 lakh respectively.

According to the Tourist Officer, Thanjavur district, Dhandapani,
various works had been completed at various places to improve
tourism.

They include improvement of infrastructure facilities at Navagraha
Kshetras-Thingalur, Kanjanur, Thirunageswaram and Suryanarkoil.

The works have been completed at a cost of Rs.33.75 lakh allotted by
the State Government.

Additional lighting facilities have been done at a cost of Rs.3 lakh
at the Palace complex at Thanjavur. Lighting facilities have been
streamlined and improved at Big Temple at Thanjavur at a cost of
Rs.16.50 lakh allotted by the Central Government.

Approach road was laid to Brahmapureeswarar Temple at
Thirupullamangai in Papanasam Panchayat Union, flood lighting has
been done to Darasuram Iravatheeswaraswamy Temple, and improvements
of infrastructure facilities have been done to Poondi Matha
Basilica.

Some works are going on. They include improvement of facilities at
Swaminathaswamy Temple at Swami Malai, Kasi Viswanathar Temple and
Adhikumbeswarar Temple.

Works are going on at Thirucherai Saranathaperumal Temple,
Nachiyarkoil Srinivasa Perumal Temple, Kandiyur Harasaba Vimochana
Perumal Temple and Punnainallur Mariamman Temple.

A sound and light show will be arranged at Big Temple.

A sum of Rs.1.60 crore has been allotted by the Central Government
for the purpose.

Out of this as first instalment Rs.1.35 crore has been released.
Tenders have been called for the work.

Kumbakonam will also be developed under Central Government's tourism
promotion scheme.

A sum of Rs.187 lakh has been allotted for this purpose. Out of this
amount Rs.149 lakh has been released initially.
