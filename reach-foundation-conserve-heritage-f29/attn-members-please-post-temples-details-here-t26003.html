Sri Vijayaraghava Swamy Temple, Vijayaraghava Puram,Kolathur (Post), Tiruttani (Taluk), Thiruvallur (Dist)

by Uma on Sun Apr 27, 2008 7:31 pm

i came across a article abt a very old Perumal temple near thiruthani,
in a tamil daily (Dhinakaran).

Village - Vijayaraghava puram, Kolathur (is in TN) near Nagari (Andhra
pradesh). From nagari, a local bus that goes to Pallipattu.. U need to
get down at Kolathur. Take left and go almost 3 kms to reach temple.
The road (the 3km distance) is narrow with a lake (not maintained
properly) and fields on the other side. We were confused initially to
see a ruined temple coz the daily mentioned that a kumbabishekam was
due on 11th of Feb. But there were no signs of a kumbabhisekham at
all... But when we turned, alas there was another temple newly painted...

The temple was empty except for 2 of the villagers and the person who
takes care of the temple (incidentally, he does the puja for the lord
in the absence of the priest). The moolavar is Vijayaraghava Perumal
along with Boodevi and Sridevi. There is a karudar statue facing the
Lord outside, and naga deivam at the right side of the temple.

The people there were very helpful but they were not able to give
sthala puranam of the temple. It was given in the tamil daily though.
It seems the temple has been renovated some 1000 years ago (yes
renovation and not construction) by Krishna Deva Rayar. They were able
to track this information from the kalvettu there, I believe. The
priest had left for the day and it was the caretaker who did the puja.
The priest was appointed only during the kumbabhishekam that too only
on a temporary basis. They are still in the look out for a permanent
priest.

We also asked about the temple in ruins on the other side. It is a
Siva temple built by Klothunga Chola I and completed by Klothunga
Chola III. Renovation of the temple is in progress, and Kumbabhishekam
might soon follow suit. But people are in want of funds. We were
blessed to visit this temple also. The moolavar is Siva in the form of
lingam with a multi-headed snake over it. It was heart breaking to see
few other statues in a dilapidated condition. If people can help, may
be we can prevent such a precious temple that had withstood testings
of time until now from deteriorating further. This is the

The address of the temple:

SRIDEVI BHOODEVI SAMETHA SRI VIJAYARAGHAVA SWAMY TEMPLE

VIJAYARAGHAVA PURAM (VILLAGE), KOLATHUR (POST)

TIRUTTANI (TALUK), THIRUVALLUR (DIST), TAMILNADU

Note: No decent restaurants when u cross thiruthani, esp for the ones
taking public transport since the next stop will be nagari. I found a
few hotels en route to nagari from tiruttani. Please ensure u have
enough food and water coz u cannot come across any shop(not even a tea
shop) once u get down at kolathur till the temple for the 3 km distance.


For people using public transport – From Nagari, many private/govt
buses are available to go to kolathur. No autos or buses available to
cover the long distance to go to vijayaraghava puram after getting
down at kolathur. Please have this in mind when u consider taking
old/sick people or kids using public transport.
Uma
