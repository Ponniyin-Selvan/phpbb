Sellandi Amman and Karuvurar

by jaybee555 on Thu Apr 10, 2003 1:00 pm

--- Inponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, Thilak wrote:
Thanks Dr.Jaybee. From: the relief, and a few other sirpams I have
seen in Saraswathy Mahal and the big temple, I see that Rajaraja didnt
have a mustache (and most other kings, for that matter), unlike the
Rajaraja and other kings we've seen in movies (mostly played by
Sivaji). Am I right?
Thilak

Some of them had moustaches and beard.

In the RajaRajEsvaram@Tanjavur Periya Koyil, RajaRaja had caused
fresco paintings to be painted along the walls surrounding the
sanctum sanctorum.

During the Thanjai Nayakar times, the Nayaks painted their own
fresco paintings over the older Cholza paintings. Thus they lay,
vanished from vision until the late 20th century.

Now people are trying to restore the older paintings. But their work was
hampered by lack of funds, man power, etc.

However, from what has been revealed, we see RajaRaja standing
with two of his wives. He is sporting moustache and beard.
He is also wearing a very transparent see-through shirt.

In my collection of photographs of the Tamil kings, I see almost
all of them wearing a side kudumi or kondai with moustache and
beard.

Here are the pictures of Kuloththunga Cholza III and
Jatavarman SundaraPandiya(Eureka).

http://www.geocities.com/jaybee555/kuun ... skers.html


Mahendravarman is clean shaven. So were the Khmer emperors.

The Nayakkars were adorning moustaches.
Here, you can see Thirumalai Nayakkar of Madurai with several
of his wives.

http://www.geocities.com/jaybee555/tiru ... ifes1.html

The style that we see in movies follows the latest style that was
around in the 18th and 19th centuries.

The Marudhu Brothers of Sivaganggai Siimai have two statues
in KunRakudi. More than life-size.
They are sporting side-burns and whiskers.

That style was going on as late as 1980 AD.
As you can see in this rather large picture.

http://www.geocities.com/jaybee555/kuun ... meone.html

Regards

JayBee
