Regarding "buidling temples", the temples are not just "worship

In the villages, where agriculture was the main activity demanding
hard physical labour but the village did not have social clubs,
theatres, Film shows, Fasion shows etc., and in the absence of
electricity and communications, Temples played vital role as we all
know - apart from them being place of worship. They protected
people during calamities (Kalki SS) and even served as local bank
during emergency.

Irigation, trade, foreign trade, education etc., were vastly
improved by Chozhas. They are the only Indian Kings to have
conquored overseas States. Influence of Vedas / Sankrit was
inevitable at a particular point of time in South Indian History -
because of matrimonial alliances and division among the native
rulers. The Kings ruled at such period adopted themselves.. like we
now follow certain Western traditions, appreciate and take advantage
of them - including the language of communication - among ourselves.

Dr. Kalaikkovan quoted from a Chingleput inscription of Rajaraja:

A Village Headman meets King Rajaraja and seeks permission to light
a perpetual in appreciation of the services rendered by the King to
the State. RR replies: Pls light it for Welfare of the people of
your village.

The headman returns and lights TWO Perpetual lamps in his village
temple: One for the Welfare of the people of his Village. Other for
the Welfare of the Chozha Emperor Rajaraja!

Believe it, Dr. Kalaikkovan added: Rajaraja was very much inspired
by Rajasimhan (Pallava) & Cheramanperumal(Chera!)

The Virtues are always appreciated and honoured by the Nobles.

views why I differ from your opinion (and possibly Aru. Ramanathan's-

1) "Chozhas could not keep a good relationship with pandyas, though
they could do with others" is a general statement. When speaking of
wars between them, we should take the time & appropriate
circumstances.

It does not appear that Chozhas always fought only with Pandyas. As
a matter fact, the thiruppurambiyam war itself is a war against
Pallavaas more so; Chozhas had to fight against Pandyas as they were
supporting Pallavaas.

2) Chozhas wanted to establish their kingdom/conquer Sri Lanka.
Since Pandyas were in support of Sri Lanka, Chozhas had to fight with
Pandyas too. No other go.

3) It appeared to me that you were saying Chozha leaned more towards
temples & religious issues than people welfare (unlike Pandyas). The
inscriptions during Rajaraja's realm will show ample evidences as to
what he did for people. The system was such that mishandlers of
public funds were severely punished. The persons who were delegated
authority were accountable for what has been allotted to them. The
irrigation system was another wonderful plot in Chozha dynasty.

because they were not supportive of Shaivism/Vaishnavism as much as
Chozhas (maybe these are not the exact words). This allegation
