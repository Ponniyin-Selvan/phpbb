On the association of Mutts of today with the royals of history, I
have some opinions,

The mutts of today are mostly Vedantin mutts, in south India those
either belong to Sankara/Vaishnava-Ramanuja/Madhav parampara.

To my knowledge of history, these people had very little socio-
political influence, at the top would be the persecution of Ramanuja
by K II.

During chozha times the Vedic Brahmins had more influence than these
Vedantic Mutts. From: What I know of history, the first such personage
to have socio-political impact was Vidyaranya of Sringeri Mutt who
was the inspiration behind Harihara and Bukka of Vijayanagar.

Only during Vijayanagar period, did they gain considerable influence
and have effectively iconised into being the heads of the Brahmin
community. Again during Vijayanagar period Sayanacharya and Madhava
did work on Vedic literature.

So even though such mutts existed in Chozha period, their influence
has not be great enough to be passed down history.
