by satish_arun on Fri Feb 18, 2005 5:47 pm

Dear all,

I am an ardent fan of Gokul and the first thing I read in varallaru
is 'Rajakesari'. Everyone knows that its going at a good pace. Hats
of to Gokul.

In the past couple of days, Gokul and I had a good discussion about a
topic, which I thought will be of interest to the group also. After
reading Rajakesari this month, I gave a comment to Gokul about a
scene he described. Mazhapadiyar and Ambalavaanar goes to the
Vanianchavadi, where they see a Palakara kadai near by. I laid my
humble comment before Gokul that, in earlier days Food stuff was
never sold in our land. Selling food was considered a sin and all
along there were annachatiram and chavadis for resting and eating for
the passer by. It was a family from Udipi who first started selling
food (hotel concept) just a century or so ago. Thats why udipi hotels
aer famous till date.It seems that family was cursed by all becuase
selling food was considered a sin. Since the story was going very
good, I felt such a scene differs from the real history.

Seeing this comment, Gokul was quite excited and wrote me back, based
on which our discussion proceeded. Since there was nothing personal,
I publish the mail chain between Gokul and myself, so that you all
can get a first hand info on what our discussion was. I wrote my
first mail as comment through the side(the crux of which is given
above) and hence dont have that mail to publish here.

Hi Gokul,

Good to see your immediate response. As I said I read the Thalayangam
and then Rajakesari first before going into any other topic -:)
The thought I shared, I think I read it in marabilakkiyam group. As
you said, we have to look at this aspect when we probe into history.
But we all know that our tradition was virundhombal where in each
family awaits for a vistor to have food before they take. Every house
had
a thinnai only for the travellers to take rest and those thinnai had
a jannal near from which the inmates can look at the thinnai and find
out
whether any visitor has come or not. If they see anybody, they will
immeidately cook for them and then only will wake them up inviting
them for food. So if this is the case, then will any person pay to
buy food, when they can get it free knocking at any door? This is a
logical
question, right.

After shooting the mail, I too thought, food to fill the stomach is
annadhanam, but what about other delicacies....like adhirasam, some
sweets etc.
Today we say Tirunelveli is famous for alwa and kovilpatti famous for
karasevu etc. Shouldnt there be something like this in the past too
which
people might love to taste?We have to check with literary people to
see whether we get any thing in the sanga ilakkiyam regarding this.
Silapadhikaram should be a good start, because I heard that there is
one episode which describes about the kadai veedhi when kovalan
and kannai walk into madurai (i havent seen even the book till date -
).

As I said, whent he udipi family first started selling food, everyone
cursed them saying its a great sin to sell food. Lets try to get some
more info on this.
Do update me if you come across something.

Regards,
Satish

Hi Gokul,

Good Morning. After going home I was thinking about this subject and
thought will disuss with Madurabarathi of Marabilakkiyam as he is
quite well versed in sanga ilakkiyam.I think you know him, because
last week when I visited him, he was saying that you called him up
and invited him for the mupperum vizha at thanjai.
I called him and asked him whether he has come across anything such
thing of selling food in sanga illakkiyam. He also said the same
thing which I wrote yesterday(ofcourse what I wrote yesterday was his
thoughts only. He has written about it some time back). He said there
is no such mention of food stall in silappathikaram. He also said it
was treated as a sin to sell food stuff till 200 years back.

I dont think this culture belongs to only TN, but entire India. Its
sanathana dharmams culture. We know that people travelled on
pilgrimage from Kanyakumar to even amarnath without any worry of stay
of food. This means that they were sure to get food and shelter
anywhere they go. Even in PS we see that vandhiyathevan keeps on
travelling and he finds shelter and food whenever needed. As you said
its really a great thing we should be proud of. For sure food was not
sold in those days.

Finve mins after i finished my call with Madurabarathi, he called me
again and told me that he suddenly remembered one incident from our
puranam.
He quoted the "tiruvilayadal Puranam" in which in 'Man sumandha
padalam', old vanthi sells pittu for her living.!!! He himself was
quite thrilled about remembering this and me too. He said this is the
only incident he can remember from our puranams or ilakiyams. He
concluded that probably for dire straits, such people sold food for
the livelihood. And again he firmly said, this is one rare incident
and food was never sold but was given as part of 'virundhombal'.

As you said, its getting quite interesting. Lets keep our eyes and
ears open to get more info on this.

Regarding the coverage of our trip. I will try to do it asap.

Regards,
Satish
