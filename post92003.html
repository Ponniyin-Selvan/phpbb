by sps10142004 on Fri Nov 23, 2007 7:52 pm

We should definitely record our appreciation and gratitude to the
British for having shown keenness in knowing Indian History,
particularly the South Indian History - through epigrahy - thanks to
the efforts of their renowned Archeologists - like Heultz and others.

They have furnished unbiased History from what they have decyphered.

Venkayya and other epigraphists assisted them in great way. But they
had details of various inscriptions. Not much co-related.

KA Neelakanta Sastry, who was Professor in Madras / Mysore
Universities, assembled almost every piece of inscription and given
shape to HISTORY of Southern India!


His " The Colas" is a manumental work.

Subsequently Sadasiva Pandarathar of Annamalai University came out
with his book Chozhar varalaaru .. followed by Dr. Maa.
Raasamaanickanar.

This is the Greatness of KAN.

Among several thousand such inscriptions, Kalki chose one inscription
from Udayarkudi @ Kaatumannargudi (which is located on the banks of
Veeranam lake - just one KM north of Veeranarayana perumal temple) -
which was published by KAN Sastry in Epigraphia Indica and wrote the
magnum opus PONNIYINSELVAN.

This inscription is still there on the Northern wall of the sanctum of
Anantheeswarar temple - wherein the 4 characters namely Ravidasan -
Parameswaran and his two other brothers figure along with Aditha
karikalan !

We have extensively discussed this : check Udayarkudi inscriptions /
Aditha karikalan murder etc., etc., in the Archieves.

Iamgine KALKI wrote 5 volumes on one inscription - published and
narrated by KAN !

That is the Greatness of Kalki.

Both are complimentary to each other ..

sps
