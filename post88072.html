Dear Gokul, Re Chandikeswarar :: Seyngalur (Near Thiruppanandal - (Chennai -

Chandikeswarar is supposed to have been born ! I saw one of your earlier
mails praising Historian SRB. He has published the excellent RR period
bronzes which includes a bronze of Chandikeswarar. Under the feet of Siva
chandikeswara sits and siva ties up a Kondrai-maalai around his head ! That
is a famous sculpture in Big temple! Whenver I see the statue, I used to
feel that the " Sirpi " has portraited Rajaraja himself as
Chandikeswarar. Like Queen Shantala Devi w/o King Vishnu Vardhanan posed
for the famous Belur (Karnataka) sculptures!

Dear Arun Krishnan, you can see Tamil inscriptions in majority of the
ancient Siva / Vishnu temples (chidambaram / Thiruvarangam for example). Re
Ravidasan - only Udayarkudi - now known as Kaattumannarkudi -
Anantheeswarar temple - for which Kumbabishekam was performed on the same
day along with Darasuram temple (about 2 months back) - located on the
banks of Veeranam @ Veeranarayana Chaturvedi mangalam - which figures in PS
Chapter 1. Thirumunaippadi naadu ends at Sethiathope (Toll Gate even
today - Rs. 5 to be paid one way to enter Chozha Naadu!). If you turn South
East - you will be on the banks of Veeranam.. Drive along Veeranam Eastern
bund, you will reach Kattumannarkudi - exactly as Kalki describes. Now is
the best time because it is full of water and looks like an ocean .. about
12 Kms long and about 10 Kms wide...
