Dear Vijay,

I remember VAYPPADU ..

Oronn onn nnu 1 x 1 = 1
eeronn renndu 2 x 1 = 2
moovonn moonnu 3 x 1 = 3 etc., etc.

Veesam (1/16) - mundhiri (1/32) - maagani (1/64) also familiar.
used by Goldsmiths. These are also figuing in several inscriptions -
particularly in grants related to gold ornaments ! (what is the
weight of a pearl / precious stone / variety - how many pearls -
fitted in how much weight of gold - and generally of what design ..
likje wise !)


The very name VAAYPPADU makes us now to realise that Mathematics
Tables were taught in POETIC form ..

so should have been majority of other verses .. and have to be
MEMORISED ..

People could read .. They were literates ..

Re Writing, they themselves should have used Aedu (palm leaf) and
Ezhuthani .. Or should have depended on " SCRIPTERS " ..

I am also familiar with a community as VALLUVAR .. (In Pudukkottai
Dt) who are experts in Astrology - General Medicine and also in
using Aedu - Ezhuthani !

Believe it they can WRITE so fast using Ezhuthani on Aedu, as we
are writing with a Ball pen on paper today .. !

And Satish, of course U Ve Sa's observations will be very useful...

topic and keep the informations furnished by Vijay - tho" Wiki -

by sampath_srinivasaraghavan on Mon Sep 24, 2007 6:24 pm

Well, in my first exposure to computer (Data General Nova series with 16
K memory and teletype I/O) the assembly programme was to be punched on a
paper tape and without editing facilities. I had to be very careful not
to make a single mistake, as the paper tape got punched as soon as a
character was typed. Now, most of us (there are exceptions of course)
cannot enter a single word without spelling mistakes or missed
characters. We are careless because we have excellent editing back up.
If backspace and erase commands are removed, our productivity will fall
drastically.

Moral: If 'first time right' is the motto, then no retraction must be
allowed.

Sampath
