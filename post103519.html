Dear Sir,

Your point is valid, but i feel its your opinion ...
I like to narrate a couple of incidents I have come across. Again no
offense meant to the muslim community , just recalling what I came
across.

almost 10 years back, when I was doing my B.Sc in Loyola college,we
had a classmate named Nayimuddin. From: the name you could have
guessed he is a muslim. We friends used to go to the Football ground
for lunch and used to chat leisurely over there. This particular
classmate was also with us. During one of the cricket seasons, we
were discussing about cricket and Indai-Pak match was going on that
day. We were talking in favour of India,(as any Indian does) and were
saying that Muslims will always support Pak, though they are indian.
Since we had a Muslim member in the group, we wanted to clarify with
him and asked him straingt, "whom do you support?". He was stunned by
the question (was obvious from his face) and managed and stammering
told us(we were nearly 10 people around) "my parents always suports
Pakistan, but I support India"...oru panai sothukku oru soru padam.

I have even heard from my Girl Friends(ayyo tappa nenaikadeenga naan
appadi patta payan ella, I meant my friends who are girls)who studied
in SIET, that during India-Pak mathches, all the college girls(almost
all are muslims, since its a muslim college)used to listen to the
commentry on the transister and shouts whenever Pak team hits a sixer
or gets a wicket and openly will be shouting slogans in favour of
Pakistan.

If such were the sitiation, 50 years after Independence, with a
generation who never witnessed the partition, freedom struggle etc,
we can very well imagine what would have been the mind set of people
during pre independence era.
Its a bitter fact that 1947 partition not only split the geography,
but also the hearts...which is still prevailing...

Again no offence meant. Just shared the incident I happened to
witness.

regards,
Satish
