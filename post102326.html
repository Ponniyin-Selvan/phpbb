If one seriously works on tabluating the no. of Nallurs, one can

Same way Vallam name also should have been formed.. Tho' there are
speculations that Thanjai was named after some Vallaba Asuran etc.,
it is quite natural to name this after Kundavai's husband
Vandhiyathevan, since he hailed from the VALLAM (Near RANIPET).. His
ancestry being Rastrakuta clane etc., and the area ruled over by
them were discussed earlier - particularly IIT Prof Rangarajan
(vijayalakshmi) putforward interesting postulates .. a couple of
years back.. He also suggested AUDIO Version of PS !
Pls check archieves.

Gokul's mention of Arinjayeeswaram (Pallipadai Temple for Arinjayan
f/o Sundara GF/o RRC - last built in his 29th yr) at Melpadi
(Western Command) also reminds us of his promise to continue more on
these temples in serial.. which he already commenced..

Western Command is also an interesting subject (if VDVN was Chief of
Western Command) since Rajendra was known as MADANDA NAAYAKAN
Commander-in-Chief, (and technically Vndthvn should have under his
Command) till was accorded Royal Title and right to inscribe under
his name in 1012 AD (2 yrs after the Big temple was consecreated)..
when RRC should have been past 60 and Rajendra past 40 (since he was
mentioned Devar in 988 AD inscriptions ).

More than the romantic part of Kundavai - Vandhiyathevan, there are
some interesting inscriptions which are found rarely in Thivuallam
and Thirumazhapadi (Our Venkat's favourite Siva temple built by
Uthama near Pazhuvur)!

Uthama had a son. He was called Madurantaka Kandradithan. During
RRC's tenure he was incharge of Temple affairs (setting right the
mismanagement / ensuring implimentation of grants to be precise). He
found a couple of staff guilty (in course of one of his Audits) and
a fellow made to keep his hands on a wooden piece and the palsm were
nailed ! This sculpture is seen inside the complex prominently!~

And strangely after 995 AD (10th year of RRC), we are not able to
locate inscriptions related to Madurantaka Kandradithan !
