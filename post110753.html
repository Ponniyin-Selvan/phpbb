Wikipedia
WIKI is an online collaborative tool. For long, the only encyclopedias available to public were in the form of printed volumes - Encyclopedia Brittanica and the like. Then, with the advent of compact disc technology, we saw Microsoft Encarta, a CD based encyclopedia. With so much happening in the world, with new inventions, wars, cyclones, displacements, momentous events creating new countries and change of regimes, all printed and CD versions ofencyclopedias were becoming obsolete.
A collaborative people's encyclopedia came up under the name Wikipedia (http://en.wikipedia.org/), in 2001. The contributors were people from the public. Poorly written or factually incorrect articles were quickly corrected and replaced by more knowledgeable people. Initially, most experts doubted that Wikipedia will ever become a respected reference point. But by around 2006, almost everyone has accepted that the world's most eminent knowledge database is Wikipedia. It still has some errors, incomplete articles, poorlywritten pieces and downright junk. But people are improving - for free - every aspect of this resource.
Subsequently, the idea of collaborative Wiki has been taken up by others tocreate more specialised databases - for science, for religion and for sports.

Tamil heritage Wiki
We propose to use the same model to build a Tamil Heritage Wiki. This will attempt to bring together all aspects of Tamil heritage - arts, literature,music and dance, language, grammar, heritage sites etc. This will consist of linked online articles, photographs, audio/video content, in both Tamil and English.
On the day of inauguration, we will have some content. This will be augmented on a regular basis.

Dear Friends,

At 5 pm promptly the meeting commenced.

http://tamilheritage.wikispaces.com/

An online encyclopedia on India's cultural heritage.

The Tamil Nadu Heritage Group, a newly formed association of people
interested in India's cultural heritage, circulated a 2 page write up
stating the main objectives are :

Preservation
Documentation and
Sensitisation.

Monthly session to listen to eminent Scholars is also planned.

Dr. V.Irai Anbu, Secy to Govt of TN (Tourism & Culture)
Mr. N. Murali, MD, The Hindu
Ms. Leela Samson, director, Kalakshetra
Mr. N. Ravikiran, Chitra Veena exponent were on the dias.

The Managing Committee included :

Thakkar Baba Vidhyalala - Mr. Ramanathan,
"Kizhakku Padippagam" Mr. Badri
Mr. T.K.Ramachandran IAS and ourbeloved
Prof. S. Swaminathan (IIT - Delhi).

From our Group, V Venkat, Madhan and Chandra attended apart from me.

Dr. Irai Anbu was precise. He beautifully classified culture and
heritage into : ( 1) What we see and (2) What we feel.

He extended his best wishes saying his Tourism Department will
provide a LINK to the Web-site !

He also remarked that in the Food festival being conducted in the
Chennai ISLAND GROUNDS, ETHNIC /TRADITIONAL TAMIL FOOD STALL is
inaguarated today drawing huge crowd.

Mr. Badri inaguarated the link and observed:

those who have information to share will be able to do so by
uploading their information on the site free of cost. The idea is
that gradually there would be a single point of reference for any
matter connected with Indian heritage.

Ms. Leela Simson was amazing with her fluent details on Indian
heritage, apart from Dance. She assured the support of her
Institution as well.

Mr. Ravikiran outlined on culture and heritage and impressed upon
preserving the same and offered supposed in his specialised field of
music.

Prof. Swaminathan has been maintaining on the importance of spreading
awareness among the youth and the children and every positive step in
that direction is an accomplishment.

With National Anthem, the meeting came to be concluded in about 90
minutes.

About 30 interested persons attended the meet... but the message like
to be spread more than by mere attendance.

On behalf our Group, we place on record our support and very best
wishes to all those behind this initiative, especially to Prof.
S.Swaminathan.

With Dasara Greetings,

Wikipedia
WIKI is an online collaborative tool. For long, the only
encyclopedias available to public were in the form of printed
volumes - Encyclopedia Brittanica and the like. Then, with the
advent
of compact disc technology, we saw Microsoft Encarta, a CD based
encyclopedia. With so much happening in the world, with new
inventions, wars, cyclones, displacements, momentous events
creating
new countries and change of regimes, all printed and CD versions of
encyclopedias were becoming obsolete.
A collaborative people's encyclopedia came up under the name
Wikipedia (http://en.wikipedia.org/), in 2001. The contributors
were
people from the public. Poorly written or factually incorrect
articles were quickly corrected and replaced by more knowledgeable
people. Initially, most experts doubted that Wikipedia will ever
become a respected reference point. But by around 2006, almost
everyone has accepted that the world's most eminent knowledge
database is Wikipedia. It still has some errors, incomplete
articles,
poorly written pieces and downright junk. But people are improving -

for free - every aspect of this resource.
Subsequently, the idea of collaborative Wiki has been taken up by
others to create more specialised databases - for science, for
religion and for sports.
