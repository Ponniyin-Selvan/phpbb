by safanmezhi on Thu Feb 23, 2006 11:30 pm

ettayapuram is a small place.but because of zamin
there were so many artists lived there. they supported
musicians and poets. muthusami deetchadar spent his
last days at ettayapuram. there is his samadhi near
bus stand. there was one more poet from there.
umarupulavar.there is his samadhi too.
regarding bharathiyars house, now maintained as his
memorial place is his uncles house. in the same street
he lived in two more houses. once our great sri
suddanada bharathi avl came tp ettayapuram and stayed
in one of the two houses. at present in the other
house the present residant is the sister of popular
writers..bhaskara thondaiman and pulavar
ragunathan.present ettayapuram is dry place but hiding
glorious memories of great people
seethalakshmi

That is interesting thread, dear Venkat.

For those in Chennai - erstwhile Madras - Music - Dance - Drama
festivals are NOT New and most of them think as part of one's social
well-being. But for those living in Rural areas - particularly like
in Pudukottai - Sivagangai - Devakottai - Ramanathapuram etc.,
which are cultural Centres - but lacking the prominence of State
Capital - a FESTIVE SEASON is ear-marked and all the Popular Artists
from Chennai make bee-line to such Festivals.

This is clearly brought out in Thillana Mohanmbal movie - where the
Drama / Dance / Music Events take place in Thiruvarur - Azhagarkoil -
Nagapattinam - but an INVITATION from Madras and a PERFORMANCE in a
prominant Sabha is what they were aiming at. Once that recognition
attained, they visit their native places and take other artists also
to pay respect to their Nativity!

Karaikkudi Kamban Vizha is also one such Festival! Area people
celebrated these as their family functions ! True harmoney could be
relished in such festivals.

anbudan / sps
