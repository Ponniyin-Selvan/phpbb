Good to hear your daughter is growing up like Poonguzhali, that is more with the times.

I have not commented or criticised Vanathi's love for Arulmozhi. Even I like the Yaanaipaga chapter, but after all she ia also a princess from a family of warriors. I can understand a commoner being docile, fearful etc. but a princess (nevertheless from a chitrarasu) & one so closely associated with Kundavai, how can she be so insipid? Even her refusing to be rescued by Poonguzhali was a foolish act. Poonguzhali was far from her equal for Vanathi to have a rivalry, which is normally among equals.
As for the arathi incident, Arulmozhi would have reacted the same way had it been anyone other than Vanathi there.
May be Kundavai madiri oru nathanar kitta adangi poradhukkagave appidi irudhalo?
Muthu Prakash Ravindran wrote:
Hmm..somebody has to take up the defense of Vanathy
again..
I think she probably is the most mis-understood
heroine based on the comments passed so far.
Vanathy can be characterized as a muse to Arulmozhi,
he says so at the very end. To say that Arunmozhi
married Vanathy for the sake of Kundavai is
disingenous. Arunmozhi confesses this to VT that if
there is someone who can disturb him, it is her. He
can talk in equal terms with Poonkuzhali, match
Nanthini's wits, but cannot bear to see her suffer.
Remember the dropping the arathi incident and what
Arunmozhi tells Kundavai.
Love is such a complex feeling, only a person who
experiences it can actually comprehend it. I sent a
mail long back comparing Vanathy to Ophelia in Hamlet.
Both are just consummed by their love and there is
nothing else that matters to them. Ophelia actually
gets drown in the brook and dies because of the
altercation with Hamlet. Nothing matters because their
love is the only thing that leaves them breathing.
Call it insipid,sombu-thooki or what not. Except
Manimegalai or may be Oomai rani, every other love in
PS is based either on the conveniance of it or because
its practical. Kundavai loves VT because it makes sure
she does not leave chola nadu. Nandhini, we all know
what she was after. Poonkuzhali loves the chola throne
and marries the guy who inherits it. Nothing comes
nearer to the love which Vanathy bears for Arunmozhi
and I do not think any one else would have made
Arunmozhi happier in the entire novel.

Lastly, the most favorite chapter in the entire PS for
me is the 'Yaanai Paaga! Yaanai Paaga!'. This is a
chapter that is full of playful dialogues and the kind
of flirting that is a treat to read. I suggest you
read this chapter a little more closely.

by mprakas on Fri Jan 25, 2008 4:23 pm

Docile is such a subjective term. I dont think she is docile. Take
the case of Kundavai trying to changing her mind abt inheriting the
throne. Vanathy refuses to entertain and stick to her sabatham. Also
in the end when she actually saves PS from the flood. Or the 14th
chapter (another fav of mine) when our hero VT throws his veera vel
into a dummy croc. Vananthy approaches the croc without fear and
says she is afraid only of roaches. But then we all have our fears.

Insipid. We all have our prejudices and calling her stupid because
of not allowing Poonkuzhali might be one. In fact,, will you call
RRC a stupid for refusing to ascend the throne because that would
have given another 15 years of his golden rule to the Cholas?
By the same yard stick, everyone in PS makes their choices and
refuse something or other. That doesn't make them insipid either.

This comparison reminds me of another great romance 'Ivan Hoe' and
the actual rivalry between Rowena and Rebecca. Rebecca is the one
with all the handicaps (a jew, poor etc) and is almost like
Poonkuzhali. Whereas Rowena is a saxon princess and hopelessly
romantic like Vanathy. Guess who marries Ivan Hoe.

But that aside, Vanathy remains one of the most romantic female
character created by Kalki. On the same terms, Poonkuzhali might be
the strongest, Nandhini the complex one.

Muthu Prakash R
