by satish_arun on Tue Jan 16, 2007 1:48 am

All,

Wish you all a very happy pongal.

So few of our group members have gone to the book fair at different
times. it would have been great if we all could have grouped
together.

Yesterday, Swetha, Natarajan Srinivasan, Chandra, Sankaran and
myself visited the book fair. A stall selling a single book and
attrackign a huge crowd is something great. Hats of venkatesh. We
are really proud of you. Swetha had asked for two copies of KM and
when returning, Venkatesh said he is busy and running out of stock,
so will deliver the book at her house. That shows the book was
selling like hot cakes.

As usualy, PS and SS and PK were the most available books in the
fair. Somehow I feel this year the fair was not that charming. I
dont know why, but i couldnt step upon a book which attracted me to
buy it immediately. Earlier, every other book used to attract
attention. Not sure whether the layout of the new location is not
proper or the stalls were not arranged to put books to catch the
attention.

Muthu Prakash, your point on a dust bin at frequent distance - I
too thought about it. Every year, in the fair, when I collect lots
of the ad pamplets, I used to search for dust bins but not even a
single bin will be in sight. I really dont understand, why such a
huge fair cant have a single dust bin. Every year I used to carry
lots of papers with me to house and dump it at my house to put it in
the bin. Incidently, today Sun TV telecasts 'Anniyan' a movie on
self discipline.

Regards,
Satish
