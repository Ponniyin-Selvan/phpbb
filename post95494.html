inagurate the Peravai. Mr. TKV Rajan - media specialist also
attended the meet as a special invitee.

peravais.

Hi all.

I am reading the very interesting discussions going on for long in this thread and feel like penning my own two-paise views on them.

First of all the characterisation of Vaanathi. I feel Kalki has introduced a soft character like her and Manimekalai to balance the stronger ones likeNandini, Kundavai and Poonguzhali. Vaanathi and Manimekalai are smitted bylove to the two heroes of the novel, PS and VD, and their entire characterisation is based on this one strong aspect while the trio of Nandini, Kundavai and Poonguzhali have been described as strong-willed and having severalother interests apart from their love interests.

I don't know whether one would like to term Vaanathi and Manimekalai as "boring" ones in the novel where
there are other characters with better strengths of mind. Some readers may consider them as less attractive for these precise attributes, I feel.

But to the credit of Kalki it must be mentioned that even these weak-kneed characters are built up into lifelike sketches by the Tamil wordsmith non-pareil. They act consistent to their characters from the first page in whichthey have been introduced to the last unlike the other three who make flip-flops at times. That's the greatness of Kalki's masterful storytelling!

Regards

Ramaswamy
