Hearty Greetings on this World Tourism Day.

Thiruvalanchuzhi : located near Kumbakonam. on the way to Swamimalai.
about 1 Km north of erstwhile Pazhayarai. Here Rajaraja's wife
Danthisakthi vitangi constructed a Kshetrabalar temple - using the
residual gold used for Hiranya karbam (entering a Golden cow to purify
sins) in Thiruvisalur. Rajendra entered a mount of Thilam in his 3rd
Regnl year. Swetha Vinyakar is post RRC period. Eka Veeri Kali is
established by Rajendra. The only temple where ONLY the ROYAL FAMILY
has given and recorded grants !
RRC 's sivadiksha name Sivapathasekaran and
Rajendra's Sivadiksha name Sivasarana sekaran are recorded.

Dr. Kalaikkovan & Dr. Nalini (RMHRC - Trichy) have brought out an
excellent book on this temple - Valanchuzhi Vaanar.

Nearby places to see: Pazhayarai @ Patteeswaram, Darasuram, Swamimalai.
Suggested place to stay: Kumbakonam.

greetings. sps

Dear Ram,

Even if there is no ignorance, additional informations will be always
helpful to taken them closer to Truth.

Brief can be in these lines:

Those who have faith in Siva worship - after chosing Rudhra
(ferocious) or Soumya (polite) form of worship - adopt a particular
process in conformity to non-Vedhic or Vedhic processes and chose
their Guru.

Guru performs Upadesam and gives the disciple another name.
Such renaming also surmised to re-birth ! This renaming is known as
Dheeksha-naamam.and the process Siva-dheeksha.

For instance Irulneekki Subramanian has been given the Dheeksha name
of Sri Jayendra Saraswathi - Saraswathi being traditional name of
Kanchimutt as Bharathi is the traditional name of Sringerimutt. Such
practices are followed as Thambiran and the like in the Darumapuram -
Thiruvavaduthurai Adheenam etc.

To know more on this, kindly go through the publications of the said
Adheenams...

The Disciple follows the instructions of his Guru....

As RRC 's Guru was Easana Siva Pandithar, he would have followed the
instructions of Easana Siva Pandithar - which are Agamaic traditions ...

I have read some where that Easana Siva Pandithar had followed the
path of Laguleesam and there are inscriptional evidences that Udayalur
Siva temple was also adopting the path of Laguleesam ..

RRCs Siva devotion is explicit from the very fact to construct a siva
temple resembling Meru .. and he was also called as Sivapathasekaran !

In Nallur @ Panchavanmadevi chaturvedi mangalam ( 3Kms West of
Pazhayarai and Trhiruvalanchuzhi) Appar hymns that Siva has set his
foot on his head .. !

RRCs ambition to reach the abode of Siva is also explicit from the
portraits of Cheramanperumal - Sundarar !

Therefore Emperor Rajraja - like the Great Ashoka after Kalinga War
renouncing Power - also would have become to be known as a
"siva-devotee" ... and in this process Easana Siva Pandithar would
have played some inportant role... as Sarva siva pandithar would have
played important role in Rajendra calling himself Siva Sarana Sekaran !

I fully concur with you that it is onething to try to understand
Rajaraja as an Emperor and it is another thing to try to understand
him as Sivapathasekaran ..

Let us search through Adheenam publications Siva Gnaa bhodham etc.

anbudan / sps

Dear SPS / Ram

Intersting discussions

This process - though sounds a BIT odd today - was a very common
concept in ancient times. Almost all brahmins had to undergo this
during their thread ceramony. They called themselves "dwija" which
directly translates to "person of twin birth". Considering all classes
of people became monks it is apparent all can become dwija

Why twin birth ? Osho beautifully explains

One is a physical birth. You are just born as a human being. That's all

Another birth happens when a proper initiation is given to you.
Initiation is very much prevalant even today. When you join ISKON
(Hare krishna movement) you are given initiation with the famous
mantra "Hare rama..." in the second stage you are given deeksha

The mantra itself is not very significant but when it is given from a
guru it becomes very powerful. Everyone knows gayathri mantra - then
why it should be initiated ? it is because of guru.

Almost all monks of any mutt - for example ramakrishna mutt - had to
change their name upon initiation.

There are two steps : One is the initiation and another is deeksha

With initiation you start. With deeksha you are all set for liberation

This is certainly religion. Not much of history though.
