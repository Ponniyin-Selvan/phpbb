Unread Kalvettus

by suganya_ml on Tue Jan 18, 2005 6:59 pm

Dear All,

I read an article is last week Kungkumam weekly magazine that most of
the kalvettus found in TamilNadu are still unread.

The article was an article by Pulavar.Rasu who is ex-head of Kalvettu
Reading Dept,Tanjore Thamizh University. He has assured that we are 95
years trailing in reading the kalvettus.

Almost 1 lakh kalvettus are found in TamilNadu alone and among which
not even 50% is read. Most pitiable to hear is, for many of the
kalvettus whose first copy taken are lost it seems.

He has mentioned that the history may be totally upside down to
whatever we read as history now. This is really scaring !!

Also, a book called "Thamizhar Varalaarum Panpaadum" but
Ra.Pi.SethuPillai too says that more than 30,000 kalvettus are unread
in TN.

I was stunned to read that article. Did any of you had a chance to
read the same? How far is this info true ??

Rgds
Suganya

Hi Suganya,

Whats is said in that article is absolutely true. In our frequent interactions with Dr. Kalaikkovan, we have heard him say that not even 20% of the inscriptions in TN have been copied. Many of the copied inscriptions are waiting to be published.

The pity however is, in the name of kumbabishekam, a lot of inscriptions are damaged. Eventhough the damage is not complete in some cases, these mahaanubhavaas will damage it to a level that makes reading the inscriptions an herculean task, thanks to the whitewashing and oilpanting they do these days. I remember seeing a paraanthaga inscription in Egowri amman temple near vallam. (Thanks to SB sir), covered with dark green oil paint.

People like Dr. Kalaikkovan, Dr. Kudavaayil Balasubramaniam have realised that no amount of cribbing and complaining would help in this regards. The only way to preserve them is not to wait for someone to do something abt. this, just get into the field and do your BIT to preserve the all important history.

--Ram.

===================
He is right. The last publication was around 1917 (First World War !)

====================
Almost 1 lakh kalvettus are found in TamilNadu alone and among which
not even 50% is read.
ABOUT 15000 are published by ASI / State Archlogy under South Indian
Inscriptions;

About 30000 are copied (estempo) and kept in Tracing sheets for the
past 70 / 80 yrs in MYSORE UNIVERSITY.

ASI was originally functioning at Ooty during the British period. An
officer was posted after Independence who hailed from Karnataka. He
preferred his office in Mysore and hence ASI Mysore. After lot of
persuation, ASI Chennai was formed in Fort St George in the sixties.
But these copied tracings are still avl only in ASI Mysore and no one
there now is capable of reading Tamil - leave alone Tamil
inscriptions of 10 Centuries back! Every Tamil Historian is aware of
these details.. but helpless.. For the past 10 yrs only Tamilnadu is
having adequate representation in the Centre - Save RV, TTK & the
alike. Slowly these matters are being taken up.

But ASI is having fund problems. Some of us in the group are working
out details to approach ASI for Private Sector paticipation (1) to
publish those copied, but not published yet (2) to arrange copies
of un-copied inscriptions (3) Using high tech cameras preserving
inscriptions (4)Ditigalising published inscriptions for easy cross-
references.

There are more than 20000 temples and likely to have atleast 10% of
such number of sources. First we are planning to concentrate on
periods between 6 AD and 12 AD - Pandyas - Thevara / Divyadesa
temples apart from temples of Archelogical importance - say about
400. Eliminate published and copied and concentrate on unpublished.
Then concentrate on Vijayanagara / Nayaks built.

It is very difficult to draw the attention of the Authorities to
accord priority to History / Inscriptions etc.

He has mentioned that the history may be totally upside down to
whatever we read as history now. This is really scaring !!
Possible. Like it was not at Kanchi, where Sundara chozha died, but
at Nandhipuram near Thirukkandiyur!

Also, a book called "Thamizhar Varalaarum Panpaadum" but
Ra.Pi.SethuPillai too says that more than 30,000 kalvettus are
unread in TN.

I was stunned to read that article. Did any of you had a chance to
read the same? How far is this info true ??
UNFORTUNATELY VERY MUCH TRUE.
BUT WHO CARES AMONG THE 40 Million Tamizhans !? It will not cross
100. We will try to improve the awareness atleast.

Thanks for bringing out this.

Regards. sps

One way of preserving the inscriptions is that more number of people have to
start learning to read the inscriptions. So that, copying can be done in
fast phase. And awareness has to be created among the people living around
the temple.

Siru thuli peru vellam.

Thanjavur and GKC escaped because they were ASI sites imagine Big temple
in multiculoured oil paint....

Now, GKC is in this status only. ASI renovated the temple recently. The
original stone sculptures are there only upto 5th floor. Rest of the
vimaanam is covered by cement and painted in pink color. When Dr.Kalaikkovan
questioned the ASI people about this renovation work, they said they had to
do it because of the pressure from local people and politicians that they
urge to do the kudamuzhukku. One good thing in this renovation is, they have
cleaned inside the vimaanam and they are going to illuminate like periya
koyil. It was amazing to see the architecture from the inside. The vimaanam
starts as square at the bottom, becomes octagonal at the middle and ends as
circle at the top. The way of handling the transformation of geometrical
shapes was excellent.

Dear All,

In this scenario, we should look at the positive(!)
side.

1. We have lakhs of school / college students eager to
learn cost-effective courses which would enhance their
earning potential.

2. We have enough unexplored historical base.

3. We have an unexploited tourism industry which can
generate crores of rupees and dollars.

4. Universities are ready to give affiliation to
anybody who has even a rented place to start colleges
/ institutes to start courses, even when they have no
enough lecturers.

Why dont we all join and start an institute where
languages, history, and archealogical work etc. are
taught.

Think of NIIT in 80s and now. Why not a HTI
(Historical Tourism Institute) where learning and
earning can go together with preservation of our
ancient history. This would also give patriotic
fervour to our younger generation who want to feel
good about our country.

In Bharathiyar's words, let us unite and
porkuvai, kasugal, vaychol, uzaippu tharuvom and give
life to our rich history.

Thanks,

Uma

I remember to have posted a SILPI portrait - taken from Kumbakonam
Mahamaham Malar - depicting the "KSHETHRA BALAR" Small temple in
Thiruvalanchuzhi - which some of our members have seen in the Mudhal
yathirai. Thiruvalanchuzhi is about 1 Km North of our famous
Pazhayarai palace - which is now known as Chozhan medu. Cauvery
takes a right round about and hence the name. There is a famous
Ashtabuja Kali of Chozha origin near Ambal sanctum. Behind
Kodimaram, is the WHITE (MINI) VINAYAGAR (Swetha Vinayagar) temple -
equivalent to the size of a normal Pillayar temple we all come across
along the road sides - again of Chozha origin - with Chozha
inscriptions - but of Hoysala architecture (through Vimaladithan ?
son-in-law of RR ?)with stone wheels buried in the soil. Inside the
sanctum there is a statue of a bearded King.

On the Northern side of the Main Entrance of the temple (North -
East) there is a water tank (with inscriptions and small statues
inside). On the Southern side is (WAS) the kshethra baalar temple
(Kala Bairavar) - size of a small Pillayar temple we normally see.
Silpi has portraited this about 50 yrs back which was published in
Kumbakonam M.Malar recently. This shows the temple, Kshethra baalar,
and so many other statues scattered around. The Kshethra baalar is
NOW in Rajarajan Manimandapam, Thanjavur (near Sangam Hotel, on
Vallam Road) along with other statues of Thiruvalanchuzhi. In the
temple, we have all seen and read the inscriptions of various grants,
including the one by NADUVIR PENN @ Madevadigal (d/o RR), Olagamadevi
and others..

Rajendra conducted the First Yr of his father(RR)'s ceremony here as
mentioned by " THILA PARVATHAM PUKKAN " in the year 1015 AD.

Hindu Religeous Endowment has announced renovations of this temple
recently. I have also put a mail earlier that one should stand and
listen to ULI Satham transcending us into the 10th century..

Last fortnight when I visited Tiruvalanchuzhi, I did not find this
kshethra Baalar Temple! It is kept in pieces on the floor ! I
immediately informed ASI Chennai and they have assured to look into
this and help renovation to retain the original form!

If this can happen to a temple which is of religious as well as
Historical importance and well worshipped, what will happen to other
assets? We are talking missing inscriptions ... We should look
forward to missing temples as well soon..

- sps
