Hai everybody,

Regarding the argument between PS & SS, just keep it aside.

But after reading SS, I travelled to vathapi (Now Badami) in Karnataka. Great place indeed and a must visit place too. I really doubt Kalki's description of Pulikesi as a tyrant and heartless brute. The cave paintings there is also really wonderful. Two places near Badami, Aihole and Pattadakkal is also great. But these are temples without vigrahams. Heard that these were looted by the barbaric islam groups which raided India.

Then I thought of visiting a great empire which has gifted us out Tanjore and all the temples in south, the Vijayanagar empire at Hampi. To speak truly, the days I spent there was not enough for me. The damage created to the great dynasty by the Delhi sultans is really hurting. When people speak about terrorist attacks in the latest days, what can we say about the looting done to our country by Ghoris, Ghazni's, the slave dynasty, moghuls and so on. I literally cried for my country which inspite of these hardships, stands head high, except that the attitude of people now are very very different from the people then.

Coming back to our PS vs SS, I have read people say SS is romantic. I really differ with them, I can say Kalki in his books has a minimal blend of romance. I think both SS and PS have a major share of politics in it. But on the other hand if we take other authors, mind you, I was really worried whether other authors would be able to efficiently handle history like Kalki, My vote goes to Mr.sandilyan. Great romantic writer indeed. So I thought I can start an arguement by comparing Kalki vs others.

Iam in Malaysia now. So would like to enquire about rajendra's and Anabaya's exploits in Kadaram here. (Kadalpura and yavanarani are his master pieces). If anyone knows details about our Cholas exploits in Srivijaya kingdom here, kindly let me know, so that I can have a visit to all these places.

Regards,
Vaidy
