Dear Friends,

CHENNAI BOOK FARE -
You all know that Chennai Book Fare starts from 6th January to 16th January in grand scale. It was very pleasure to note that large crowds were gathering at Arts College Grounds in Mount Road for the past few years (during this book season). I had been there last before year.

I would like to utilize this great opportunity to request our friends to visit M/s. NARMADHA PADHIPPAGAM for my (Stall D-20) History Novels books.

Vamsatara (Rs. 200/-) (2 parts).
Thirumalai Thirudan (Rs. 150/-).

Though I feel sorry for this type of self promotion through our site but at the same time request encouragement from our friends towards history based novels. After reading the books, kindly send your comments. Our Sri has already sent his comments which are very valuable I treat.

The books are appreciated by famous authors like Sujata (through Ananda Vikatan), K.R.A.Narasiah, and Dr. Prema Nanda Kumar, Prof. Sirpi Balasubramanian and several Tamil weeklies and dailies. The reviews appeared in ?The Hindu? and the same is reproduced in my blogsite ?http://www.vamsadhara.blogspot.com? . The Publishers has just announced the 2nd edition of Vamsadhara during this year, which it self a healthy sign of improvement in book reading.

My thanks to Mr. K. R. A. Narasiah, he went a step further and recommended Jaya T.V for introduction of TIRUMALAI TIRUDAN by Sudhangan and the same was telecast during last May.

During your visit to book fair, I suggest you to purchase one extra copy of Ponniyin Selvan (now available in pocket size too) and present as gift to our near and dear. Another writer the great ?DEVAN? novels are now available with Alliance Publishers. ?Devan? was simply great and one way equally compared to Kalki on social subjects. His novels include ?Ms. Janaki?, Kalyaani, Mr. Vedantam and ?Justice Jagannathan? are excellent to read. The new and upcoming writers may take Devan writings as basic. Wonderful story teller.

Lot of other new books will be coming to the fare and Chennaites are really lucky enough. Music season gone ? Book season enter.

Best wishes,

DHIVAKAR.

CHENNAI BOOK FARE

by vkasirajan on Thu Jan 12, 2006 12:02 pm

I went to the book fare yesterday. Though a BIT
crowded, it is worth paying a visit.

I could see KANS books "The Colas" and "Foreign notes
of South India" at the Madras University stall.

Another interesting book is "Kadal Vali Vanigam" by
Narasiah which is in Palaniappa Bros stall.

I also bought books on Sangam age coinage by Dinamalar
editor & some other books on Indus script all
available in Saiva Siddantha Kalagam stall.

Reprints of Jayakanthan, some books by
Jeyamohan(korravai), Ramakrishnan (urupasi) were major
attractions.

Ponniyinselvan is stall a major selling item. They are
also recommending people to buy Nanthipuratthu Nayagi
by Vikraman - it is now available as a single book.

Titles such as "kurai onrum illai" were also sold in
largge numbers.
