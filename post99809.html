Yes, these Matrikass existed even before all the avatars have been
in place. In Kumarasambava of Mahakavi Kalidas, when Great Lord
Parameshwara, starts for his wedding with Paravati, the Sapta
Matukas appear with ornaments, clothes and all the accessories for
him. But, he will touch them as a acknowledgement, and will
transform himself for his role.
So, Goddess Parvati, when she was about to take bath in Ganges, all
the Devas will worship the all mighty mother for saving them from
Shumba-Nishumba. That time, Devi Kaushigi (Mahasarasvati), will
emerge from Parvati and will fight for Devas. During the fierce
battle Kali will emerge out of Kaushigi, and will kill Chandan and
Mundan, the invincible warriors of Shumban-Nishumban. So she will
bear the name as Cha-Mun-Di. That time, she will be granted the a
place in Saptamatrukas. The actual number of Matrikaas, are more
than seven, and not seven, as per Devi Mahatmyam.


The concept of Shanmata, was introduced by Adhi Shankara, and hence
we might not see the correlation between Saptamaatrikas and Shanmata.

After why this sanctitity abut 7? Saptasindhu, saptarishi etc.,
etc., Even a giat scholar like Dhavalikar went on to identify seven
figures depicted in aindua seal as "atrikas".

In any insstances, atrikas are to be worshipped by all ostly by
traders who leave the town. There used be matrika shrines at the
cross roads.

the earlier matrika temple excavated at Aihole, a trading town,
never had more than four matrikas. The standardisation of seven,
i.r.o matrikas comes around in 6-7th CAD in a depition of the same
at Ravalphadi cave temple at Aihole where they are depicted around a
beautiful (perhaps the best) depiction of Natarja.
The epics/puranas never reduce the matrikas to 7

But there is a distinct association of them as protectors of
waterbodies, particulalry large tanks, is widely vougue in Tamil
Nadu and Karnataka.

Do you know that the earliest dated saptamatrika teple is in
Chennai - the Selliamman teple at Velachery, to the right of the
Velachery main road. I observed here non-traditional archakas
performing pujas in excellently chanted Sanskrit mantras!!!!
