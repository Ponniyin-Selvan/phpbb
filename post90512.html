dear friends,
it was acutally vijayalaya chola who captured
thanjavur from muthhariyar chief then feudtories of
pallavas. Hence he was called tanjai konda.....
parataka was hailed as madurai konda after he won over
the pandyas.
nothing can be derived abt kalbras except they were
generally considered as evil rulers and there were
some exceptions too.
here we have to understand one more thing....
present day state boundries cannot be a yardstick to
ascertain the medieval period linguistic areas.
so what can infer is language is no bar to the
conquests of mighty chola empire. chola inscriptions
were found in tamil, telugu as well as in sanskrit.
we must also remember that the well trained army of
RRC went as far as present day orissa(kalinga) and it
culminated in grand victory over pala kings of bengal
during rajendra's reign.
this is not over, chola navy crossed the palk strait
won over sinhala king of srilanka then crossed bay of
bengal to defeat the army of srivijaya .
its king vijayatungavarman accepted to be vassal of
chola empire. chola navy ransacked kadaram and
rajendra was hailed as kadaram konda.
chola empire extended upto e .asia and perhaps one of
the biggest in the medeival world.for 50 years cholas
controled the sea trade and it is generally believed
that rajendra undertook the kadaram operation not only
to extend the empire but also to check the pirates who
troubled indian and chinese merchants and these
pirates were encouraged by vijayatungavarman.
i hope we can understand the might of chola empire.
regards,
ramnath.

reg cheraman perumal...i think in those days in
malabar the language prevalant was koodum tamil..a
mixture of sanskrit and ancient tamil..forerunner to
present day malayalam.

DEAR NANDA :: FOR QUICK RECOLLECTION :

AROUND 3RD AD : (TAMIL ) SANGAM AGE. NO INSCRIPTION. NO STANDING
EVIDENCE. DURING THE BRITISH, ATLEAST TILL RECENTLY. THANKS TO
TSUNAMI, DR. THIAGA SATHIYAMURTHY (AASAI Founder) Retd Suptt
Archeologist FOUND & IDENTIFIED A TEMPLE - MURUGA TEMPLE - WITH
THREE TIER BASEMENT - BRICK - STONE - BRICK AND ATTRIBUTED TO SAMGAM
AGE. NOW ASI - AFTER SCIENTIFIC TESTS - HAS AGREED TO THIS TO BE OF
SANGAM AGE TEMPLE - STANDING EVIDENCE.

3RD TO 5TH AD :: KALABRAS. SPECULATIONS OF THEIR ORIGIN.. INCLUDING
WHETHER THEY ARE JAINS... KAADVARKON PANDYA IS CONSIDERED KALABRA -
WHO IS DESIGNATED NAAYANMAR.

6 to 8th AD :: " Jithath Simha Vishnu, Thanja Haraha " is the
inscription (as you rightly mentioned ) in Saskrit - in Rock Fort
Temple, Trichy, CONFIRMING that Trichy - Thanjavur - Cauvery Belt
was under Pallava Rule from Simhavishnu Days ..

Around 850 AD : (Recall PS - Kalki - Chozha details) :: Vijayalayan
conquored and occupied Thanjai. Thiruvalangadu Copper plates are
silent on the Mutharayas who were subdued. Our Group has discussed
this extensively - thanks to Srilankan member SriHari :: SAATAN
PAZHIYILI was the Mutharaya - who constructed a Siva temple and
named after him as Pazhayileeswaram in Naarthamalai (enroute
Pudukkottai from Trichy) - around mid 9th century (850 or so ). But
from Senthalai (Near Nemam / Thirukkaattupalli inscriptions) we have
KAVARAN MAARAN and Co., as Mutharayas operated from Thanjavur around
that period. So it was postulated that Two Sects of Mutharayas -
one Around the bordering with Pandyas at Pudukkottai and other sect
at Nemam - close to Thanjai and most probably this sect of
Mutharayas would have been conquered by Vijayalayan.

Dr. Nagaswamy, on his study of Muthurasas postulated that the
VRIDHARAJA found at Mysore around 5th AD are MUTHURAJAS from TN who
paved way for this sect and probably this is time for evolution of
Kannada language !

Therefore with Tamil Brahmi inscriptions (pre Sangam) - Adhiyaman
period inscriptions in Thirukkoilore / Dharmapuri (Sangam period),
we have Sanskrit / Grantha mix inscriptions followed and then Tamil
inscriptions again.... with Sankrit separated. For instance
Thiruvalangadu Copper plates copper plates (Released by Rajendra I)
comprised of Sanskrit Verses first followed by Tamil scripts..

PLEASE PLACE KARIKALA around 500 -550 AD only.

Telugu / Kannada / Malayam are of later period in comparison with
Tamil - almost in that Order.

Linguistic experts may step in and enlighten.

regards/ sps
