Dear Dr.Jaybee
Thanks for a very nice and interesting account. I've thoroughly
enjoyed your highly informative mails on Pazhaiyarai, Gangai Konda
cholapuram, Ganges conquest of Rajendra and Siddhar Muthu vaduga
nathar - in agathiyam e group. Not many well informed people have
come forward to share their wide knowledge - you are one exception.
We are all very delighted by your presence and we seek your blessings
for the development and growth of this group.

All,
Please do not feel about the downfall of Chola empire. Like all other
empires, chola regime also got very corrupt during the period of
Maravarman Sundara Pandiya(I think his chola counterpart was
Rajaraja III - we have epigraphic evidences of revolts in many
places - people sacrificing/immolating themselves to oppose
corruption in temples etc.) And any regime which has lost the support
of people will have to fall - this is one fact that keeps popping up
again and again in
history.

I understood that it was Jatavarman who donated for the golden vimana
of Srirangam. I've wondered whether it was gold plated already or
whether it was done with the gold donated by Jatavarman. The
pranavakara vimanam of srirangam is believed to be very very ancient -
even today it looks absolutely beautiful!

Coming to Dr.Jaybee's question : I think they should have used some
form of Platform with a beam in the centre - asking elephant to climb
one end of platform and balancing the gold from other side. The
conventional tharasu may not have worked.

One unrelated piece : I've seem very huge well like pits inside
srirangam temple - they say it was for storing ghee or rice etc.(It
is there even today). Looking at the size of it, I'm not surprised at
all, at the donation of Pandiyan king.

Cheers
Gokul.S
