Hi,
Talking abt the original PS version in Kalki, I have seen it and read it after borrowing the volumes from some neighbours who are no longer staying in my vicinity. I had collected the early 1970s version when it was serialised for the second time by kalki mag, after almost two decades which introduced so many new lovers of tamil novels to the beauty of PS.
Abt the 1950 serlialisation the talking point is not only the writing but also the drawings of maniam. One drawing in particular captures what kalki has said through words, the most important event in the novel - the killing or suicide of Aditya Karikalan. I can still vividly see in my mind's eye the drawing of maniam which depicted karikalan's body on the ground, nandini with her hand over to mouth to stifle the scream as she sees periya pazhuvettarayar entering the room after grabbing VD by his throat and making him unconscious.
The figure of ravidasan as he's abt to throw the tiger's skin on periya pazhuvettarayar with his 'kolli kangal' and the witnessing of the whole drama by manimekalai stand out. It's an outstanding pictorial representation. And the way maniam had depicted nandini and VD in the seriliasation was not matched later by either vinu (1970s) or maniam selvan.
And another drawing that I still remember vividly is abt periya pazhuvettarayar drinking kanji from the earthen pot served by nandini (talaivirikolam) and then the pazhuvoor elaya rani galloping on horseback in the chapter "nandiyinum marandal'.
