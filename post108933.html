correct, it was maya created by Krishna to test the pandavas
perseverance, dont remember the story fully, but surely later the maya
is relaxed and Pandavas goes to heaven and kauravas to hell.

Trivia - Just yesterday I was reading 'Practical vedanta' by Swami
Vivekanand. What i understand is, there is no mention of Hell in any
of our vedas. Only Purnas have reference to hell. Puranas, as we all
know is latter addition, with history mixed with lots of myth and
flavour. Vedas talk only about Heaven and returning to earth after the
good karma is over, because as per vedas taking birth itself is the
highest punishment.

In fact both Yudhistir and Duryodhana supposedly ruled over very prosperous kingdoms.

There is another way of looking at this whole thing - from Duryodhana's perspective. My grandmother said there used to be several street plays and folk narratives of the Mahabharat from various perspective that disappeard over time. One of them is like this - Duryodhana was not legally obligated to share any part of his kingdom with the pandavas, since they were not his birth cousins only half cousins. From childhood the elders and family memberswere partial to the pandavas since Pandu died young and the five children were very accomplished. That instigated anger in Duryodhana who in turn grew up to be a vengeful, resentful prince and refuses to see the Pandavas as his equals.
