So lets plan in an order...for example,

1) What are the sections?
a) Bigtemple Photos
b) Historical
c) Bigtemple details
d) Kalvettu details

2) What are all the subsections?
a) Bigtemple Photos
i) Architectural
ii) Paintings
iii) Kalvettu
b) Historical
i) About Bigtemple
ii) About rajaraja
iii) About chozas (to the extent applicable to Bigtemple)

navigation is easier and any information can be accessed within
two/three pages away from any given page.

Then, there is the role details...

1) Who are all the different types of people who would access the site?
a) Casual visitor
b) History students
c) Scholars
e) Programmers
d) Site admin
e) Content developers
f) Search engine bots

2) Scope of access
a) Casual visitor-view site content
b) History students-view site content
c) Scholars-view site content
e) Programmers-view/edit source code, templates
d) Site admin-manage content, restrict directories
e) Content developers-add content, modify content
f) Search engine bots-view site content

3) Mode of access
a) Casual visitor-browser
b) History students-browser
c) Scholars-browser, web service
e) Programmers-FTP
d) Site admin-browser interface
e) Content developers-browser interface
f) Search engine bots-browser

It can be further drilled down. After all those things are decided, we
can then decide upon what CMS would be perfect for our business. We
may end up adopting a CMS as it is, may need to write some extra code,
may need to write a CMS from the scratch on our own.
