Dear SPS,

The front runner to KAN was actually S.Krishnaswamy Iyengar.
The British Elites (those days) wholly relied on his work. SKI went
by walk from Mysore to Vijayanagaram and back to Chennai (like our
Thamizh Thatha on Sangham work compiling) to gather details on
Vizianagara and Chola dynasty.
He was promoted as a cheif of History Dept at Madras University
till 1919 which was taken over by KAN.

Burton Stein, who sourced lot of informations from Indians only,
paid great tributes to SKI and KAN for bringing the history of South
India as straight one. B.A.Saletore wrote Karnataka History,
N.Venkatramanayya writing on Andhra History, in 1930s, both adopted
Krishnaswamy Iyengars's alternative-reliance upon literary evidence.
T.V.Mahalingam was encouraged by KAN during his chairmanship of
Madras University History dept.
H.Kriishna Sastri, S.M.Natesa Sastri and S.Krishnaswami Aiengar
were the trio who have done the much of epigraphycal and textual
analysis of Sewell and other Europen founders of pre-modern history
in South India. This was very much acknowledged by British Scholars
C.A.Bayly of Cambridge and John F.Richards of Du8ke University.
SO WE MUST BE PROUD THAT WE OURSELVES HAVE REDISCOVERED THE
HISTORY THAT KARIKALAN DID NOT BUILD BIG TEMPLE BUT RAJARAJA.

But One thing was certain. What I had given above only few
prominents. But there were several Indians were searching for truth
and proof of documents during the British era.
Our recent historians only added further to their elders'
greatest efforts. Burtain Stein agains says in his 2003 published
work of 'Vijayanagara' that ' But none had yet surpassed the breadth
of vision of Krishnaswamy Aiyengar during the early years of
twentieth century.
