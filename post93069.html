Rahul, Very well put!! I feel similar. Also just
historically another example Arthur Conan doyle, the
author of Sherlock Holmes himself got so fed up
writing of the same guy that he killed his hero in one
episode. The angry fans went to court demanding the
hero be resurrected!! Obviously the court ruled in
favor of the author saying he can do what he likes
with his character.

But Conan doyle was so moved by his fans love for his
character that he resurrected him and wrote few more
episodes. We are unfortunate Amarar Kalki did not live
to see his Nandhini being discussed so many years
after he created her or he would have given her more
detail and a sequel.

--- Rahul dhinakaran
wrote:

many things on poor
nandini's head

Sir;
you hit the nail in the head!! Im sure Kalki felt
complete freedom when it
came to characterizing Nandini. He gave her a
villainous touch, then a
damsel in distress, then revenge and last but not
least ethereal beauty, for
which everyone from Veerapandya to Aditya to our
dear Mr.SPS (and many more
in our group) have fallen hook,line and sinker.
I think Kalki felt creatively stifled when writing
about
Arulmozhi,Aditya,Kundavai,etc as they were all
historically valid
characters.
On the other hand he was in full flow when writing
about
Alwarkadiyan,Nandini, both of whom, in my humble
opinion, were the best
characters in the whole of PS. He felt totally free
as he dint have to live
upto any copper plates or kalvettus, just write!!!
Kalki was so involved that he dint know how to stop!
This can be seen in Dumas' 3 Musketeers also. When
we are just expecting
more lines or actions from the Queen, Dumas cuts and
goes straight to
Milady,Pothos or some other imaginary character.
Maybe its what great writers do to keep the interest
from stopping but i
feel they feel more free in characters which are not
burdened by history.

Regards
Rahul

who tells aaditya
the direction to veerapandya's hiding place
next mention of nandini is she is being bound and
about to be thrown
into veera pandya's pyre.
next she is on the road and pazhuvetaraiyar
forcibly kidnaps her to
his palace.
a pandya prince being brought up in the forest
calls her mother.

i sometimes wonder if kalki was thrusting too many
things on poor
nandini's head

Rahul and RV,

I agree Kalki might have mistaken in juggling Nandini's character
across the PS, but I still hold it as his masterstroke. May be not
intentional. Tell me which is most two discussed point in PS,

My take would be
1. Who killed Aditya?
2. Who is Nandini's father?

Had Kalki had wanted he could have given the answer at the post
script. But he never gave an hint for either of the above questions. I
think he was aware of the questions that might arise in the readers
mind and wanted to keep it a lingering thought in all the readers and
he has achieved it.
