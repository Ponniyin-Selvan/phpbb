THE USUAL RE-CYCLING ..

Let us recall from PS :

In 850 Ad Vijayalaya was a Commander.. assisted Pallava.. Got
permission to rule independently his Mother land .. collided with
Mutharayas and paved way for expansion.

Then Adithan - Parantaka I - Arinjaya etc.. ruled with the help of
Kurunila Mannargal.

Kandraditha - Sundara - Aditha Karikala - Uthama episode is
discussed in Ponniyinselvan widely by Kalki.

We have seen the roles of Kurunila mannargal with Authority.

Rajaraja handled them effectively and curtailed their Authority, but
reinforced matrimonial relationships ..

Rajendra I handled Pandyas more effectively creating CHOLA -
PANDYAS, by positioning offsprings of such alliances ..

We have discussed Rajendra I's children already in detail (about 40
year rule).

In 1070 Kulothunga I came to Power. Rajaraja I's daughter Kundavai +
Vimaladithan's son married to Rajendra's granddaughter and
Vikramaditha VI was renamed as Kulothunga I. (Pls Ref Archieves
again).

He maintained the same Authority of RRC and RJC.

Dilusion from his son Vikrama Chola.

Kulothunga II's rule depended more on the Authority of Kurunila
mannars ! (which I earlier hinted in one of my mails.. )

The cycle prevailed during Sundara - Uthama period (in about 970 -
985) was again surfaced during 1140 or so ..!

But there is more to it ...

The intelligence of Rajaraja I & Rajendra I was missing in
Kulothunga I.

(With due respects to Vizag Dhivakar who has been keeping Kulothunga
I at par with RRC and RJC) ..

Recall the current affairs (21st Century) in the neighbouring
nations ..

Buddhist Power Centres ADVISING (Controlling) Political Power
Centres .. This was happening from the time immemorial..

It was PARANTAKA I -
(First Chozha King who conquered Madurai and Ealam - Mathirayum
Eazhamum konda Parakesari)
(Sundara was Mathiraiyum Eazhamum konda Rajakesari )
(Incidentally these couple of lines only paved way for PRASASTHI -
Meykeerthi - thirumagal Pola perunichelvi ... by Rajaraja I )
- repeat PARANTKA I - who identified this Lacuna in the Power System
and filled it..

YES.. HE IS BEING CALLED AS THE " BEE AT THE LOTUS FEET OF LORD
SIVA " .. By this He has assumed the Religeous Authority as well,
aprt from his Political Authority..

Rajaraja I .. followed suit.. Sivapatha sekaran
Rajendra I .. Siva Sarana Sekaran ..

Kulothunga I .. did not have this line up... so handled Chidambaram
renovation / expansion (which shrine is important for Vishnu &
Siva ).. but he was NOT the religeous Authority of either Saivites
or Vaishnavites..

So was the case with his son Vikrama Chola...

Kulothunga II was weighing options with Jeevagasinthamani .. then
considered Saivism .. was instumental in bringing out
Periyapuranam .. but messed up in Chidambaram ..

And he was ALSO NOT the religeous Authority of either Saivites or
Vaishnavites...

Take the Great PANDYA -- Sundara Pandian .. who again handled
Srirangam & Chidambaram ...

He was ALSO NOT the religeous Authority of either Saivites or
Vaishnavites...

With Mohammeden invasion, the Power Centres could not hold together
and started crumbling..

No one would have predicted 1000 year Rule of any Dynasty ... having
seen Harshar to Asoka .. !

But none would have predicted a Non-Hindu Rule in the Southern
States either during the 10th Century !

regards/ sps

by vinjamoor_venkatesh on Thu May 08, 2008 9:40 am

Yes, that is what I read too. During the fag end of the term of
Kulothunga I, the Hoysala King Vishnuvardhana did invade the Chola
Kingdom and caused a significant damage to the credentials of the
Cholas. Probably that was only a spark. But looking at the history,
it is clear that, inspite of this, the Cholas were able to hold on
with authority, for atleast another 3 or 4 generations until
Kulothunga III and then the actual disintegration / decline started.

Regarding, losing the patronage of the Vaishnavas after the
Chidambaram incident, I would only say that it was insignificant.
Looking back, the Tamizh Vaishnavas were never a dictating force in
history. Yes they lacked the number to dictate anything. And
moreover, it was not democracy then, but monarchy. Both these facts,
indicate that there was not a big problem due to the incident, that
the decline would have started.

Definitely the "comparative", I repeat, the "comparative"
incompetency of the successors of RajaRaja and Rajendra were the
main reasons, like in case of any other dynasty. In fact, it was
recorded that even Rajendra I did not have a peaceful reign and had
to involve himself in a lot of "instigated" wars, though he won all
or most of them. But this was just indicative of the fact that his
enemies were not some incompetent bunch of local landlords and they
were a "power" by themselves who could not be discarded.

All these combined efforts of the common enemies + the "comparative
incompetency" of the subsequent Chola Kings could have been the
natural cause of a decline.

Regarding SundaraPandyan returning the attack on Kulonthunga III
within 3 years, I dont think there was any impossibilities in it. As
can be seen from the fact that Narasimhavarman I returned the attack
on Pulikesi in 9 years, Sundara Pandyan did it in 3 years. The
lesser number of years somewhat indicate a rapid decline in the
competencies of Kulothunga III.

Regards,
Venkatesh
