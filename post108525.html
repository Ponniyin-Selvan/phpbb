WHY KALKI COINED THE TITLE 'PONNIYIN SELVAN' WHILE RAJA RAJA HAS
SEVERAL OTHER TITLES? CORRECT ME IF I AM WRONG, I COULDN'T FIND THIS
TITLE AMONG OTHER TITLES FOR RAJA RAJA.
=====================

Excellent lead !

I guess our R Venkat is yet to see this mail. awaiting response.

However, I have also been pondering, wondering and appreciating this
Title from the time I read this .. say in 1980s..

All the titles of RRC is enslisted and can be had from the
Archieves..

Kalki did not like to chose any of them for his magnum opus.

If we look at RRC titles, majority are attributed by virtue of his
victories over a ruler / land.

then there are mass-appealing titles :: Jananathan etc.,

In praise of his some qualities : Kshatriya sigamani etc.

Rajarajan ... to establish himself as King of Kings.

Sivapathasekaran ... in line with his ancestors .. particularly
Parantaka I .. at the lotus feet of Lord siva..

KALKI should have (of course - HE IS ) been extraordinarily clever
to chose the title PONNIYIN SELVAN ..

The Cauvery .. which was not subject matter of dispute in 1950 when
Kalki wrote this Wonder.

Whoever is born on the banks of / associated with Cauvery (or
Vaigai, Ghodavari, Ganges, Sindh or any such jeeva-nadhis) would
love to identify atleast a part of such self with that river ..

Kalki must have also seen himself as Ponniyinselvan (in his field -
not in Polity / Power)!


Thank you Priya.. for this wonderful lead.

mailing this precisely from Thanjai (now)!

Ponniyin Selvan - selection of Title by Kalki

by sampath_srinivasaraghavan on Sat Aug 09, 2008 4:43 pm

Kalki's story covers that phase of Arulmozhi's life when he was not
even an 'ilavarasan' and had no title whatsoever. Of course, Kalki
indicates several times that Arulmozhi has the potential to become a
famous ruler, in due course. Hence the title "ponniyin selvan" is more
appropriate than the titles inherited by Arulmozhi later in life.

Sampath

by karikal84 on Sun Aug 10, 2008 1:37 am

HI,

Just a thought.
Kalki at more than one point, emphasizes through Kundavai that Arulmozhi was called as 'Ponniyin Selvan' as he was saved by Ponni/Kaveri herself in his young age. Also, Sundara Chozhar also remembers that event as a proof forEzhathu Nachchiyar's revenge-seeking acts towards Sundarar's children. Thesame incidence, from Ezhatharasi's point of view and Arulmozhi's point of view, shows the love she had in Sundarar which turned towards his children.Thus the same incidence gets discussed again and again by various characters in the storyline and also happens to be one that gave Arulmozhi a title.Kalki would have thought of this title to be more suitable for his story, which connects Ezhathu nachchiyar to the story through the incidence, As her relationship with Sundara Cholar was the triggering point for the complete story if we analyse.

Remember how kalki explained his naming of the chapter 'Thyaga Sigaram'? Comparing with that reasoning, i too think that due to above mentioned reasons 'Ponniyin Selvan' is the best suited title for the story.

Nandrigaludan,
Amar
