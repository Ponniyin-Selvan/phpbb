die hard pandyas

by abhivencat on Mon Jul 18, 2005 10:37 pm

i am sending across a mail from Dr. Jaybee on the pandyas


The Pandyas are hard-core die-hards.
Their history goes back far into the dim-past.
Mangudi Marudhan of the Third Sangam says that PalyagaSalai
Mudhukudumi Peruvalzudhi was an ancestor of Pandyan
Nedunjelziyan. PalYagasalai lived before 600 BC.
The Third Sangam disappeared in 283 AD. There was
a combination of holocausts in Pandya nadu and the country
was depopulated and a large part of it was wilderness and
wasteland. In addition, there were aliens who invaded and
captured the country. After ten generations, the Pandya king
repopulated the country and in a few more generations, the
Panda King Kadungon got rid of the alien rule.
He and his descendants made the Pandya country
very prosperous and powerful.
And then the expansion of the Cholzas began and
direct confrontation took place for around a century.
Even RajaRaja I who captured it finally, could not rid
the country of the Pandyas who appeared and reappeared.
There were five lines of Pandyas at any one time ruling
from five different places. The main one was in Madurai who
was the chief among the five.
Rajendra went to the extent of abolishing the Pandya
dynasty altogether. He let his sons and the sons of his sons
take turns to rule as Cholza-Pandya Viceroys.
But he could do so only for about forty years.
Kuloththunga I created many cantonments along the
north to south axis of Pandyanadu. He let go of Ceylon and
Venginadu totally. But he let the Pandyas rule as his subordinates.
One of the Pandyas played a major rule in the Kalinga War
of Kuloththunga.
During the last part of his rule, King SriVallabha Pandya was
issuing his own inscriptions in his name in the northern Pandya nadu
which was bordering on Cholza country. This was in 1120 AD.
The Pandyas were ruling as subordinates to the Cholzas
mostly for namesake until 1168 when the First Pandyan Civil War
took place. Sinhalese intervention helped one faction of the
Pandyas. It almost destroyed the Cholza kingdom.
But Rajadhiraja II and Kuloththunga III managed to subdue them.
The last two years of Kuloththunga Cholza III's rule saw
the rise of the Pandyas under Maravarman Sundara Pandya I
who partially destroyed the Cholza country.
1276AD., the Cholzas disappeared and the whole of Tamilnadu
came under the Pandya rule.
In 1210AD, the second Pandyan Civil War started. One of the
warring parties invited Sultan Khalji's help. Malik Kafur, Khusrau,
and
Ulugh Khan's invasion, looting, plunder and destruction took place
from 1313 until 1323 when the Pandya country was annexed to the
Delhi Sultanate.
But the Pandyas were fighting against the Turks and between
themselves. One of the most effective one, Parakrama Pandya
was taken to Delhi in chains.
When the Vijayanagarians captured the country in 1376 AD,
the Pandyas were around as minor rulers in several places.
Visvanatha Nayakka had to fight a duel against one of the
Pancha Pandyas.
We hear about Pandyas as minor rulers until the late 1600's
and early seventeen hundreds. The great grandfather of Kattabommu
Nayakkan appropriated some land from the contemporary Pandyas .
He double-crossed his boss, Ettappa Nayakkan from whom also he
misappropriated some land and kept him in check by instilling a fear
about the Pandyas.
The Pandyas became Polygars under the Nayakdom of Madurai
and later on under the Arcot Nawab. Under the British rule, they
became
zamindars.
Yes, siree.
They are still aound.
Right from the City of Then-Madurai and KapataPuram of
Kumari,
onwards to Madurai which they occupied for more than two thousand
years
and then to Thirunelveli, and later on to ThenKasi and
KarivalamvandhaNalluur,
and KayaththaaRu and now along the out-skirts of the Western Ghats.
Real Die-Hards, huh?
