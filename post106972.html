Ha Ha Ha, if this is intended towards me, I am no expert Ravi. I
mean this. However, from an unbiased view point, especially when
you
compare Andal and Ayana siRpi, I believe, they both had great
passions on their objectives, only that Andal was more determined
and the destiny favoured Her ("un manathAl en ninaindhirundhAi")
while Ayana siRpi was less determined and so was his destiny. We
can
easily compare the passion of both. Andal's passion made her go
against the ritualistic practice of not adorning the garland,
before
offering it to God, while Ayana siRpi's passion, made him mad, even
to the extent that he struck a deal with the enemy of His king
(Mahendravarman) who he respected so much.

This is height of devotion. Not knowing what one does out of great
devotion. Periyazhwar, father of Andal, incidentally describes this
state of mind in his periyAzhwAr thirumozhi says "atthA ariyE endRu
nAn azhaithAl, pithA enbar piRar..."

Thirumangai Azhwar personifies this state of bliss, unmindful of
the
worldly logics, that he even went to the extent of
performing "madal
eduthal". Actually, madal eduthal, as per the Tamizh Ilakkiyams is
ordained only for the male, who will wear a garland out of the palm
leaf and sit on a Donkey and go around the town saying that the
women he loves does not turn her eyes towards him. This is
something
like emotional black mail. The reason I say that Thirumangai Azhwar
went against the norm is that, he had sung the two
prabhandhams "siRiya thirumaDal" and "periya thirumaDal" in nAyaki
bhAvam, means, thinking of him as a women. So a women should not be
doing this as per the customs prevalent in those times. But his
love
for reaching God and the frustration that He is not showing up,
made
him go well against the customs and rituals, prevalent at those
times.

So the Love, Devotion towards God are in one's thought and no
customs and rituals can stipulate a boundary for these, when the
jeevAtmA goes into a state of eternal bliss.

Ravi,

I think you have misunderstood my words. I agree that true Bhakthi
is definitely above anything and everything. Like Vairam indicated
clearly, look only at the simile. Just the uvamai. Andalukku inai
Andal is very correct. But what I meant to say is that the Passion
of Andal towards Ranganathar and the passion of Ayana siRpi towards
his sculpture is same.

Just take this for example. Thondaradippodi Azhwar says

"pacchai mAmalai pOl mEni, pavaLa vAi kamalch chengaNN"

Here he quotes the Green Mountain as an uvamai for the pleasing body
of Ranganatha. Does this mean that the Mountain is higher than
Ranganathan Himself? Or the Pavalam is greater than the Mouth which
gave the Gita, or the Lotus is greater than the Eyes, with which He
graces the world? They are just similes/uvamai. Similarly the
Passion of Andal and Passion of Ayanar is just the same. OnRukku
OnRu uvamai. Here the point of consideration is the passion and not
the final objective.

Trust I have clarified.
