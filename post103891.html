Vikrama chola alias Uttama chola

by seshadrigokul on Tue Apr 25, 2006 9:40 am

The following inscription also seems to suggest that Uttama was
known as Vikrama chola
---------------------------------------------------------------------
SII Vol 8

No. 63.

(A.R. No. 29 of 1922.)

Tiruvamattur, Villupuram Taluk, South Arcot District.

On the north wall of the room inside the ardha-mandapa,
Abhiramesvara temple.

A gift of 96 sheep for a perpetual lamp in the temple at
Tiruvamattur, a devadana in Vavalur-nadu to the west of Aruva-nadu
by Miladudaiyan Raman Siddhavadan alias Vikramasola Â– Miladudaiyan
of the Bharggava-gotra. This chief figures also in No. 7 of 1905 in
the 5th year of Rajakesarivarman from Kilur in the same district,
wherein he is stated to have married a Pandya princess. Vikrama is
probably a title of Uttama-Chola (M.E.R. 1929, II. 29). The
inscription evidently one of Rajaraja I.

hi all
there is a legend that all chola coins were confisicated and molten by
the pandyas.
when the pandyas seeking revenge for the destruction of their land by
cholas,( after waiting for 2 centuries) captured chola land all traces
of chola culture were eradicated in a systematic manner.buildings and
coinage bore the brunt. temples were left alone. as well as buildings
of literary importance.

so that could be one reason why we do not find many chola coins.
