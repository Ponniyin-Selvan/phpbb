sps wrote on 12.9.2003 ::

Today I found some time to scan through Udayarkudi inscriptions.
I am furnishing a jist :

On the SOUTH WALL of Central Shrine of Ananteeswaraswami temple, as
per South India Inscriptions (SII) records of 1920:

Swasathishree Kopparakesari (2nd yr) (Parakesari is one who is NOT
entitled to share the throne; Rajakesari is one who is likely to
succeed the Crown King. Uthama Chola was parakesari and
Adithakarikala - subsequently Rajaraja were Rajakesaris: Sastry)

These Records are offerings by various persons for burning a
perpetual lamp - called NONDA VILAKKU (mostly in memory of a
departed soul - even now, we call it Motshadeepam : Personally some
of us did this after the fateful 11th Sept 2001 massacre. The gift
was either 12.5 Kazhanju gold for buying 96 sheep, from whose ghee,
the lamp would be burnt perpetually : as per inscription "till
Suryan and Chandran are there?! - "Charadithar ullavarai")
=======================
Record #537: Nakkan Pattalaki 12.5 Kazhanju gold -for Nondavilakku;

BY SEMBIAN MADEVIAR ::

540: "Merkezhundu arulina devar Kandradithadevar Deviyar
Mazhavarayar magalar parandagan Madevadigalarana SEMBIAN
MADEVIYAR .." gifted 96 sheep for burning a perpetual (Nonda
vilakku) lamp;
--------------

607 : KODANDARAMACHERI Kaanjai Kramavithan:12.5K;

On the Wall of the South Verandah in the Prakara, behind the image
of Gaja Lakshmi in the Same Anantheeswaraswami Temple of
Udayarkudi (now known as Kattumannarkudi - near Veeranam @
Veeranarayanapuram - where our PS Hero - Vandiyathevan commences
this epic), as per Record
AR 609 of 1920, SII Vol XIX : PAZHUVETTARAYAN KODHANDAN
TAPPILDARMAN also donated 12.5 Kazhanju......
================================================

THEN COMES THE LIST OF RAJAKESARI'S (RAJARAJA's) 3RD YEAR OFFERINGS:
Example :
(Gangan Ambalavana Kandradithan @ Mummudichola Vizhupparayan
(PERUNDANAM OR HIGHLY PLACED OFFICIAL) : 614 of 1920 :: who
acquired about 12 veli of lands from Sarvisara Trinetra Dasapuriya
Bhattan, who himself acquired from Uthama)

THE ABOVE INSCRIPTIONS CLEARLY INDICATE THAT :

1. After Aditha Karikalan's death ( Oct 969!), those from various
Theinja Kaikkolapadai made offerings - recorded in the 2nd regenal
yr of Uthama Chola (971 - but Sundarachola was not there, since the
year is denoted as the ascendency of Uthamaa's year);

2. Atleast some of them committed suicide (on their behalf
offerings were made!)

3. VIPs included : Sembian Madevi, one Pazhuvettarayar and a
concubine - Madeviyar of AK;

4. Again, in the 3rd Regenal yr of Rajaraja - offerings were made;

5. The famous "KAANDALUR CHALAI Kalamarutharuli" event took place
in this year 988 only.

IT IS SOUTH INDIAN INSCRIPTIONS :: VOL XIX :: 22.

(AR no: 594 of 1920 - On the North Wall of Anantheeswarar temple)
(KOPPARAKESARI 2nd YEAR )
--------------------------
Quote:

This records a gift of 42 1/2 Kalanju of gold (tulaippon) with the
GANAPPERUMAKKALOF THE YEAR, for burning a perpetual lamp in the
temple and for the daily mid-day offering to the gold, with which a
BHATTA well-versed in the VEDAWAS to be fed able. The offering
comprised rice, KUMMAAYAM and three other dishes, ghee, curd, sugar,
plantains and beetal-nut and leaves. The donor's name is given as
KOYIL-PERRAL @ GUNAVAN MAHADEVIAR, daughter of BHUPALASEKHARIYAR on
whose behehalf the KING (DEVAR)is said to have paid. It is perhaps
to be inferred that she was his concubine.

-----------------------------------

On the South Wall (AR 537 1920)a PARAKESARI 2nd year inscription
SIMHA - YUVA - AMVATSYA - MONDAY - MAGHA is found equivalent to July
27, 968 .. (Uthama ? ) a NAKKAN BATTALAGI (a Song stress (musician)
attached to PERIYA VELAM (Royal Palace ) gave a grant !

On the same wall (AR 540 of 1920) a PARAKESARI 2nd year
inscription is found granting 96 sheep - for burning perpetual lamp
by PARANTAKAN MADEVADIGAL @ Sembian Madeviyar (This is what our
SWETHA quoted recently in SUPPORT OF MADEVADIGAL DENOTING SIVA
DEVOTEE and NOT necessarily a JAIN) the daughter of Malavaraiyar and
Queen of Merkezhundarulina Dhevar Gandadithadevar.
