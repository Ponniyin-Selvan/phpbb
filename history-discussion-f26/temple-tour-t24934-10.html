RRC is just named it after him, left few inscriptions and no
sculptures of him(or only one?). But if you look at Ramses II of Egypt
(Ramses the great), he build a massive temple Abu Simbel
(http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abu_Simbel) for Sun God where he even
shown equal to God (Sitting in Garbhagriha with the Gods) and you can
see only his statues (4 60 foot in the front and around 8 inside hall
on both sides) and his heroic activities on the wall. Same story at
Temple of Karnak (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karnak)

I can quote from Balakumaran where as soon as you look and compare the
temple with yourself, you will feel that you are nothing, you are not
even dust. I feel the same whenever I look at the vimana. And another
quote where "All kings palaces were destroyed and lost only the
temples remain" which again tells that we are just dust compare to
supreme and make us feel humble.

No offense meant.

by abhivencat on Wed Mar 05, 2008 9:26 pm

Hi
great
we should be humbled before god and his home.
i happened to travel yesterday to thirunindravur.
that was a place pusalar - no better than a beggar built a temple for
shiva.
he didnt build it out of stone and mortar. how could he? a man
struggling to be fed. but so great was his devotion that he built the
temple for the lord in his heart. today a temple stands where he
imagined the temple and aptly called hiruthayaalishwarar.
i wasnt keen to go to the temple. how could a monument of stone ever
replace that supreme devotion.
it was a devotion that was answered by god. who told the pallava
monarch in his dream that the pauper's imaginary temple was better
and preferred by him than the stone temple the monarch was raising.

yes sir
i was humbled when i crossed thiruninravur yesterday

venketesh

temple tour

by shobhainsf on Fri Mar 14, 2008 9:47 pm

Hi
Had an excellent trip of Thanjai &other temples in the area.
Dr. Kudavoyil said that ASI was not permitting going up, due to security reasons on account of Sivarathiri & the Natya Vizha.
However was overawed by the magnificence of The Big Temple & enjoyed seeing the temple in the late evening, a De Ja Vu kind of experience.
It was really painful to see people lighting lamps everywhere & throwing the paper plates (on which the lamps are sold) all over as also the empty plstic pkts after eating snacks from the prasada(?) stall.
Visited Big temple on day1 & also Mariamman, Bangaru Kamakshi & Vadabadrakali temples & went back again to Big temple.
Day2 : Thiruvaiyaru, Saint Thyagarayar's Samadhi ( was lucky to listen to a mini aradhanai which I believe they do every Sivarathri) P.S. Narayanaswamy, O.S.Arun & few other vocalists, Violinists & Mridangam players were going back from Unjavridhi & sang the Pancharatna Keerthanais.
Thirukandiyur, Thirumazhapadi, Thanjai Mamanikundram.
Day3- Tiruvarur, Thirukannamangai, Enkann & Arur Paravayul Mandali
Day4- Thirukaravasal, Thirukuvalai, Ettukudi, Thiruvaimoor, Velankanni, Nagaikaronam, Nagai Soundrarajaperumal, Sikkal, Thirunallar, Thirumarugal, Thiruchengaatangudi, Thirupugalur & Thirukannapuram (phew)! Had the Muniyodaran pongal prasadam aftrer ardhajamam in Thirukannapuram
Day5- Thiruvilamar, Thiru Alangudi, a quick look again at the Big Temple & back home.
 
The heat is opressive already, took me 3 days to get back the energy. But the experience was wonderful esp. got to see the Vidangar Abhishekam in 3 vidanga sthalams. Waking up thought since I returned is always of 'Siva'
 
 
Tiruvarur  also has a Lingam form by name  ' Putridamkondaar' or Vanmeeganathar.
I read in at least four websites that the ' Tyagaraja ' in all Vidangar sthalams is s'posed to be ' Somaskanda ' but the archagas did not mention it in any of these places nor is Skanda visible. Is it true that it is 'Somaskanda ' if so would they hide the Skanda underneath?
