--- Inponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, "Indumathi.S"
wrote:

Fm Taiwan ! I remeber your earlier mail too. Greetings ..

In the FIRST issue of http://www.varalaaru.com, an article under the caption:
Udayaloril Pallippadaya ? is published. In this article, the details
of inscriptions have been gone into.. and finally ending saying
nothing in the inscription is related to Pallippadai ..

The details are like this:

There was a Sivapathasekara Mangalam..

(Is it udayalur or nearby is yet to be idetified). In present day
Udayalur, there are about 3 temples. 1. Siva Temple. 2. Perumal
(Vinnagaram). 3. Paalkulathamman koil (on the banks of a village tank.
(In the entrance pillar exists the inscription - which is almost half
cemented - thanks to lack of concern to our History).

Kudanthai Sethuraman ( of Raman & Raman bus Service - mega
Industrialist) published a series of articles way back in 70 - which
included an article that this was related to RRC's samadhi;

(Now retired) Principal of Thiruvaiyaru Arts College - Dr. Thangamani
has supported this view (as Published in Vikadan Diwali Malar -
reported in the Gp earlier).

Kudavoil Dr. Balu (of Saraswathi Mahal Library Thanjavur / now
retired) claims that the detrails are 90% true (as reported in the
Junior Vikadan current issue..) but not 100% ..

What is NOT reported widely is THAT THESE INSCRIPTIONS are FOUND by
the British Archeologists as early in 1927-28 and Published in the
Annual Report of Epigraphy (ARE) under the heading "Samadhi Temples "
and " Pallipadais"

Sethuraman quoted apart from this inscription, other inscriptions also :

1. Easana Siva Pandithar (who was the chief Priest of the Big Temple )
was incharge of Sivapathasekaramangalam temple !

2. They were practicing Mahavradham and known as Mahavradhis. Which
is known as Paasupatha - Laguleesam and this sect normally maintains
Pallippadais and they did maintain the Pallippadai of Panchavan Madevi
- which is located about 3 KMs West of this temple !

3. The dilapidated Mandap (Mundril) was renovated - after the
renovator OBSERVED VRADHAMs ! - after about 100 years ! (There is an
instance where another Dilapidated Siva temple nearby was renovated,
but the renovator DID NOT perform Vradham ).

4. RRC - atleast after making RJC as Heir - apparent in 1012 AD (both
co-ruled for 3 years) - appeared to have become Godly person (Pls go
through Archives - Godification of RRC) - assumed the name
Sivapathasekaran and his idols were kept and worshipped in the Big
Temple even when he was alive and several other temples including
Papanasam - after he passed away.

5. Easana Siva Pandithar has installed his OWN IDOL in the big temple
- when he was alive ! and several grants were accorded!
Means he was kept in very high esteem by RRC as well as RJC!

Summarising ..

The line of thinking that RRC was Godified and his mortals were
accorded Godly status is not out of place ..

Whether Udayalur is the Venue is to be decided beyond traces of doubt ..

Time will reveal this .. may be sooner than we anticipate..

regards/ sps
