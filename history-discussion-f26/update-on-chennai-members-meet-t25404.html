Update on Chennai members meet

by satish_arun on Mon Nov 22, 2004 5:15 pm

Dear all,

The Marina Meet was a success and we all enjoyed it very much. A
breif update on what happend at Marina and later.

After the confirmation from the group mates, I reached Marina around
5.55PM but could not take a right turn to the parking lot and took
quite a long drive throuh the beach road. By the time I took a U
turn, found a parking lot which was quite some distance from the
Gandhi statue, parked my car and reached the premisis, it was 6.15PM.
I saw three people discussing seriously and thought they should be
the PS members. But was a BIT reluctant to approach as I was not
sure,because they were talking very freely and didnt look like a
first time meeting. I thought I will go near them and if they are PS
group members, they will call me. As expected, when I went near them,
I heard a voice "Ponnyin Selvan" and thats it, jothiyil aikiyam
ayittom. Mr.Ganesan, Shankar, Kripa were there and we got introduced
to each other.
Shortly Kamal joined us and soon Mr.Saravanan and Mr.Rajesh(not PS
memebers) joined the group. We discussed many things about PS, Chola,
history etc and in between Saravanan and Rajesh left as they had some
work. So we five continued our discussion. Lavanya joined the
discussion sometime later..puzzeled how she came into the
picture..she joined through teleconference. She called Kamal on his
mobile and over the speaker phone we talked for sometime. Kamal gave
a briefing about the history of the group, about the groups
interaction with Dr.Kalaikovan, and many news to us about the group
and group members.

We never realised that the time was 7.40PM and its already late for
us to leave. So we came to the agenda of the meeting, our trip around
chennai. We all felt that a trip should be made once a month,because
only if we target once a month, once a quarter atleast will be
possible. But all were supporting the thought that once a month a
trip can be organized and whoever is free during that time can join.
Kamal suggested we make a trip tomorrow iteself.ie. sunday 21st. We
agreed, but Mr.Ganesan said his brother is coming and so he cannot
join.Kamal called Lavanya and she said if shes free she will join
us.Kamal suggested that we will go to Madambakkam and Manimangalam.
We decided the time to be 3 pm and all dispersed around 8PM. It was
great meeting the unknown faces whom we met only through mails.

Sunday I left my house and reached the planned place in Pallavaram at
3.05PM. Kamal was waiting there and within minutes Shankaran joined
us. We three started in my car and headed for Manimangalam. Kamal
said, being a sunday, Madamabakkam will be crowded for Rahukala pujai
and so we skipped it from our itenary. Throughout our journey we
listened to a cassette , which Shankaran had brought. It had the
conversation between Kudavoyil Balu and Balakumaran regarding the big
temple, its construction, uthama cholan, etc. We also discussed about
the same.

By 4.10PM we reached the 'Dharmeswarar' Temple in Manmangalam. The
place was exotic. Lush gree fields around and a small but majestic
temple structure in betwee were a treat to the eyes.The temple, as
per ASI sign board was built by Narasima Pallavan III after his
deciding war with Pulikesi III at this place. It was locked and we
were told that in the evening its generally not opened and we can go
and call the archakar who resides nearby. We went to his house and
the archakar came and opened the temple for us. Had a good Dharshan
of Darmeswarar and Devambikai. Felt really bad that such a beautiful
temple doesnt attract any crowd. As soon as we entered and had our
dharshan, Mr. Ganesan called me on my mobile to enquire about the
status. He really missed the trip which was evident in his voice.
Kamal explained to us about the architecture, the different parts of
the temple, the difference between the Pallava construction and that
of the cholas etc. Shankaran and I were realy thrilled to see and
learn such things.(en maramandaila niraya erala, cant rememeber much
of the terminologies which Kamal used but conceptually I could
understand things...maybe it will take many more trips for me to
memorise them...-:) ).

Then we left to 'Rajagopala Swamy' Temple another one and a half km
away. The temple was a bigger than the sivan temple, with big garden
(not properly maintainted) with rose plantations.This temple is also
around 1300 years old. Kamal already warned us that we should not get
tensed seeing things inside the temple. We didnt understand that
untill we entered the temple. On almost all the walls of the temple,
large number of kalvettu were found, but we understood what Kamal
warned us. Everything has been covered by white and maroon chunnambu,
in the name of painting the walls. We were shell shocked to see that.
Kamal also showed a kalvettu of RR, which the varalaru.com team found
during the visit with Dr.KK. The archakar told us that the speciality
of the temple is that, the sangu and the chakram of the perumal are
otherway here, ie. the chakram on the left and the sangu on the
right. He said its the only temple like this, but inspite of its
speciality its not one among the 108 divyadesam. He said it
symbolises that the lord is blessing Arjuna before the war with his
sangu ready to be blown.Another thing is that he is calling all
the 'pasu madu' by blowing his sangu with his right hand.

We three discussed many more things related to cholas and set off to
chennai at 5.30. At 6.15 I dropped both of them at Pallavaram and
went home with a great feeling of satisfaction.

Hope I didnt bother you all by this lengthy description. I feel
really happy and thirrled by this trip. Though short, it was a sweet
one. A stepping stone for me into the great history. Hope to learn
more from many more trips in this group.

Regards,
Satish
