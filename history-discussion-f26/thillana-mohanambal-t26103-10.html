Hi Malathi
Yes TM is a wonderful work by Subbu. The bound version from AV which has Gopulu's pics. ( how beatiful Mohana looks) is a treasure, but since AV does not do any reruns of novels serialised , you do not get a good set of bound volumes. The paper is brittle & the volumes are dog earred etc. After a lot of hunting I settled down for the Palaniappa edition

If you stay long enough in Chennai you can borrow from a lending library & enjoy TM along with Gopulu's pics.

dear venkat

i would differ on that, even though the concept
was god send - i
think the whole curriculum is ...... more with an
aim to cram and
pass the exam - which unfortunately was ( is) how
students see the
exam. it is refreshing for eg, when we see how the
curriculum for
the local schools in singapore is - its more hands
on, instead of
read / memorise - the focus is on fundamentals and
understanding.
frankly tamil was a more dreaded subject to me
than even maths. it
was a chore even to read the text let alone look
outside...so at
that time the notes were best - mug /vomit...fill
it shut it forget
it. ( the best part was i still remember - they
were passed on from
hand to hand - so cant even call it second
hand...and i still
remember mine was a full 8 year old book.....)
nothing changed...the
questions remained the same ( maybe the guys
setting the papers
refered to the notes as well)....so easiest was to
skip ( sleep) all
tamil class - take last 5 years paper and you
could work out 10
questions and ( no need to even catch the ant....)
- you had almost
80% of the possible question paper on hand. i was
a past expert at
this form of divining...

while on the subject, my english teacher was smart
- she would
prepare us for the larger goals at hand - her
monthly test were very
simple - 2 questions - 50 marks each....simple
questions but aimed
at bringing out the best in you in form of command
over the
language...she used to push us to read a lot of
other texts/novels (
hardy boys, secret seven, famous five, nancy drew
included)...

it stood us good steed, when we worked on
professional exams -
infact our proffessors would encourage open book
exams - believe me
they are the toughest...

that why now in hindsight, i blame the system for
not instigating a
love for the language...which could have been so
easy for something
as divine as tamil.....instead the notes, gave an
easy escape route.
we won the war but lost the battle..

Shoba, yes Gopulu's pictures were beautiful. Have you noticed how much
Vaitthi looks like Nagesh? My mother used to say this character was

I would love to read it again. I had no idea chennai lending libraries
had such classics!!

by thirumalaikv on Fri Apr 25, 2008 7:24 am

There are lot of treasure book stalls in chennai, we call them second
hand book stalls and others are lending library. I have seen
Triplicane beach side book stalls, Luz corner book stalls and another
one near Mount Road and Higginbothoms, I hope the book stall is still
there which is small where I used to buy computer books. All these
second hand book stalls are treasures, if you visit once in a while
and buy, then you can have a little library. Other man's waste our
treasure

My brother used to buy Kumudam and other weekly books specifically to
extract Sandilyan novel episodes and bind them.
