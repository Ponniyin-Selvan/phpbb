The name February is believed to have derived from the name 'Februa' taken
from the Roman 'Festival of Purification'. The root 'februo' meaning to 'I
purify by sacrifice'.

*Architectural Heritage*

- *Raipur Haat, Chattisgarh:* The project work is progressing well.
150 boundary wall columns have been casted, museum blocks RCC base, column
footings and reinforcement have been done. Garbage clearing and digging has
been completed in Food Court/Craft shots Zone 'Z'.
- *Moti Mahal, Gwalior: *The restoration work at Moti Mahal Complex is
progressing well.

**

*Indian Council of Conservation Institutes (ICCI) *

- *National Mission for Manuscripts: *

*Lucknow**: * The Lucknow Centre carried out conservation of 1057 folios of
5 paper manuscripts received from Sri Mahasaraswati Vidyapeeth, Etawah, Sri
Nemi Nath Digambar Jain Mandir, Chowk, Lucknow, Shri Valmiki Shodh Sansthan,
Ayodhya, and Porf. Shyam Sundar Shukla, Varanasi.

*Bhubaneshwar: * The work of survey and preventive conservation of
manuscripts progressed at the following places - Utkal University, Berhampur
University, Orissa State Museum, and Orissa State Archives. Preventive
conservation of 90 palm leaf manuscripts having 11,207 folios and curative
conservation of 119 manuscripts with 1951 folios was carried out.

*Bangalore:* 128 palm leaf manuscripts having 12,300 folios belonging to two
different collectors were given preventive conservation treatment. Curative
conservation was carried out in 7 palm leaf manuscripts belonging to United
Theological College, Bangalore. Four important collections were surveyed and
will be taken up for conservation in due course.

*Tripunithura:* Preventive conservation of 30 books of paper manuscripts
having 3,300 folios and curative conservation of 3 palm leaf manuscripts
having 389 folios was carried out.

- *K R Cama Oriental Institute, Mumbai (Phase 3):* (Bhubaneshwar
Centre) - 2853 pages of important manuscripts were conserved and 12
manuscripts were bound.
- *Conservation of Murals at Moti Mahal, Gwalior:* (Lucknow Centre)
Conservation work is in progress.
- *Jain Projects:* (Lucknow Centre) Preventive conservation of
manuscripts at Sri Numinath Digambar Jain Temple was carried out.
- *Conservation work at Umaid Bhawan Palace, Jodhpur:* (Jodhpur
Centre) Conservtion of 28 miniature paintings in Umaid Bhawan Palace was
carried out.
- *Kalari Kovalikam Palace, Kollangode (Kerala):* 2 oil paintings are
under restoration.
- *Napier Museum, Thiruvananthapuram Phase II:* (Tripunithura Centre)
Conservation work is being carried out on 14 images.
- *Kolkata Centre:* Conservation work was carried out on 4 oil
paintings, 2 paper photographs, and one cloth painting.

*Workshops and Meets*

- *Curative Conservation of Manuscripts: *A 45-day Internship on
Curative Conservation of Manuscripts started at ICCI Lucknow on Jan 18,
2007. The participants are undergoing an extensive training on curative
conservation of palm leaf and paper manuscripts.
- Mr Deepak Joseph attended training on conservation of manuscripts
organized by National Mission of Manuscripts at Lucknow from Dec 1, 06 to
Jan 15, 07.


*Heritage Education *
Heritage Education and Communication Service (HECS)

- *Heritage Clubs in Schools, Varanasi: *A teacher training workshop
on Setting up Heritage Clubs in Schools was organized by the Varanasi
Chapter. This workshop at the Theosophical Society of India (TSI) was
attended by 36 teachers from 16 schools. The workshop had sessions on local
heritage and culture by INTACH Members. HECS conducted sessions on heritage
education and formation of heritage clubs in schools. Mr Sundaram,
Secretary, TSI, conducted a site visit for teachers in the TSI campus that
is rich in natural and built heritage.
- *Art Education in Delhi Schools: *Following the huge success of
INTACH's outreach programme for the month long contemporary Indian Art
exhibition at National Gallery of Modern Art, HECS is receiving requeststo
conduct such programmes for schools and other exhibitions. A programme is
being developed to undertake workshops, activities and resouce development
for contemporary art education in schools.
- *Y**oung INTACH Newsletter: *The Jan-March 2007 issue on 'Costumes
of India' has been printed and posted to all Heritage Clubs, Governing
Council Members and Embassies. The Young INTACH Newsletter is now
open for individual subscriptions also @ Rs 50 per year (4 issues).
