Dear Sampath, Nice subject.

NARAKKAN KRISHNAN IRAMAN, served under Rajaraja I (Not to be confused
with Anirudh Brahma Rayar - PS) from 1002 to 1014 and under Rajendra
I upto 1018 or thereabouts, belonged to the brahmadeya village called
AMANKUDI alias KERALANTAKA - CHATURVEDIMANGALAM in Ven-nadu or
Uyyakondar Valanadu.

iN His name NARAKKAN was the ancestral village from where he hailed;
KRISHNAN was his father's name. IRAMAN was his Personal name!
His title was Mummudichozha Brahma Marayan when served under RR I and
Rajendra Chozha Brahma marayan under Rajendra I. The title consists
of three parts : King's name + brhama + status (marayan). Marayan @
Maharajan, Adhirajan @ Adhiraiyan are also titles. He was Senapathi
from 1002 to 1006, olai nayagam 1008/9, and again Senapathy from 1010
to 1015 snf Olai nayagam till 1018.

His son 1 was Narakkan Iraman Arumozhi @ Uthamachoha brahma Marayan!
(1018 - 1034)- Senapathy.
Son 2 was Narakkan Marayan Jananathan @ Rajendra Chozha Brahma -
Adhirajan (1019 - 1028) Naduvirkai in 1019;
Son 3 was Narakkan Marayan Rajarajan @ Rajaraja Brahma Marayan 1025 -
1028. Olai nayagam.

An inscription in 1034 (SII V 636 / 637), refers to one Iraman
Chozhakula Sundariar - making a mention to " our VENKATESH'S
THIRUMALPADI " - PRESUMABLY Krishnan Raman's daughter - who was the
wife of Brahmana Kuravai Narana-Kiramavittar @ Madurantaka Brahma
Marayan!

There is a mention of Narakkan Marayan Gangaikonda Chozhan @ Uthama
Chozha Brahma marayan - in the year 1063, served as Olai Nayagam, who
might have been Krishnan Iraman's GRAND-SON!

Adhikari (official) , Senapathi (In Military), Olai Nayagam (In
Secretariat), Danda Nayagam (Supreme Commander / Military),
Naduvirukkai (Judicial) are superior officials in Chozha State.

Similarly Moovendavelalan / Vizhupparayan were also titles conferred
on other officials in Chozha State - more related agricultural /
irrigation activities.

Anbudan - sps

dear Meena
thiru muthu kundram ( vridachalam)means old
this hill was formed before and vanished before the rest
the rock bed below the temple is given as evidence.
however the name old is added as prefix to the god and amman
sundarar refuses to sing here because he is irritated by the name.
why sing about an old man?
so another idol- balambika is installed and sundarar sings

what does he sing?
he asks for gold for his dancer wife
then he drops the gold in the mani muthar here and picks it up in
thiruvarur temple tank- kamalalayam.

(so tamils first thought of speed transfer of funds)

co incidentally balaambika is the name of the second amman in
malapaaadi and thirukadaiyur( perhaps they meant junior amman)


regards
venketesh

Uyyakondar Valanadu.

iN His name NARAKKAN was the ancestral village from where he
hailed;
KRISHNAN was his father's name. IRAMAN was his Personal name!
His title was Mummudichozha Brahma Marayan when served under
RR
I
and
Rajendra Chozha Brahma marayan under Rajendra I. The title
consists
of three parts : King's name + brhama + status (marayan).
Marayan
@
Maharajan, Adhirajan @ Adhiraiyan are also titles. He was
Senapathi
from 1002 to 1006, olai nayagam 1008/9, and again Senapathy
from
1010
to 1015 snf Olai nayagam till 1018.

His son 1 was Narakkan Iraman Arumozhi @ Uthamachoha brahma
Marayan!
(1018 - 1034)- Senapathy.
Son 2 was Narakkan Marayan Jananathan @ Rajendra Chozha
Brahma -
Adhirajan (1019 - 1028) Naduvirkai in 1019;
Son 3 was Narakkan Marayan Rajarajan @ Rajaraja Brahma
Marayan
1025 -
1028. Olai nayagam.

An inscription in 1034 (SII V 636 / 637), refers to one
Iraman
Chozhakula Sundariar - making a mention to " our VENKATESH'S
THIRUMALPADI " - PRESUMABLY Krishnan Raman's daughter - who
was
the
wife of Brahmana Kuravai Narana-Kiramavittar @ Madurantaka
Brahma
Marayan!

There is a mention of Narakkan Marayan Gangaikonda Chozhan @
Uthama
Chozha Brahma marayan - in the year 1063, served as Olai
Nayagam,
who
might have been Krishnan Iraman's GRAND-SON!

Adhikari (official) , Senapathi (In Military), Olai Nayagam
(In
Secretariat), Danda Nayagam (Supreme Commander / Military),
Naduvirukkai (Judicial) are superior officials in Chozha
State.

Similarly Moovendavelalan / Vizhupparayan were also titles
conferred
on other officials in Chozha State - more related
agricultural /
irrigation activities.
