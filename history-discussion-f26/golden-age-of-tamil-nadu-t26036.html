Golden Age of Tamil Nadu

by venkdhivakar on Sat Apr 12, 2008 12:38 pm

Dear SPS, 
 
In a recent post, you have said that the ruling period of Rajaraja Chola and his son Rajendra (985 CE to 1044) was said to be a golden period.
 
In general to define a golden period, what are the credential needed? The Gupta's period during Chandragupta-1 - Samudhra Gupta - & Vikramathiya, was already mentioned in history books of India as 'Golden Age of India'. So many sanscritic classics works were born during that period including Kalidasa.
 
Rajaraja's rule was remarkably well during that period except some warring times. The wars also were won, and the people said to be living in happy, so that the King was having enough  time in spending his concentration on Big Temple and other connected works, apart from the other administrative handlings. The period was generally calm. The Kingbrought out a democratic ruling culture in village level. The able administration was another crediteria so that to keep federal cheif's under the King's control. Most importantly nature also  have co-operated so that not many calamities.
 
But this situation was extended till Kulottungan-2 and even his son thatwas upto 1185 CE.
So can we say the years of 985 to 1185 CE or upto 1200 CE was the Golden Age of
Tamilnadu? Like north India, the tamil classical works like collections of Nambi-andar-Nambi, Periyapuranam, Kalingattu Parani and Kambaramayanam were born in those periods. Scores of Temples were built and art works displayed all over the state. Except some small times during Vikraman ruling there were not much natural calamities. Almost wars ended with Northern neighbores.
 
These 200 years, the whole Tamilnadu and Kerala as well  of eastern part of present Karnataka and Southern Andhra (almost like British Madras State during Pre-independent India,) was under the complete control of Cholas. Most importantly except some warring times, the peace prevailed almost in all the part of this particular chola country.
 
Great Philosophers like Ramanuja, Meykantar were born in this period. And intellectual think-tank was highly seen as satisfactory in this period. Naval strength was commandable. Sea pirates were not seen in the eastern sea, so the merchandism was well handled at ports. Economically most of 200 years the chola country was in strongest terms of wealth management..
 
I have taken only positive happenings for defining the Golden Age. You can add more inputs. 
 
Dhivakar.

Third Edition (Not revised) - 2004. Contains only 105 pages devoted
to Sangam Arts, Tamilnadu Temples, Koil ilakkanam, varalaru,
gopurangal, sirpangal, bronze and paintings (all in Tamilnadu
temples).

Sangam Arts deals with Vaasthu & Building designs (Noolari Pulavar -
Nedunalvadai) - Koil (wood - clay Kottams) - Aranmanai - Entrance in
which elephants with flags could pass through !) - Paintings
===========================================================
(Chithira maadam) - Thugiligai - Oviam - Chithiram - PADAM -
PADAAM -
=========================================================


One is Pa- da - m : Picture : of human / natural scenes etc.,
Another is Pa - daam : Picture of God / Goddess.

In early ages, Sudhai idols were worshipped. Pictures of Gods /
Goddess were painted over the clothes and they were worshipped in
the temples. Eloborate process is also discussed in the subject
book. When the Padaam becomes worn out, it is necessary to replace
the same and the worn-out Padaam is known as NIRMALYAM.

Present Govindaraja in Thillai is established only in the 15th
Century.

During Chozhas period padaam of Govindaraja is only to have been
under worship. Nirmalyam - to avoid contact with human foot - used
to be put in the sea - after Balalayam - the Power of God / goddess
transferred temporarily to another object of worhsip till the
ultimate object of worship is put position and life inducted.

It was only Nirmalyam padaam was put in the sea..

Chozhas - for that matter all the Tamil Kings - are all more
secular - permitting worship of various Faiths including Buddha /
Jain and gave grants / constructed Viharas.
