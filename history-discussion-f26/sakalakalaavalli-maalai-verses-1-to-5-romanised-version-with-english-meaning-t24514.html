SakalaKalaaValli Maalai

Romanised Version with English Meaning

1.

veN-dhaamaraikku anRi nin padham thaangga en veLLai yuLLam
thaN-thaamaraikkuth thagaadhu kolO sagam Elzum aLiththu
uNdaan uRangga, olziththaan piththaaga, uNdaakkum vaNNam
kaNdaan suvaikoL karumbE! sakala-kalaa-valliyE

The God VishNu is the preserver/protector of the seven worlds. He also
devours them and sleeps in the Cosmic Ocean. The God Siva is the
destroyer of the seven worlds. He becomes a piththan. This is caused
by the power of creation of Brahma. O Goddess Sakala Kalaa Valli! You
are the sweet sugar cane enjoyed by Brahma. While Your Feet are being
borne by the White Lotus, does'nt the cool lotus of my heart have the
privilege and honou possessed by that White Lotus?

2.

naadum soRchuvai porut-chucai thOythara naaRkaviyum
paadum paNiyil paNiththu aruLvaay! panggaya aasanaththil
kuudum pasumpoR-kodiyE! kana-dhanak kunRum aimbaal
kaadum sumakkum karumbE! sakala-kalaa-valliyE!

You are a fresh tender tendril which is seated on a seat of lotus.
You possess hill-like breasts. You have hair dressed with five styles
which also resembles a forest. You are adorned with that, O Devi Who
is sweet like the sugar-cane. There are four types of poetry - aasu,
madhuram, chiththiram, and madhuram. Let those types be enriched by
the richness of meaning and floral verbal beauties. I seek that boon
from you.

3.

aLikkum selzum-thamilzth theL amudhu aarndhu un arut-kadalil
kuLikkum padikku enRu kuudum-kolO? uLam-koNdu theLLith
theLikkum panuval pulavOr kavi-malzai sindhak-kaNdu
kaLikkum kalaaba mayilE! sakala-kalaa-valliyE!

With melted hearts, poets who are capable of sprinkling literary
creations with clarity, pour forth torrents of poetry and You enjoy
that, O Sakalakalaavalli! Would I be able to imbibe the ambrosia of
rich Tamil bestowed by You, and bathe in the sea of Your Grace?

4.

thuukkum panuval thuRai thOyndha kalviyum soR-chuvai-thOy
vaakkum perugap paNiththu aruLvaay! vadanuul kadalum
thEkkum selzum-thamilzch chelvamum thoNdar sennaavil ninRu
kaakkum karuNaik kadalE! sakala-kalaa-valliyE!

Being seated on the blessed tongues of devotees, You preserve the
wealth of Classical Tamil and the sea of Sanskrit. O Sakalakalaavalli,
the Sea Mercy! You must bestow me with speech which is rich with
sweetness of words and education which is obtained by rationality in
many fields of knowledge.

5.

panju appu, idhamtharum seyya poR-paadha panggErugam enna
nenjath-thadaththu alaraadhadhu ennE? nedum-thaaL kamalaththu
anjath-thuvasam uyarndhOn sennaavum agamum veLLaik
kanjath-thavisu oththirundhaay! sakala-kalaa-valliyE!

Brahma has the lotus with a long stalk as his flag. You consider His
tongue and His heart as being same as Your seat of white lotus; and
You are seated upon His tongue and heart,
O Sakalakalaavalli! Your beautiful red golden lotus Feet which bestow
all the goodness; why does it not blossom in the pond of my heart?
