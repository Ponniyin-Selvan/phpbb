--- "S.Swaminathan" wrote:
Dear Krupa Shankar

I read your mail with ineterest. I may reply to some
of the queries you have
made.

You have asked whether prakrit (or prakritham, as we
Tamils would call) is
no more a language. All the Indo-germanic languages
of India, for example,
Hindi, Bengali, Gujaarti, Marathi and Odiya, for
that matter all Indian
languages you find in the currecy notes excluding
the four south Indian
languages and Kashmiri, Sindhi and Urdu, all
developed from various local
dialects of Sanskrit. Generally it is said all these
are decendents of
Sanskrit. There is still some lack of finality
whetehr these languages
decended directly from Sanskrit or fromt he dialects
of Prakrit.

The famed village, called mattur in the Shimoga
district in Karnataka speaks
Sanskrit, a great tradition. The tradition is
because of certain historical
reasons. I can send you more information on this if
you want.

You have asked "The present Devanagari script was
there long back? You have
any idea how long this was present? Devangari
script did not undergo any
"seerthiruththam" in those days?"

The Devanagari script came into being rather later
around the Gupta time.
The famous Girnar inscription of the 6th century is
the earliest extant
inscription. This script, like script of all Indian
languages developed from
Brahmi. Though there were lot changes in the
characters, there was no
'seerthiruththam' in it, like Panini wrought on the
Sanskrit language in the
6th century BC. Perhaps you may know that the
special feature of Devanagari
is syllabic, just suited to to the prosodic
requirement of Sanskrit. If I
have not made myself clear on this last point, I can
elaborate, if you want
to know. But we may be digressing.


You wanted to know whether i will have any
objection in sharing my replies
with members of the Group. I have no objections
whatsoever. I was only
wondering whether what I write would be of general
interst. In fact I would
be very happy to discuss further on the points
raised above.

At once stage some one from your group wanted to
know whetehr i can start a
serial on the Ajanta paintings in the egroup. I
didn't hear anything on this
after I had informed my readiness.

It would give a gretaa pleasure to be in touch with
you, more when I can
stimulate people like you.

With best wishes
Swaminathan
