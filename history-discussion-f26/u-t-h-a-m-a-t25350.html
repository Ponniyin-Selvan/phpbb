Dear Venkat & Gokul,

Undoubtedly this is a very interesting thread.

Characterising UTHAMA should have haunted Kalki and VERY
BRILLIANTLY he split the characterisation into TWO and fascinated
all of us.

While Characterising, Sembian Madevi also should have posed
challenges to Kalki.

Here let us keep certain things in mind :

When Udayaalur Pallippadai details are discussed among us NOW, most
of the discussions zero on Dr. Thangamani (Princi / Thiruvayaru
Arts - 10 yrs back).. Some go back to Kudanthai Sethuraman (Raman &
Raman Bus) active in 1975 - 87 or so.

But the British (ASI) have published the details as early in 1927-
28.

Means even though published 60 yrs back, no one took notice of RRC's
pallipadai-speculated temple till 1975 / 85!

Same way, Sastri / Pandarathar details, based on Hultzch findings,
who concentrated on releasing ASI Volumes - first of which initially
dealt with Kanchi Kailasanathar temple, Big temple, Anbil -
thiruvalangadu copper plates etc., only.

Kalki relied on these details alone for his work. Udayarkudi
finding was nearer and newer by about 25 years when he wrote PS in
1950.

Inscriptions-reading in Thirunallam @ Konerirajapuram and other
prominent temples were taken up by ASI later and direct access to
inscriptions would have been difficult at such an early stage.

Following inputs would have helped Kalki in characterisations:

KANDRADITHAR - Thiruvisaippa - God fearing King relinquishing Power.
SEMBIANMADEVI - converting brick structures to Katrali - Devoted
Saivite - leading saintly life, committing more to religion than
committing sati and wasting life.
UTHAMA 1 - Peaceful Pious King
UTHAMA 2 - Counter balance
ARUMOZHI - Giving away Mahudam - Thiruvalangadu Copper plates
RAVIDASAN & CO.. - Udayarkudi
Sundara - Anirudh - Anbil CP;
Sundara - Periapazhuvettarayar - Pazhuvur inscriptions.
Arumozhi - Kundavai nexus - Big temple inscriptions.

Now we have more materials in hand - with State Archlgy working on
more than 10000 inscriptions last year alone.

That is how picturisation gets closer and clearer.

After Kandraditha though Sundara occupied Power, his declaring
Aditha Karikala as Heir-apparent should have attracted criticisms
and Uthama should have got help from certain quarters which made him
occupy the position. But his political free-will wings would have
been clipped. RRC could have ultilised the period for stabilising.

After coming to power, RRC could have methodically removed the
clips on the political free-will wings.. realising UTHAMA's JUST and
also helplessness.

In Karanthai @ Karunthattankudi (near Thanjai) inscriptions,
Rajendra calls himself UTHAMA !

As a matter of fact there are 3 similarities between Rajendra and
UTHAMA s/o Sembianmadevi !

PARAKESARI UTHAMA RAJENDRA
MADURANTHAKAN UTHAMA RAJENDRA
UTHAMA UTHAMA RAJENDRA

Let us try to put History by adding new inputs to available
informations first.

I have read that Kalki kept extensive hand notes and detailed breakup of
chapters with references. It will be very useful if such information is
still available and accessible. Even though Kalki passed away after
writing a few chapters of Amaradhara, his daughter was able to complete
the novel with the help of the notes left by him. He also visited the
places (including Srilanka) he used in his novels. He might have
received additional inputs - may be even from the local people.

It is not a bad idea to collate all the reference material used by
Kalki, Balakumaran, Akilan and others. This may be stored in the Files
section for use by members.

Study of Indology has been well supported and funded by many
universities. Tamil is not so lucky. Millions of man and scholar hours
are needed to collect hard evidence, study existing evidence and propose
viable theories.

A loose remark about drunk and gold hungry poets has generated lot of
hot air, as the recently reported speech by Vairamuthu about inebriated
poets. (Kalki magazine was a lead player in this episode) We can now
move on.

Dear venkat

Dr Poovannan's Phd thesis "Kalkiyin varalaatru naavalgal - oru aivu"
provides rare gilmpses on how he wrote the novel. HE REFERRED ALL THE
NOTES OF KALKI WITH THE ACTUAL HISTORY BOOKS HE USED.

In all probability it could have happened like this:

- Kalki first read Cholas in which KAN straight away accused uttama
for aditya's murder. This left lasting impression in kalki's mind and
hence the character old mathuranthakan. At this point of time he had
no intention of making senthan a major character. If you read the
initial chapters and the introduction of senthan amudhan you have no
clue about what he is going to become..

- The Book "Pirkaalach chozhar sariththiram" came out as the novel was
in progress. This book was used page by page by kalki. Here sadasiva
pandarathar arguued that KAN was wrong in accusing uttama. Kalki
should have been in a fix. But he was a powerful writer who could
twist things in a convincing manner

His son rajendran recollects: "...One day kalki was doing sirasaasana
and suddenly he got up and said - why not make senthan a raaja?"

Thus the decision to make uttama a pious man was a sudden decision of
kalki. If you analyse the events leading to uttama's accession they
look slightly confusing. kalki was still powerful enough to convince
the readers and nobody made big noise.

THAT was the power of kalki's pen.
