Dear Vijay,

posting a sample related to Annual Epigraphy Report::

--- Inponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, "Sivapathasekaran"
wrote: 15391 ::

In the FIRST issue of http://www.varalaaru.com, an article under the
caption:

Udayaloril Pallippadaya ? is published. In this article, the
details of inscriptions have been gone into.. and finally ending
saying nothing in the inscription is related to Pallippadai ..

The details are like this:

There was a Sivapathasekara Mangalam..

(Is it udayalur or nearby is yet to be idetified). In present day
Udayalur, there are about 3 temples. 1. Siva Temple. 2. Perumal
(Vinnagaram). 3. Paalkulathamman koil (on the banks of a village
tank. (In the entrance pillar exists the inscription - which is
almost half cemented - thanks to lack of concern to our History).

Kudanthai Sethuraman ( of Raman & Raman bus Service - mega
Industrialist) published a series of articles way back in 70 - which
included an article that this was related to RRC's samadhi;

(Now retired) Principal of Thiruvaiyaru Arts College - Dr.
Thangamani has supported this view (as Published in Vikadan Diwali
Malar - reported in the Gp earlier).

Kudavoil Dr. Balu (of Saraswathi Mahal Library Thanjavur / now
retired) claims that the detrails are 90% true (as reported in the
Junior Vikadan current issue..) but not 100% ..

What is NOT reported widely is THAT THESE INSCRIPTIONS are FOUND
by the British Archeologists as early in 1927-28 and Published in
the Annual Report of Epigraphy (ARE) under the heading "Samadhi
Temples " and " Pallipadais"

Sethuraman quoted apart from this inscription, other inscriptions
also :

1. Easana Siva Pandithar (who was the chief Priest of the Big
Temple ) was incharge of Sivapathasekaramangalam temple !

2. They were practicing Mahavradham and known as Mahavradhis. Which
is known as Paasupatha - Laguleesam and this sect normally maintains
Pallippadais and they did maintain the Pallippadai of Panchavan
Madevi - which is located about 3 KMs West of this temple !

3. The dilapidated Mandap (Mundril) was renovated - after the
renovator OBSERVED VRADHAMs ! - after about 100 years ! (There is
an instance where another Dilapidated Siva temple nearby was
renovated, but the renovator DID NOT perform Vradham ).

4. RRC - atleast after making RJC as Heir - apparent in 1012 AD
(both co-ruled for 3 years) - appeared to have become Godly person
(Pls go through Archives - Godification of RRC) - assumed the name
Sivapathasekaran and his idols were kept and worshipped in the Big
Temple even when he was alive and several other temples including
Papanasam - after he passed away.

5. Easana Siva Pandithar has installed his OWN IDOL in the big
temple - when he was alive ! and several grants were accorded!
Means he was kept in very high esteem by RRC as well as RJC!

Summarising ..

The line of thinking that RRC was Godified and his mortals were
accorded Godly status is not out of place ..

Whether Udayalur is the Venue is to be decided beyond traces of
doubt ..

Time will reveal this ..

regards/ sps

DEAR VIJAY,

This is a very very interesting and important inscription ::

This inscription runs to 107 lines.

3rd and 4th line :: pertains to Grants made in 25th yr 312th day.
6 to 9 :: 26th yr 14th Day
10 to 16 :: 26th yr 27th day
17 :: 26th yr 34th day

18th :: 25th Yr 275th day
19th to 32 :: 26th yr 104th day
33 :: 26th yr 318th day
34 to 50 :: 26th yr 319th day
51 to 107 :: 23rd to 29th yr

In the light of the above when we read the inscriptions, we can
understand clearly that (1) THESE INSCRIPTIONS WERE SCRIBED AFTER
THE 29TH YEAR; (2) EVEN SUCH GRANTS OF 23RD YR WERE SCRIBED AFTER
29TH YEAR ONLY (3) 18th Line indicates that Grants made in 26th Yr
preceded the Grant made in 25th Yr (Gopura Kalasam !).

He has assumed the title Rajaraja even prior to 23rd Yr.

Exactly when, will find and let you all know.
