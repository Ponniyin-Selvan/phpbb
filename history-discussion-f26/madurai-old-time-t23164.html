MADURAI - OLD TIME

by venkdhivakar on Thu May 03, 2007 6:42 pm

Modern times Madurai ok! - but can we see how the old Madurai looks like, A description of Madurai at the time of Pandyan Nedunjadaiyan, based on the information provided in Maduraikkanji (Last Tamil Sangham period). Mr.Sundaram Pillai published this in Madras Christian College Magazine in the year `1901. (First - Madurai during day hours.............)
----------------------------------------------------------------------
It was probably with the early dawn that we started towards the city from the Vaigai side, and we may, therefore suppose that it was seven or so when we got to the streets. They are, however, by that time quite busy and full.

We now reach the Gateways, which are as high as the cloud-capped mountains, and which are secured by doors, brightened by frequent offerings of oil to the goddess guarding them.

We elbow our way through the crowded gateways flooded ceaselessly with passengers, as the Vaigai is with the freshes. The streets are broad as river and the houses too, on either side are well ventilated with many windows. The streets are with endless traffic and eternal din, the different aspect of which, through out the twenty four hours of the day.

The surging crowds move to and fro and in the confusion of tongues they create, we hear nothing but a roar of sea when lashed by the high winds. The drumming, singing and dancing in diverse quarters would show, that pleasure pleasure is even more noisy than business.

With our ears thus assailed on all sides, we resign ourselves to sight seeing.

Here and there, everywhere over each liquor shop dance the merry streamers with unparalleled virtues of the beverage retailed by their owners. Recalling our eyes from them to observe what is closure to us, plenty of petty vendors of sweetmeats flowers, garlands, perfumes, betel, nuts surrounding us asking to purchase.

Madurai being a capital, the ordinary traffic is now and then stopped or suspended by the occasional appearance of King?s forces, elephants that break from their bondage, and rush from the royal stables into streets. Chariots swift as winds, horses that march for camp exercises and worst of that fierce soldiers drenched in toddy passed through the crowds and disturbing the flow of traffic, the crowds either excited or terror-stricken, as if life itself is in danger and it takes sometimes before peace is restored. After these incidents, the usual business of petty vendors resumes their commercial activities.

Thus continues the traffic all through the day incapable of any perceptible increase, or decrease, like the ocean which remains ever full whether the clouds pour in or draws off its contents.

And Evening, the declining hours of day is more interesting to watch???..
