Audio Link :
http://podbazaar.castmetrix.net/download/144115188075857194/1/TamilPodcast67VaaramOruAlayamArulmiguVilvavananatharThirukkovilThirukkollamputhur.mp3
Audio Duration : 6m 26 sec

Location&Direction:This
temple is located in Thirukollambuttur-Tanjavur District ,situated at
Nannilam circle near Koradacheri. We can also reach to this place Via
Kumbakonam-Koradacheri bus route by getting down at Sellur.

Current Name : Kollamputtur
MainDeity : Vilvavananathar
Godesses : Azhagiya Nachiyar
Theertham : Agathiyar Theertham,Brahma Theertham, Arjuna Theertham
Holytree : Villvam

When
Pandavas were in Exile, Arjuna followed the advise given by Kowmiya
Saint to worship Vilvavananathar, Arjuna was blessed with
Pashupathaasthram by Vilvananathar which he used during Mahabaratha
War.
Another famous story is, when Sage Thirugyanasambandar along
with his disciples visted this place there was cyclone and he rowed the
Boat without the Paddle by singing the verse "Kottamae Kamazhum" and by
Gods Grace reached the other side of the bank and worshiped
Vilvavananathar, hence the name "Odampokiyaaru"

Hi satish

you are right...it was more a detterrant...in that way similar to the
nukes of the cold war .... if both sides have equally destructive
weapons they kind of cancel out each others potency ( not necessarily
like the ramanand sagar version of arrow to arrow)

actually the verse depicting the nuke aspect of the astram is used for
the brama astra which aswatama invokes ( albieit with a blade of
grass)...which krishna explains the true implication of the
weapon...that not even a bulb of onion or blade of grass will grow for
eons....a nuclear holocaust....and seeks him to take back but
unfortunately aswathama had only learnt to send not take back ( guess
he was practical like me...why take back something that is sent) maybe
a moral there as well....in the end he is forced or rather krishna
conspires to get it to hit the unborn child of ajuna in the
womb...which krishan again brings back to life...

the pasupatha astra is also tangled with another puzzle of mallai, mr
swaminathan's upcoming book on mallai has a lovely explanation as below

...actually coming closer to chennai - in mallai the famous penance
panel - there is a lively debate if it portrays arjuna's penance to
receive the pasupatha astra or does it signify the penance of
bagiratha / descent of ganges.

to answer this puzzle you need to explore mallai more and come to the
other side of the govardhana panel - you will see a test panel - kind
of dress rehearsal of the penance panel - cut into low relief. here
the two stones have split ( in the penance panel they have brilliantly
used a natural cleft in the rock)....you can see the the boon seeker
actually to the right of shiva ( in the penance panel he is to the
left)...shiva has four arms but no astra( in the penance panel you can
see shiva holding a over sized arrow or spear)...in the test panel all
the other beings are moving towards the cleft...a clear indication of
what the panel was meant to indicate. the initial story board was
purely bagiratha prayathanam....

to understand more you need to see what modifications were done from
this test panel to the final panel...apart from what i gave
earlier....shiva and boon seeker are moved much closer to the cleft in
the final panel...so it was a deliberate act to integrate both the
stories into one maze.....what an amazing creation. no wonder it
continues to catch the awe of all ... the largest outdoor relief in
the world.. a tribute to the master craftsmanship of the pallavas....

but some others claim its not just two stories but three....including
the coming of the pallavas from the north.

anway the penance panel has its own share of puzzles...the praying cat
and mice, the goat legged kinnara couples with men sporting dandams....

takes us back to sivagimyin sabatham....ayana sirpi..
