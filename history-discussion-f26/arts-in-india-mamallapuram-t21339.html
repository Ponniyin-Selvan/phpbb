by sps10142004 on Mon Dec 24, 2007 6:46 pm

Dear friends,

Mails from Venkat - Sridhar are related to taking photography of
certain protected monuments - particularly the Thanjai Big temple,
paintings on the First floor of the temple, which was supposedly
held by Behl.

To clarify ::

The Peruvudayar (Massive Lingam) is positioned at the Ground level
inside the sanctum.

Above this, existed an opening, which is approachable from the
present top level - using ladder. There is a vestible, called
SAANDHAARAM. A group of persons can go through this and see the
paintings inside both these walls. These were originally paintings
by Sarafoji Kings (200 / 300 yrs back). Underneath the layer,
there were Chozha paintings (1000 yrs baqck), which were found by
one Dr. Govindasamy of Annamalai University... 60 / 70 years back.

ASI painstakingly removed the layers and exposed the Chozha
paintings. Our Group has discussed vbarious aspects of these
paintings in detail several occasions earlier and also in our Second
Yaathirai to Big temple.

Frontline - about six months back - published a very detailed
article on this.

DR. Sathiyamurthy, as ASI Suptt, (Founder REACH) has arranged
photography of these paintings and exhibited them in a Museum within
the temple complex, behind Varahi temple, on the southern side.

Sriraman (our Group member) has photographed and organised this.

Before photographing these, when we approached ASI, we were told
that Behl had the rights as he was assigned this work on behalf of
Indira Gandhi Institute, Delhi.

Dr. Sathiyamurthy got special permission from ASI Delhi and
exhibited this for viewing by all the visitors and Frontline made
this public also.

Prior to that we were hesitating even to post a photo of the
painting in our Photo section because of copy right issue.

sps

Hi sirs,

checked the site - For their outstanding sculpture in stone and
bronze casting - my person'l view - their paintings seem a BIT
amateurish

- the large sauce pan eyes,
- the squarish jaws ( their stone/bronze works dont have this
distint jawline, rather nice oval pleasing lines )
- the men are depicted as rather short and stout with thick legs.
- the facial expressions lack the serenity to be associated with
their normal works.
- the paintings are mostly uni dimensional ( can see how they draw
the legs - both facing one side - you will see this style in
cambodian sculpture) while even in pallava times stone was worked to
show different feet positions.
- the depiction of animals ( especially monkeys) with the exception
of elphants seems a BIT out of sync with chola art.

maybe they had different canons for paintings and obviously a much
more evolved one for sculpture.

Also have the panels/atleast the main theme been identified -
meaning there could atleast be some titles for the panels - they are
already very difficult to understand - maybe a image title could
help.

rgds
