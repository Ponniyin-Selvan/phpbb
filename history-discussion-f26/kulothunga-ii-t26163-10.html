Dear V. Venkatesh,

Please answer this simple question ::

In which Ancient Literature it is mentioned that the presiding
deity
is named as GOVINDARAJAR in Chitrakudam - Chidambaram.

To find more details, I just skimmed through 108 Divya Desangal by
Karaikkudi based Vishnava Siddhantha Noorpadhipu kazhagam -
Ethijan.


He quotes :

Siru + Ambalam = Chithambaram !

Whereas most of us know that " THE KOIL " is Srirangam for
Vaishnavites and CHIDAMBARAM for Saivaites...

Chith (Chitham - Determination in Mind ) which itself a Temple is
the root word.

Pls read quotes on CONCEPT OF SIVA (By Dr. Nagaswamy) posted by me
a
year back in the Archieves.

Also pls read Jagasirpiyan fiction.

The Chidambaram temple is a CONCEPT temple :

With number of bones,
number of breathes (nails ) ,
number of veins (cross beams )etc.,

Karbagraham like heart
with an inlet / outlet etc...
depicting human body ..
in which Moolasthanam (Moolattanar - Appar hymned in 7th AD) is
Chit-
Ambalam - which LINGAM is supposedly erected on the JEEVA SAMADHI
of
Thirumoolar - who is reportedly hymned 1 Stanza one year for 3000
years - which later became to be known as Thirumandhiram ! Which
is
Devine Manthra for the Tamil World.

In that Hall Adhiseshan is reportedly gave details on Mahabashyam
behind a curtain and a lonely student (with others cutting the
class) when opened the curtain got perished ..

Pls read my quote on " AJABA " in the Archieves..

Jaba .. repeatedly chanting / adopting with effort.

A+Jaba : Spontaneous :: without effort

Over the heart of Sriman Narayan Nataraja is positioned .. and HIS
MOVEMENTS - SPONTANEOUS AND EFFORTLESS SYNCRONISING WITH SRIMAN
NARAYAN'S BREATHE - IS A DANCE KNOWN AS " AJABA" . When you come
out of Thiruvarur Temple Sanctum (East facing, So southern exit),
on
the South facing wall - you will find the pictorial depiction of
this.

Our respected member Kathie has rightly put - Saivism and
Vaishanavism are intertwined in weaving the lovely Hindusim fabric.

When a Scholar of Dr. Nagaswamy's stature is referring
Ottakothar's
statement as NIRMALYAM, we have to bring this into open.

When another Scholar SR Balasubramanian observes after discussing
in
detail that Kulothunga I & St Ramanujar's rift was without
evidence
that also has to be brought out ..


If we think a Sculpture of present Govindaraja's magnitude is
identified as " Siru Deivam " by Ottakoothar then he has erred..

But if Krishnas with almost 10 leelas, Ulagalandan, sitting vishnu
+
2 Devis, standing Rama (Sri Rama was never alone without Sita +
Lakshman + Hanuman atleast) with " ARAVANAIYIL KIDANTHAN " as
could
be seen from 7th Century hymns, I leave it you to name this entity
whether could have been as bigger as of present Sculpture..

And in the entire Discussion the CURCIAL WORD CHITRA (picture) IS
NOT TAKEN INTO CONSIDERATION !

We are all talking of Sculptures !!!

I earlier concluded that Kulothunga II in his 18 years span has
contributed only to 5 temples including chidambaram + Srirangam.

Please read on Kulothunga II.

His officials behaved with (Independent) Authority..

And Poet would have been more loyal than the King in certain
issues..

vj

As Siva commences his dance in the evening. Ratnakara imagines,
in
the
loftiest terms the sun and moons as the cymbals used by the
goddess
of
prosperity herself for tala and laya, the most important in the
nritta
aspect of dance. At the commencement of dance by Siva at dusk,
with
the sun disc setting near the astagiri and the full moon
emerging
into
light from his locks, it seems to make the celestial goddess of
prosperity hold as it were these two as cymbals for the Lord's
musical
orchestra:
astavalambiravibimbitayodayadsrichudonmishatsakalachandrataya
cha sayam sandhyapranrittaharavadyagrihitakamsyataladvayeva
samalakshyata nakalakshmih (Haravijya 19.5).

This exacting musical orchestra, the vina played by the Lady of
music,
Sarasvati herself, the flute by Indra who excels, Brahma keeping
time
and Vishnu himself sounding the drum, is because Siva himself is
an
adept in all the musical instruments. In the Sivasahasranama,
there
is
an elaboration of his musical accomplishments. He is described as
Sarvaturyaninadi, he is also Vainavi, Panavi, Vini, Tali and
Nali:
vainavi panavi kalah kalakanthah katamkatah?.. vini cha panavi
tali
nail kalikatus tatha sarvaturyaninadi cha sarvavyuapyaparigrahah
(Lingamahapurana 1, 65, 11) tumbuvino mahakopah vamsavadi
hyaninditah
(Linegamahapurana 1, 65, 21) naikatanaratas svarah
(Lingamahapurana
1,
65, 40). The commentary here explains tumbu vina as the vina
provided
with two gourds and popularly known as Rudra vina. Siva delights
in
innumerable murchhanas or tanas, and he is of the very nature of
the
svaras, not only udatta, anudatta, svarita, but the sangita
svaras,
the seven famous notes. Siva is also called Rathagita: akshayo
rathagitas cha (Lingamahapurana 1, 65, 44). Ratha is
Rathantarasama,
by the chant of which he is invoked. Siva's fondness for Sama is
very
well known.

aspect of dance. At the commencement of dance by Siva at
dusk,
with
the sun disc setting near the astagiri and the full moon
emerging
into
light from his locks, it seems to make the celestial
goddess
of
prosperity hold as it were these two as cymbals for the

musical
orchestra:

astavalambiravibimbitayodayadsrichudonmishatsakalachandrataya
cha sayam sandhyapranrittaharavadyagrihitakamsyataladvayeva
samalakshyata nakalakshmih (Haravijya 19.5).

of
