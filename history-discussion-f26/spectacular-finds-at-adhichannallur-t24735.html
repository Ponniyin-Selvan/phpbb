Thanks for the news, Mr.Arun.

I have read that movements of human beings in this region -
adichanallur etc - goes back by 1,00,000 years (Refer "Tholliyal in
Tamizhakam" by Tamilnadu archaeological div).

Humans have inhabited this place for a very long time. This new
reference is stunning because it can shed light on pre-sangam age in
tamil nadu about which we have little or no clue.

It is only with such vital evidences will we be able to see if there
have been any connections between Indus region and the south.

I am sure Dr.Rajavelu would have read the scripts in the newly found
tablets by now. Let us await further news in this direction.

Spectacular finds at Adhichannallur..

by sillybuffer on Thu May 27, 2004 2:09 pm

Hi All,

I have often wondered as to why we don't really come across more finds like
this in India. You know.. more tombs etc of people from the past. The answer
I came up with was that perhaps, since Indians have traditionally cremated
their dead, there was no chance of having such finds.


Imagine if one could find the tombstone of RajaRaja Chozhan. Wouldn't THAT
be amazing!


Oh well! Anyway, this IS wonderful news for all those Romila Thapar clones
who somehow refuse to see the antiquity of the south. Hopefully, this find
and the one in Kutch (of the submerged Dwaraka --- They even found an ID
'card" that citizens used to carry and which has been mentioned in either
the Puranas or the Mahabharatha) will lead to a reinterpretation of Indian
history.

Cheers,

Arun

keep the posts coming!
