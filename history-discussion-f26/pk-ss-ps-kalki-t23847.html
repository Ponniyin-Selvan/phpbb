Hi Satish & Krupa,

Krupa - you should join Satish office, and he yours, so that both can
enjoy your new envorns.. !

I read all PK & SS about 10 yrs back.. once / twice., PS more &
recently.

PK is related to the dream of a Chozha prince getting back his mother
land under the guidance of Scholar Pallava Ruler ..

Mahendra was a Scholar in Sanskrit, wrote poems (On Cauvery -
inscribed in Trichy Rockfort), dramas (Kamal is writing in Series in
http://www.varalaaru.com - inspired rock temples (which popular thought is
recently destroyed by Dr.Kalaikkovan's Athyanthakaamam) and fought
wars. Unfortunately he lost to Pulikesin.

SS was related to avenging of that defeat - by Mahendra's son
Narasimhar - who did that to save the pride of his Nation and NOT to
honour the Sabadham of (an imaginary Dancer / Lover ) Sivakami !

All said and done, Kalki could not find scope for Navarasams in both
these .. He chose to write on the success Story of Chozhas .. He
should have had too much of materials collected by then to pour ..

He chose the best theme Chozha History has provided - Aditha
Karikalan's murder !

He touched the Chozha History where he left in PK .. He did not mix
romance much since he handled it effectively in SS .. He has mixed
everything in perfect ratio ... and served us the grand feast - in PS.

Re Aall Maarattam - DISGUISING - was a practice adopted by the Rulers
in those days .. Can see in Rajaraja Chozhan movie also.. Read Porkai
Pandiyan Story .. To protect the subject's interest the Just Pandya
King - in disguise - knocked a door in the night and when found he
made a mistake, he repeated knocking on other doors and severed his
hand in the Court and was rewarded a golden arm ..

Re Madurantakan matter, it is different .. When Historian - Sastry -
suspected Uthama as a co-conspirator - but RR's inscriptions
highlighted him as "Sembiyanmadevi thiruvaryiruditha Uthama
Chozhan .. " Kalki tried to accommodate both the versions ...

Kabaligam - Laguleesam - Siva / Vishnu exchanges were much discussed
in those days and so Kalki tried to highlight the pictures through
his vivid descriptions ...

Are you not able to visualise an "Annapakshi" padagu (boat) when you
see the (dried) water ways near Kudanthai / Thanjavur ?

Don't you hear the stone-chiselling sound when visiting
Mamallapuram ? (Let it be Rajasimhan in History !)

Are you not looking for a Mahishasura Mardhini - or one handed
Neelakesi or a Kabalika when visiting the hillocks around Kanchi ?!

We are discussing this even after more than 50 yrs ..

fondly, sps

Dear SPS ,

A good comprehensive mail. But I think Krupa wont be
convinced. He wrote three mails about SS and its
romance. Here are my views.

Unlike PS, SS is a streight forward story - son
taking revenge for father's defeat. Kalki added
masaalaa to these historical events mainly by this
love affair. A simple equation

History + Romance = SS.

But here Kalki shows his own class by limiting the
romance element well within the limits.(At least
within my limits!). Saandilyan romance elutha innikki
aarambichchaa naalannaikkuthaan mudippaar. It is very
easy for me read his works simply skipping all his
'varunanais' - be it on romance or otherwise. But with
Kalki I want to dwell along his varunanais. Even I
have read some chapters in PS repeatedly for two three
times simply for varunanais.

Generally we men dont like egoistic ladies. In SS
Sivakaami's words and deeds hurt Maamallaa many a
times. So we are biased and poor Sivakaami though she
got all the sympathies of her creator Kalki, she
miserably fails to earn the sympathies of the readers.

In PS many kinds of loves and romances are descibed.
Everything is different and at the same time
absolutely enjoyable. If SS is not compared with PS
then it is a very good historic novel. Here Kalki
himself could not resist describing the discomfort
felt by Maamalla by Sivakaami's behaviour.

Mahendraavaa Sivaajiyai ninaichchukallam.
Maamallaavaa Kamlai ninaichchukallam. Sivakaamiyaa
yaarai ninaikkarathu? allaaraiyum namakku romba
pudikkume?

In Padayappaa Nilaambari is a villi. But Sivakaami is
a heroin having some elements of Nilaambari. So her
love is not much appealing. Kalki chose the name
Sivakaami bcas she is an expert dancer. But the name
itself neithr different nor historic like KUNDAVAI.

The most likable charector in SS is Paranjothi. Kalki
started the SS story with a heroic deed of Paranjothi
but this charector was not much elaborated. Being a
real historic charector his romance and other
activities if elaborated would have been a real treat.

I appreciate Krupa - he wants good men to love only
good girls - a good heart !

I have forgotten the whole of PK. I have to read it
again.

yours
Natarajan .

by satish_arun on Tue May 24, 2005 1:53 pm

Krupa,

I completed SS yesterday night(1 mani varaikkum vidama padichi
mudichiten). I too agree with Arun. I liked SS very much for its story
line. But there are few things which I cant accept. One, I go with
krupa,the romantic descriptions are at times quite eloborative and a
couple of times boring too. Probably , I like SS because i read it
recently. I started PS for the second time and left with the last
bagam. But SS being a new story to me, took precedence. I have continue
PS from where I left.

The other thing I can digest is, If Mahendra was defeated by Pulikesi,
why Kalki has projected that Pulikesi could not defeat Mahendra and
laid a false Vijayastambam in vathapi. History is history and we should
not change it. This is what i didnt like in SS as compared to PS or
PK.If in PS, he handled the situation by makin Senthan amudan Uttama,
thats ok to some extent. But changing history is not acceptable,
atleast to me. Intha edathula Kalki koncham sarikitarnu than naan
solluven. Also, when narasimha's vicotory over vathapi is history,
kalki projects as if only because of Bikshu, Pallavas could win. Again
twisting history, which I didnt like. Bikshu fighting as Pulikesi in
Manimangalam and vathapi are too much.
Otherwise SS is a great story about the war.

Regards,
Satish
