1. Publishing a maganine AIRAVATHI in Association with Dr.

Trichy;
2. Review of Ananda Vikatan Diwali Malar (whether it was RRC's
Pallippadai at Udayarkudi - No - discussed in our Group as well
earlier)
3. Piran Malai temple artcle by Dr. Nalini
4. Editorial and 2 articles related to Artist SILPI !

Very much Interesting to read.

Dr. Kalaikkovan has written his experience as to how came into
History Research apart from being an Eye specialist.
The highpoints he projected are very impressive, interesting and
will definitely form guidelines for those interested in knowing
more about History of our land.

Commented : " Wonderful curve and prominent high points ! From: a
single temple (Uraiyur) rare sculpture - Sthalapuranam -
Periyapurana Aaraychi - KAN Sastri - Pandarathar - SRB's The Chozha
Temples - South Indian Inscription Volumes ... to Dr. Rasamani-
ckanaar Hisoty Research Centre Trichy - Scholarly Status - Associate
Scholars (Dr. Nalini - Dr. Akila - Dr. Lalithambal) - Able
Disciples (varalaaru Team)on the path to Scholarship .. VARALAARU !"

Re Rajarajan Pallippadai, the idea figured as early from in 1927 -
28 ARE Volumes - in British Rule itself - and yet the Authorities
have not anything in this direction.

A detailed excavation at Pazhayarai & GKC is a must. It is the
duty of all the History - lovers to bring this to the attention of
Archeology Depatt of Tamilnadu State atleast.
