Is Mecca really Makhesvara?

by mail2venky on Fri Oct 01, 2004 9:13 am

Hi all,

I was reading a book A brief history of India by Alain Danielou.
He is supposed to be one of the many authorities of Hindu religion.

In the book he has stated that present world Mecca was (is!)
a Hindu site by name Makhesvara with Shiva as the deity.
(No hurt intended for Muslims in our group)
He quotes sources of this place from ancient Greek philosphers and historians.

The book is interesting, as he also compares Shiva with a greek God
by name Dionysus, the similarities are abundant.
He also delves into 3 forms of Saivism, one of which is the Kapalika sect
described by Kalki in PS.

http://www.zhakanini.org/English/democd.php

( may be except China ). And these nations are trading giants. Can't we become one.

"Enna Valam illai intha thirunaatil"

According to me, unless we comeup and start showing the world, what Tamil is, we cannot win.

And "WE WIN WIN"..).. I am optimistic.
