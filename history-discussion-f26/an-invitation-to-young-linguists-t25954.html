An Invitation to Young Linguists

I am drafting this on the suggestion and request of V. Raghavan who is as interested as I am in the recovery of the original roots of C. Tamil and Sanskrit languages which has an importance greater than simply studying linguistics and developing new branches. There have been many DISTORTIONS in the development of Indian Thinking and which have led to many undesirable social and political consequences which cannot be solved unless the TRUTHS about the roots of Indian civilization are RECOVERED.

So let me propose the following as a way of interesting a band of young linguists who may want to take up such studies and give substance to the process of recovery and retrieval of the original essence of the Indian Civilization.

1.

I have shown that Sumerian is Archaic Tamil and that it has served not only the embryonic form from which C. Tamil has evolved but also the base from which Sanskrit has also evolved. As far as the primary sources go, there is an abundance of them and what is lacking are scholars who would master these THREE primary sources and establish more firmly and on valid scientific grounds the connections and continuity that have been shown already and revising some of them if necessary. Now such studies involve a number of investigations as listed below.

2.

The first is that of phonological studies where it appears to be the case that both C.Tamil and Sk took off from the base phonology of SumeroTamil but going about in slightly different ways contributing the differences that obviously exist between the two languages. It appears to me that here we cannot take it for granted that the phonology of Tol where the Vadaezuttu, the sibilants like ?j? and the voiced plosives like ?g? are to be deleted from Tamil words is in fact the original phonology. It appears that these phonemes were present in Archaic Tamil and while Sk built upon it with developing secondary vowels as V Raghavan has noted, in Tamil there has been a reduction of the phonemic values of such sounds perhaps prompted by developing a script that is simple and efficient for writing. The script development has been an integral part of Tamil tradition and the phonological studies went along with it.

3.

Then we have the need to investigate the morphologies of the NP and VP where along with similarities there are differences. Both C.Tamil and Sk resemble the SumeruTamil (ST) in having the case markers suffixed to the noun stem though there is a reversal in the order of the attributive term. While in SumeroTamil, such terms FOLLOW the main noun, in C.Tamil it precedes. The situation with SK seems to confusing and hence a matter for further investigation.

4.

The situation is even more complex with respect to the morphology of VB. The ST has both prefixes and suffixes and which is RETAINED by SK but C.Tamil has only suffixes but indicating the presence of prefixes in the deep past by some frozen forms like mun-eeRu where the ?mun? is one of the prefixes in ST wide in use. A wider study of Indian Prakrit languages and modern derivatives may also help here.

5

Another area of fruitful investigations is that of Compound Words with abound in all the three languages and which are related to the fact all these languages are agglutinative. While Tamil and Dravidian languages are commonly accepted as agglutinative, this is denied in relation to Sk but more for the reason of showing that it belongs to the IndoAryan family of languages which are inflectional. This goes against PaNini and many other Indian linguists who have studied the grammar of Sk. There are case markers as well as various kinds of Compound Words that show that Sk is just as agglutinative as the Dravidian languages like ST C.Tamil and so forth. Since this still remains a matter of controversy, the young scholars can take up such studies without any prejudices. They can also take up the study pf Indian languages particularly the tribal languages all over India with such objectives in mind.

6.

Now of course the most important field of study remains Etymological Studies where in the fashion of Paavanai we LOCATE the ROOTS of the main words both in the phonemic morphology and semantics and RETRACE the various phonological and semantic changes and come to the current forms. Our believe is that Sk language does NOT have these roots and that in contrast to that C.Tamil contains them and because of which it can be said Sk has Tamil as its basis. This is a new field of Uri-Linguistics where we take up the suggestion of Tol that there are words so transformed that we have to peel off the surface layers in order to recover the original phonetic shape and meaning. It is here that the ST texts become enormously important as sources of such etymons. Perhaps we can extend such studies to include also the Nubian Meroitc and such other ancient languages of Africa. We can include also the Japanese Korean Turkish and such other languages related to the Dravidian.

7.

For such studies the forum Tolkaappiyar can be used and hence those interested in such studies are cordially welcome to subscribe to it and join the band of scholars already engaged in such and related studies.
