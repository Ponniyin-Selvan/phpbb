What The Gita Says About Free Will And Destiny
'Paramarthi Raina'

We take for granted that we have the free will to make conscious
decisions to choose between different possible courses of action.
However, recently conducted experiments in the Max Planck Institute in
Germany on the functioning of the human brain, have put that presumption
into doubt. The researchers have concluded that free will could, in
fact, be "little more than an illusion".

The experiments indicated that our subconscious mind decides on and
dictates our actions, long before our conscious mind actually realises
it.

Food for thought!!

--- Inponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, "atpu555" atpu555@ wrote:

Does this mean that we don't have any 'free will' at all?

I thought that when we say 'All Thy Will' as all the
Thevaaram/Thiruvaasagam/Paasurams are saying, it is an attitude of
mind to
have, in order to surrender to Him and annhililate our Ego.

Yes, according to this philosophy, there is absolutely no free will
on part of the Athma. This, is a point of debate between various
sects of Hinduism and even within Srivaishnavism itself. Some say
that the Atma is indeed given a free will first, and based on its
initial action, the good or bad karma starts accumulating for the
Atma. This case is presented, so that one shall not say that the God
is biased when He supposedly blesses some while troubling (I wouldn't
call it a punishment) others for no mistake of the Atma.

And some others say that the Athma is absolutely independant and
hence the karma accrues to it according to its deed.

A Logical Rational Analysis:-

The people who ascribe a "Free Will" to Atma just to ensure that no
one says anything wrong on the part of the God, forget the fact that
when there is absolutely no one equal to or greater than Him, no
blame accumulate on His part. When He is THE Supreme Being and the
owner of everything, just about everything, then can't He act the way
He wishes? Why should He be bound by what the mere humans, created by
Him thinks?

On the other hand, the supporters of "No Free Will" just resign to
the fact that He is the Sarva Swami (Swami-means the absolute owner
of everything) and that we are all willing slaves. No matter whatever
we think about Him, He is what He is. Just think about this. No
matter what a person thinks about it, a Mountain is indeed a
Mountain. Just take case of Brahmaputra river. While we in India call
it as Brahmaputra, the Chinese at it origin call it Yang Tse. Does it
changes the character of the river? Similarly, the God remains unique
in all aspects and He just acts the way He wishes, no matter what His
creatures think of Him. Thinking that He is biased is the limitation
of the Jeevatma. He is definitely not bound by what the Jeeva thinks.
The moment one says that He is bounded by what the Jeevatma thinks of
Him, then immediately the Supremity of the God is put to question as
how can a person, who can be questioned for His acts, be called a
Supreme Being or Brahman.

And I would never think that the Azhwars and Nayanmars were doing Lip
Service when they said that "It is Him who acts and not them the
mortals". They had absolute faith on this aspect of God and that is
what is the essence of their wonderful pourings.

Finally as I said in the caveat above, let me give you the supporting
verses which confirm that there is no "one correct" method to reach
the God.

Nammazhwar says in Thiruviruttham,

vaNangum thuRigaL pala pala Akki, madhi vikaRppAl
piNangum samayam pala pala Akki, avai avai thORu
aNangum pala pala Akki, nin mUrthy parappi vaithhAi
iNangu innOrai illai, nin KAN vEtkai yezhuvippanE.

The essence of the above pAsuram is that:- He has created so many
religions and hence so many ways for the Jeevatma to attain Him and
then He is present as the Supreme God in every Religion of the world
and such God is created a non-ending thirst on part of the Azhwar to
reach His lotus feet as soon as possible.

This is the proof that every religion, sect, subsect et all are just
different routes to attain God.

And finally, God Himself ensures that all His creations reach Him
back when He wishes. Nammazhwar, again, this time in Thiruvaimozhi,
says, "avar avar vidhi vazhi adaya ninRanarE" in the first decad and
in the penultimate decad says " vaikuntham puguvadhu maNNavar
vidihyE". This two gives a better understanding of what is Destiny
or "vidhi". That, everyone born in this world are destined to reach
Him and that Destiny is He Himself and His wishes.

The fact that everyone reaches Him finally is also corroborated by
Thirumangai Azhwar. I have quoted this just two days back

"manjADu varai yEzhum, vAnagamum maNNagamum, maRRum ellAm
enjAmal vayiRRadakki Alin mEl Or iLam thaLiril KAN vaLarndha eesan"

So, forgetting the fact that one sect is ascribing to this
philosophy, when one does a logical analysis, then I am sure they
will come to this conclusion.

In fact this is exactly why I wanted our members to come out with an
answer of what they perceive their Ideal God to possess as.

I dont know if I was able to satisfy your query. Please let me know
if you need more clarifications on this perspective.

Regards,

Satish

Simple - straight forward analysis.

The I does not go unless we understand the core of what we are. Only
when we realize that we do not control anything, right from the air
we breathe to the action that happens, we must resign ourselves to
the fact that the I has never been there in the first place and we
are holding on to something that is never been there, hence we have
been cast out from our real space with the eternity.

This 'I' here is totally not related or combined with the sentence 'I
am ravi or I am satish' - that I is physical, very much there.

The I here is above the physical concept, it is attached to the mind
and action or thought....

Romba kozappiteno

-- R

--- Inponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, "SatishKumar Arunachalam"
wrote:
I am no expert like VV, Ravi et al.

Just trying to put forth what I understand.

The first step of bondage of the self is to think that we are not
free. The atman is free and till we recognize it we are caught in
the
samsara sagaram. This is because we attribute every action to 'I',
thinking that I am doing this, I am doing that. This 'I' is the
cause
of Karma. Once we relinquish this 'I' and do our duty, then we are
not
bound by karma. Very difficult to realize and attain this and trying
to do things without expecting results is the first step of
karmayoga.

Easiest way is bakthi yoga, where we surrender to the God saying
everything is yours, whatever I do is because of you. When we come
to
this state,as VV referred as 'willing slaves', then we dont take
responsibility of our actions, and slowly the 'I' in us will be
dissolved and we can be free. Till then, yes, there is no free will
but the struggle of life is only to be free.

There is nothing called wrong or right in this world. There is only
'sathya', and if we say someone is doing a mistake, it means that
they
are moving from a lower truth to higher truth to attain the ultimate
truth. So whatever we feel/do, let it be free will or slavery,
nothing
is wrong as long as we try to move forward towards the ultimate
sathya.

Regards,
Satish
