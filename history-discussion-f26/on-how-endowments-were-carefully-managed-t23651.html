On how endowments were carefully managed

by seshadrigokul on Thu Jul 29, 2004 2:27 pm

Hi all,

Read an inscription found by Dr.Rajamanickanar institute of Historic
research in Pullamangai Thiru Alanthuraiyar temple (Near Kumbakonam).

It belongs to 24th regional year of Rajaraja.

The king had made few of the lands tax free (Iraiyili) since they
were belonging to odhuvars (Nindrothum sattap peru makkal) in his
12th regional year. In 24th year he found that there was no
thiruppatiyam chanting happening - there were no odhuvars available.
Probably the inspecting officer should have observed this and
silently made an observatory remark - so the king issued a royal
order to take away the iraiyili and make it taxable land.

This inscription throws a rare glipmse of how endowments provided to
temples were not only audited but were even taken back when the
purpose was not served.

Given their rudimentary infrastructure, it is amazing to see the
quality of governance.

Anbudan

DEAR GOKUL,

Nice information.

There is a Vallam village near Thanjavur - which I mistook as the
Village from where Vandhiyathevan hailed - when I visited the place
about 10 - 15 yrs back. This was MUTHARAYARs town as mentioned in
earlier mails. Two years back I came to know that it is THIRUVALLAM -
located near Ranipet / BHEL Township - from where Vandhiyathevan
hailed. Kundavi & Vthvn ruled together from this place after their
wedding. MELPADI (Western Command) is hardly 10 Kms away -where in
his 30th Rgnl Yr - RR constructed Arinjayeswaram (Pallippadai) in
memory of his grand father Arinjayan - probably the LAST of the
temple he constructed in his 30 yrs span of Rule. Most of RR's
inscriptions in Big Temple denote his 29th Rgnl Yr.

In the Thiruvallam temple, inscriptions mention of one MADURANTAKA
KANDRADITHAN (who is supposed to be the son of Uthama Chozha.)His
inscriptions are not found after 997 AD (according to Sethuraman). He
was incharge of auditing the temple accounts in RR's Rule and had the
authority to punish those who mismanaged the temple funds / affairs.

At the entrance of the sanctum, statue of a person is seen in the
sitting posture with both his hands NAILED to the floor!

Inscriptions mention that he was punished by MADURANTAKA KANDRADITHAN
on behalf of the Emperor - for having misused temple funds!

There are similar instances in Suseendram, Nagerkoil etc.

Another thing : Dr. Jaybee has mentioned in the Agathiyar Group that
in the Siva Temple of (Ponnamaravathi Thirupathur) T. Vairavanpatti
(not the one near Pillayarpatti), inscriptions of Rajaraja in his 3rd
Regnl Year are found in VATTEZHUTU - which was normally found in
Pandyas' days only! Spoke to Dr. Kkvn last night.. He was also
thrilled and needed more information ..
