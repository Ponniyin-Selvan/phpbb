brahmin. A boy becomes a bramhin (bramachari) only

Hi chitra

Am threading on a very thin line - but since lots of you have already
posted on this, am just putting a few pointers - just for better
understanding - for i sincerely believe, all are born equal and its
the deeds we do that mark greatness during our short stay.

antiquity in verse

The mantra recited at the time of Yajnopavita is given in
Brahmopanishad and it goes like this:

'Yagnopavitam paramam pavitram prajapateryatsahajam purastat'
(Yajnopavita is high and sacred; it was born with Prajapati, of old)

anitiquity in practise

some pointers to this is that a piece of deer skin is traditionally
bound into thread. does this vestige - point to that it was more a
way
of wearing the upper garment during a religious ceremony? we can
argue, but this could point to the fact that this could be a practise
from pre cloth days!!

Who was worshipped

It must be pointed out again that the great mantra popularly known as
Gayatri, after the metre in which it is composed is really called
Savitri since it is dedicated to Savita, the sun-god. This mantra is
found in the Rigveda. So in essence pointer again to worshipping
nature - sun god


Who wore it

The sacrificial string of a Brahmana shall be made of cotton, (shall
be) twisted to the right and consist of three threads; that of a
Kshatriya of hempen threads; and that of a Vaisya of woollen threads.
again ref from the Rig veda

now,we can again see references in literate to this - maybe someone
can view references to ramayana - and post.

fortunately or unfortunately there is never a mention here of man or
women.

traditionally the wards father would take him to the appropriate guru
and then this ceremony would happen. So to prove that this was not a
excl boy's act, we need to see ref of women being sent to school aka
gurukuls in literature. ( Art seems to already point this, as seen
from the sculpture)

what is it made off:

earlier mentions seem to just point it to be an upper garment that
was
worn in the particular way during performance of certain rituals.
depiction is sculpture/art - also follows this argument - as its
shown
to be much more thicker than just the present day thread.

The components as of today

The yajnopavita consists of three cords and each cord is formed by
twisting three threads into one. Actually it is made up of one single
cord only. At the one end of the cord of required length a loop will
be formed through which the other end of the cord is pulled three
times to form three lines of equal length and at the same time the
cord is also passed round the loop to ultimately form a knot at the
centre. This knot is known as Brahma-granthi, Brahma-knot, indeed a
very significant name
for it represents the formless Brahman, the Supreme Principle

In days of yore, a Brahmana meditating upon the Gayatri holding the
Brahma knot, had the power to bless and curse others.

The different stages

There were strict control on the dates of graduation from one stage
to
other - like 5-8 seems the limit for the first stage. dont want to
confuse more with the actual names, but this is when he proceeds from
being a baby into a student - invested with his first responsibilites
- he is given one string ( made of three loops of the thread) ....

as he passes into his next stage - gets married he gets another set -
which is interpreted as taking on the addnl responsibilites of his
partner - wife. this can be again concluded as - at times the thread
was worn by both man & women - but inorder to free up the women for
the other work, the burden was passed on to the man.

Again - not fool proof, could be a later idai sorugal, but we could
rake up literature...

the final stage is the transition from 2 -3, unfortunate happening of
you becoming the head of the family...loosing your...so you take on
his burden as well.

what does it signify?

The yajnopavita hanging from the left shoulder and passing under the
right arm, constantly reminds a man that he is a bonded worker for an
indefinite period and that he can free himself of this bondage made
up
of three gunas by discharging his duties to his ancestors, to the
Rishis, and ultimately to the Gods.

Again there are different versions of this - incl the every present
diff in thickness of thread between the two major sects...which is a
discussion within itself.
