women in ps

by meena_peru on Thu Sep 08, 2005 9:10 am

We should take into account the important fact that character & behaviour of every women in PS is greatly influenced by the way they were brought up/comforts/family background/exposture.Family plays a very important role as females are more emotionally attached to everything.No women in PS is potrayed as blunt rude or villi.

Accept the fact or not male races are greatly influenced by women in general knowingly or unknowingly.

Kundavai- She had maids to attend her personal chores, all the comforts and exposture and the freedom of voice and offcource her drive to learn new things. She involved herself too much in 'porantha veedu' (like most of the females do after they get married) the only difference is she was ahead that she wanted to marry a person who gives her a hand in doing that(mind u she doesn't choose a dumb person like kandamaran/parthiban who would be even more obliging than vandiyatevan) . she believed in Horoscope of her brother 100% that he will go places. Take into fact that chola desam at the stage was badly in need of a shape and good team co-ordination she goes one step ahead leading the good set of males she knew(vandiyatevan/rajaraja and in future rajendra). She doesn't influence her brother since she although dislikes sendan becoming a king accepts her brothers decision. Priority for her here is chola desam.

The rest of the females I think have a BIT of sitha bramhamai as kalki says which again influenced by various other factors.

Vanathi- She grew up in diffenent places. She has seen death of her father. She takes kundavai as her true friend. We can say she might have manipulated to some extent but her actions was rather ameture taken into fact that kundavai is very smart lady.

Nandini- She is totally confusing. She can be said to enjoy the fact that she could make all the males a puppet.Her way of punishing males who were either arogant or proud. Other than that she doesn't benefit from her beauty nor manipulation. She has to either marry a oldie or slog in forest living in disguise .

Poongulali- again she is slightly off..taken into fact that she has to live all by herself protecting & defending...She is more practical in understanding that she cannot live in luxury ( a koondu killi)..she is more a nature lover than any one else..She could have influenced rajaraja but think she changes her mind after being deeply hurted by people who claims she trys to captivate a prince..

Regards,
Meena.

by sillybuffer on Thu Sep 08, 2005 11:02 am

You know .. I am most fascinated by Kundavai (the historical character.. not
the PS character). Think about it this way.. Tamizh Nadu, even today is
fairly male dominated and male chauvinistic. Let us assume for an instance
that things haven't changed all that much in over a 1000 years (of course
people might well claim that a 1000 years ago TN was less male chauvinistic
than it is today.. although I wouldn't want to bet on it). In which case the
fact that a woman was so heavily involved in her "porantha veedu" is
completely amazing.. Given societal norms, would Vandiyathevan have been a
laughing stock of the makkaL in general? Kinda like "atho paaru da..
pondatti avanai vittututu inga irukkan".. "avanukku pondattiya vechchu
kaappaaththa thuppu illai" etc

Just something to ponder about!

Cheers,

AK
