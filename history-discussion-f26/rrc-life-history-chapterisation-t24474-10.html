Dear Siva,

Inscriptions, Copper plates are known as Primary source.

Books or periodicals written based on these are known as
Secondary
sources.

Ancient Literatures (say Kalingathuparan, Moovarula ) cannot be
considered as Second Source. But they do contain inflated
informations. Hence they cannot be depended as being Primary
sources also. THIS IS THE REASON FOR ANANDAVIKATAN MADHAN's
REMARKS.

But there have been enormous research and Publications through
Private Bodies - which have NOT REACHED even the attention of a
Popular Media personality like Mr. Madhan.

We should identify this area and draw the attention of the
interested intellecutals.

Primary Sources are as of now are:
1. South Indian Inscriptions Volumes published by ASI.
2. Kalvettukkal Volumes Published by Tamilnadu (State)
Archeology;
3. Annual Report on Indian Epigraphy (ASI)
4. Epigraphia Indica, ASI

Apart from these, (i) State Archeology is publishing Kalvettu -
monthly issues also. (ii) Tamilnadu Tholliyam Kazhagam,
eadquatered
at Tamil University Thanjavur, under leader guidance ofDr.
Subbarayalu, Dyon among contemporary Historians (His agronanian
research works - KAVERI - in coordination with Dr. Kuroshima-
is
well recognised)is publishing AAVANAM (Document). (iii) Dr.
Kalaikkovan & Dr. Nalini are publishing from RMHRC, Trichy,
Varalaaru, issues related to the inscriptions identified,
copied
and analysed by them. (iv) TN Government HRCE
Road "Thirukkoil" -
monthly publication does containd details on Temple
inscriptions.
