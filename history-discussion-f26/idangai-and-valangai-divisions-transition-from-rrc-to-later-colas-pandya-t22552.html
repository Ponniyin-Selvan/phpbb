Nobody knows how this division came into existance. No solid
theories. But it represents a drive on the part of some groups to
stand united. The madura manual lists many many interesting
practices on specific castes of Idangai / valangai. Most of these
customs are not prevelant to my limited knowledge. One of these is
that in a family, the wife belongs to Idangai whereas husband
belongs to valangai. Meaning the females and males of this
particular caste belong to two different divisions.

DR.SUBBARAYALU has taken up the studies and comprised them in 4
segments in his Book Kaveri, (Pannpattu Veliyittagam, - Student Edn
450/- Library Edn Rs. 1000/- email:subraja@....) as
follows:
800 - 985 ( Pallavas included - Early Chozhas Upto RRC)
986 - 1070 (RRC / RJC / sons)
1071 - 1178 - Kulothunga & co
1179 - 1350 - Fall of Chozhas - Later Pandyas.

The terms Valangai and Idangai figure in inscriptions from the 11th
Century onwards and figuring in the British records of the 18th /
19th Century. It is related to the Study of Social change. Study
made by one Mr Stein that these are caste related is rejected on
more than one Ground.

First Valangai figures in RRC period related to Military. In the
11th, the Valangai - Idangai Magamai (Tax. There is no evidence
that these are related to Castes till the 12th Century. Study on the
introduction of VILLI (Bowmen) and then formation Regiments, as
members of the ever VICTORIOUS MILITARY establishment of the
Imperial Chozhas, the Military people were introduced to Palins
culture and to the opulence of the Chozha Government. The enormous
wealth obtained through plunder and tribute would have naturally
been shared by all the fighting people.

IT HAS BEEN ARGUED BY KARASHIMA that an an important factor for the
emergence of Private Landholding was this NEW WEALTH imported in to
the Country. A corollary is incorporation of tribalfolk into settled
communities !

1318 AD Pandya inscription in Adudurai, referring 1122 Chozha
inscription "PANNAATTAR" Contribution of One Panam per Bow - by
Land Holders of Matrial Arts Artisans!

Interestingly, this record addresses PALLI - Martial Arts Bowmen -
who are settled as peasants in the VAST TERRITORY of about 10000
sq.Km (100 Km x 100 Km)bounded on the EAST by VIRANARAYAN LAKE (!)
west by Pachhai malai, NORTH by Pennaiyar and SOUTH By Cauvery
River ! - COMRPISED in present Trichy - Perambalur - Vallalar -
Thanjai - Nagai Districts!

What started as regimental designation in RRC period turned to caste-
based designation over a lapse of about 300 yrs!

Dear Gokul, In simple understandable words, we are used to
Elephant Regiment - Horse Regiment - Radham (Chariot) - Infantry -
Divisions in Indian / South Indian / Chozha Army.

Infantry & Chariot should have had BOW / ARROW, SPEARs - SWORDs
Batallions.

Those Warriors capable of using them were either Right Handed or
Left handed, but the word RIGHT HAND surfaced in RRC's period. In
the 11th Century, there was Tax collected @ ONE PANAM per Bow from
both RIGHT HAND and LEFT HAND USERS! (Magamai - tax).

Within next two centuries, by mid 1250 - such warriors - who became
wealthy and need not go to battle field - could buy FERTILE lands
on the banks of the Cauvery - Veeranarayanapuram (Anaikkari to
Vadalur) - East of GST - Perambalur - Trichy Trunk Road! - in about
10000 sqkm and got settled as Peasants and were started identified
as a Caste - Villi - to begin with!

Study of Veeranarayanachaturvedi mangalam reveals that earlier this
was Vellala Settlement and became Vedic settlement during Pallavas -
patronage continued under Chozhas Parantaka I (H/O Veeranarayanai)
& more during Uthama and after Aditha Karikalan's murder, certain
large piece of lands were Auctioned and proceeds deposited in temple
for feeding Siva devotees by the end of 10th Century and by mid 13th
Century the Settlement was takenover by "Villis"! - as aforesaid!

Like wise, with inscriptional evidence, study of NATTAR, NAGARATHAR,
MUDALI etc.,are also undertaken in this Book.

A very detailed study Indeed.
