Great answers, MrVenketesh. Im also of the opinion that Nandhini was added
as a sex siren. We all know about the similarities between Alexander Dumas
and Kalki. Kalki's Nandhini has a lot of similarities with Dumas' Milady De
Winter (pardon me if i got the spelling wrong) of Three Musketeers.. Even
the description of Nandhini by Kalki is similar to Dumas's description of
Milady. Milady was also described by Dumas as an incredible beauty on whom
anybody who set his eyes would fall in love (D'Artagnan in the ship to
England). There is a similar situation with our hero Vandhiyethevan becoming
speechless on seeing Nandhini.
Nandhini did not have a Fleur De Lis but the event of Karikalan (lets not
call him Aditya, Karikalan is more heroic) killing the pandya in the
presence of Nandhini is probably Kalki's version of Fleur De Lis.

Nandhini was probably more glamorised to increase the readership circle.
Probably the concept of "poison inside a beauty" was exciting to the youth
of those days (it is still exciting now

Wondeful thread, hope we get more in this subject.

BTW before somebody crucifies me for talking about Dumas, let me make it
clear that I strongly believe Kalki was way superior to Dumas in
characterisation.

Regards
Rahul

Mr.Venketesh;

There are lots of similarities between Dumas and Kalki. I think this is a
well known fact amongst the knowledgeable member of this group. Even the
first scene where our hero Vandhiyathevan rides along the river Ponni is
similar to D'Artagnan riding his funny looking horse in the first scene of 3
musketeers.

Also the scene where Vandhiyathevan takes on Arulmozhi without knowing who
he is, is similar to D'Artagnan taking on each of the 3 Musketeers without
knowing who they are.

These are small instances but i think the major portion of Nandhini's
character is too close to Milady De Winter. Remember everybody who read 3
Musketters at one stage or the other fell in love with Milady, even when she
is about to be executed Athos still loves her. Im sure every body (men folk
only ) who read PS must have fascinated by Nandhini in a similar fashion.
Especially the way Kalki describes her during the night, its awesome!!

Before comparing the 2 great authors letrs understand that Dumas was never
under any pressure while writing 3 Musketeers. Kalki had to deliver every
week! Infact it is these errors who make the novel and man more fascinating.


Who wants to discuss about some 3 drunken idiots from France fighting a
priest wearing a red robe or about a stupid French queen loosing her jewels
in the Buckingham palace? PS is thousand times more complicated and
fascinating.

Rahul
