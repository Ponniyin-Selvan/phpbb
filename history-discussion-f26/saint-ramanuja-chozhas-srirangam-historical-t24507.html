DEAR FRIENDS,

THIS MAIL WILL GO INTO HISTORY IF WHAT IS PUBLISHED BY ASI - VOLUME
XXIV - SRIRANGAM INSCRIPTIONS IS TRUE.

We all have been reading that :

1. Rajaraja I ascended the throne in 985
2. He ruled for about 30 yrs and all his grants are recorded till
Year 29th
3. From: Thiruvalanchuzhi records it is seen that Rajendra co-ruled in
Rajaraja 29th year and that is how we assume RJC ascnedned throne in 1012;
4. In this year Rajendra "Thilaparvatham pukkarulinar" - which is
interpreted as (a) RJC pefroemd first yr Death Anniversary of RRC -
Dr. Sethuraman / Kudanthai (b) Dr, Kalaikovan refuting (c) Dr.
Kudavoil and Dr. Vasanthakalyani (State Archlogy) offering other
explanations to " Thilaparvatham Pukkaruli" ..
5. General speculation that RRC did not live beyond 1014!

QUOTING FROM ASI VOL XXIV :

INSCRIPTION NO: 19 - AR 370 OF 1953 - 54 - FOUND ON THE HEXOGONAL
COLUMNS OF A PILLAR LYING IN FRONT OF ANCIENT PADDY STORING ROOM IN
THE KOTTARAM

" slightly damaged in the beginning. Contains the prastati commencing
with Thirumagal-pola etc., Registers a gift oa hundread cows for the
daily supply of four Naazhi of milk measured by the (liquid- measure
called) SENANADAI for the PAALAMIDHU service to the God MADURANTAKAN
SUNDARA CHOZHAN alias RAJARAJARA ILANGOVELAR in the
32 (THIRY - SECOND ) (! ! ! ... ?) regnal year of RAJRAJA I.

"
(Text - Part)..... pugazh thara eezha mandalamum ... sezhiyarai ...
koviraja kesari varmarana Sree Rajarajathevarkku yaandu 32vathu
(thiruvarangathu) perumal .... (selithuvip)pommanom Sreerangathu
sabaiyom.. idhu sree vaishnava rakshai ! "

Means Rajraja did not die upto the year 985 + 32 = 1016 atleast !

Means Rajendra Thilaparvathampukkaruliyathu in his 3rd Regnl year is
not for First Death Anniversary of RRC ...

Means Rajendra - RRC co-rule is for more than 5 years ... !

This information is already passed on to the Thanjai Seminar in
Session this morning concluding this evening and experts are planning
to visit Srirangam to check this inscription personally ...

Will here more soon ..

thanks sps sir for making us take note of this inscription. i am no
epigraphist so i turned to dr rajavelu. while he confirmed the
authenticity of the information that the epigraph belong to RR, he
casted his doubts on the regnal year.

1. they have used numerals instead of words. therefore, the engraver
might have committed a mistake as the tamil numeral for 2 and 3 are more
or similar.

2. KAN sastri is of the opinion that the donations of RR recorded at
Tanjavur ends with 29th year. therefore the same was taken to be thelast
regnal year.

3, it is common fact that the regions located far away from the capitral
contiunue to use the regnal years of a king in spite of the fact he died
long back. for example the long regnal years for the pallava kings in
the chengal nadukarkkal.

4. this case being in the heartland sems to be a aberation. hence given
no importance by the historian.

5. the record is incomplete shows the unprofessional, and possibly the
no-backing of any royal authority.

6, rajavelu will come back with more concrete information. i hope.

sriraman PS
