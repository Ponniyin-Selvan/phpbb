Dr. Vaidyanathan Ganapati Sthapati...

by vj_episteme on Mon May 21, 2007 10:24 am

"Following his profitable conquest of Karnataka, Kerala and northern
Sri Lanka, the Tamil King Rajaraja Chola found himself with a
considerable budget surplus. With vast tracts of fertile farmland
already under year-round irrigation, he decided to build the largest
Siva temple in the world, Brihadisvara, at Thanjavur (Tanjore in
modern Tamil Nadu state), a stunning masterpiece of stonecraft
engineering. The temple, now on the United Nations World Heritage
List, was crowned in the king's vision. He capped the 216-foot main
sanctum with a single stone weighing 86 tons and set in place by
elephants' nudging it up a gentle sand ramp starting five miles
away. The great sthapati (architect and sculptor) Kunjara Mallan
Raja Raja Perunthachan was commissioned for the work.

That's a story that Dr. Vaidyanathan Ganapati Sthapati, 72, loves to
hear, for he is a lineal descendent of that same Perunthachan of a
thousand years ago. The great man's story reminds Ganapati Sthapati
today of the tradition he is commissioned by birth to uphold. and of
the indomitable spirit of his caste, the silpis, lords and and
masters of South India's hard and heavy granite.

Though fascinating, there are disturbing facts :

1. The so called 86 ton Single piece is made of 8 pieces and joined
as Sikaram.

2. The RAMP was not 5 mile long. Instead it is kind of COIL type
RAMP along the CONE (Vimanam ) structure..

3. More interestingly while the Vimanam was constructed, there
appears to have been huge sand-filling to support the TOP weight,
which was removed later...

4. There was another addition also.. The Lingam and Avudayar were
established earlier.. By Balalayam process, poojas performed to
SUBSSTITUTE. Then before Consecration, the Main LINGAM was made
LIVE (Deity concept inducted).

5. The shadow of the Sthupi also is cast on the Ground.


These are the views shared during course of various discussions in
our Group! pls ref articles by Dr. Kalikkovan / others in
http://www.varalaru.com also.

Brahadeeswara Temple is GREAT in several other aspects..

We do place again on record our PROFOUND respects to Dr. Ganapathy
Sthapathi - who has Edited and Published AINDHIRAM and
PRANAVAVEDHAM - a year back !

He is the brain behind several off-shore deities apart from 133'
Thiruvalluvar and 48' Thillaikkali (Chidambaram). The tallest
Anjaneyar which is erected at Panchavadi (Near Tindivanam - Pondy) !

regards / sps
