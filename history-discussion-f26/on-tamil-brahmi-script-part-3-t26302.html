Dear All,

Here we go with the 3rd part of the series.

In the second part, we went through the pre-history of Tamilnadu and how writing came into about from rock art to graffiti to Brahmi script. We came across the influence of Indus script in rock art and pottery graffiti of Tamilnadu. We also learnt how the Brahmi script was adopted for Tamil.

In the 3rd part, we look at the controversial origin of the Brahmi script itself again and examine a scholar's opinion that it was developed in the Maurian court during the Asokan period itself! We then go back to our original interests of Tamil Brahmi inscriptions and learn about certain early decipherments. We also introduce to our readers, the three variants of the Tamil Brahmi script ( see attached JPEG) and provide a paleographic chart ( seeattached JPEG image ) of the TB script itself. Readers are requested to gothrough the chart and understand how individual letters were written. It is self explanatory. This knowledge is essential for the forthcoming parts.

Next week, we shall study the 3 variants of the TB script in detail and visit some of the ancient caves to read and decipher important inscriptions ourselves and date them according to the paleographic evidence.

A week later we shall read few more inscriptions and learn about the transition of TB script and evolution of Vatteluthu. We finally conclude the series with the question, was TB the precursor to Maurian Brahmi ?

Happy reading.
