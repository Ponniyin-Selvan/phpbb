the unfinished gopuram at madurai is the Raya

It lies to the east of Pudu mandapam. Measuring 200
by 120 feet, it
is nearly twice the size of the base of the East
gopuram. If it had
been completed, it would have been one of the
biggest gopurams in
South India,
the structure owes its origin to Thirumalai
Naicker.

The monolithic pillars of Raya Gopuram are over 50
feet high and mark
the high degree of proficiency of the Dravidian
stonemasons. Its
pavilion pillars, lion-based pilasters and the
carvings on the jambs
display a keen sense of ornamentation. There are
many reliefs
(pudaippu sirpam) of Thirmalai Naicker and his
queens and a figure of
Meenakshi's coronation on the wall structure.
