Dear Friends,

As we are aware, Krupa (Technical) Gokul (History Research) Kamal
(Specialising Temple Architecture) Lavanya (Inscriptions &
Literature) & Ram (Inscriptions & Carnatic Music) have been the
success behind http://www.varalaaru.com team which is launched under the
able Guidance of Dr. Kalaikkovan & Dr. Nalini of Dr.Rasamanickanar
Historical Research Centre, Trichy. (Late) Dr.Rasamanickanar is the
father of Dr.Kalaikkovan - author of Pallavar varalaaru and other
famous History books. Presently Dr. RMHRC is also publishing
VARALAARU Hard copy bi-monthly - which is viewed as an Authentic
Historical Document by several Scholars.

Those who are following the e-issues month by month are bound to
notice the development of talent in each of them (team members) and
their improvement in ability to share informations with clarity, ease
& precise. Not a single data of varalaaru.com is refuted by any
Historical Scholar so far in these 12 months span!. Majority of
them are reading them with intereset and appreciation.

Apart from sharing Historical informations and writing "Rajakesari",
Mr. Gokul has exhibited his talent in reconstructing Tanjore temple
paintings of Rajaraja period. He is the youngest - shall we say
History Researcher - to render the Lecture and Live Demonstration -
honoured by Dr. Kalaikkovan - under the auspicious of Dr. Kura
Sreenivasan Trust - Guru of Iravatham Mahadevan - in January 2005,
which is well appreciated by Dr. Kalaikkovan, Dr. Nalini, Dr. Akila,
Dr. Lalithambal, Dr. Rajavelu, Dr. Kudavoil Balu and Vellayampattu
Sundaram of Sekar Pathipagam - which specialises in publishing
History Books - on the dias and by Dr. Sathiamurthy of ASI - in his
office in repeat demonstration at Chennai. Prior to Gokul, Dr.
Iravatham, Dr. Sathiamurthy have rendered such lectures to the
Scholar studded audience in earlier years.. Those who were present -
including myself, Lavanya, Kamal, Krupa , Ram, Satish & Sankaran have
shed tears in happiness and ecstacy in the ASI Complex, Tanjore Big
temple.

This forum "Ponniyinselvan yahoo Group" is the Mother of such great
children and we all take pride that our group is able to accomplish
such feats. Dr. Sridhar and other overseas members have also shared
their happiness on such accomplishments.

Now we can plan a couple of things : IDENTIFY A PONNIYINSELVAN DAY -
either the Day on which our Group was incorporated or AIPPASI
SADHAYAM (Natal star of PS) and chalk out some programme every year
related to all group activities with a meet and Seminar.

Varalaaru.com day - devoted to Tamil Varalaaru - in particular.

Some of us have been contributing privately Rs. 1000 per month to
bring out certain Historical Research Books - the first of
which "Penn Deyva Vazhipaadu" was also written and released by
Dr.Kalaikkovan in Jan 05. Another activity of the team.

Let us see whether it is possible to include more members in the team
so that we can work towards bringing out unpublished inscriptions /
tabulising and putting them in the search mode for speedy reference
etc.

New talents, who can allocate some attention and time, need to
strengthen our team.

I take this opportunity to request our members to go through
http://www.varalaaru.com issues whenever they find time and opine.

anbudan, sps

by suganya_ml on Fri Jun 24, 2005 6:25 am

Dear All,

Meeting u all after quite some time.
I was not in India for the past 2 weeks but travelled to London and
currently in California.

Just completed reading all the mails in the group. And, to begin with
my hearty CONGRATULATIONS to the VARALAARU team for their remarkable
achievement. HATS OFF to everyone of them.

Secondly I wanted to put forward the details I saw in the London
Museum. In straight words, I saw enormous number of Lord Shiva,
Parvathi, Natrajar and Nandhi idols (aging 10th - 12th Century) from
India in the museum. All the idols had the tags saying "Cola Period
10th/12th century South India". Even such idols we do not have in the
Madras Govt Museum. Every idol was so beautiful without any "oocham"
in it.

Also, I saw lot of Buddha statues taken from South India. As a crown
to all these, I saw a photograph of RockFort, Trichirapalli and
BigTemple, Thanjavur taken in 19th century which were amazingly beautiful.

In a way I was very happy to see India's treasures in a foreign
country but also felt hurt to see the way they preserve our treasure
which we fail to do.

I have taken pictures of all the idols I saw in London Museum. Will
post them in the group once I come back to India.

Lastly, I would go with all the points given by Sridhar except the
Malar idea. I am still interested in reading a book than reading from
a PDF file. I believe many people prefer to read from books than from
the screen. And, also if we release a Malar it will be a book worth
having for years.

SPS sir, I would suggest the Malar issue shall not be a free issue but
for cost. Our group members themselves will be interested in buying it.

Also, I would like to remind something. We had plans for the Monthly
NewsLetter in our group couple of months ago. We can refurbish that
idea as well and start it in this occasion.

Expecting ur views.

Cheers
Suganya
