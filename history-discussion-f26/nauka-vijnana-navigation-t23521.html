Nauka Vijnana - Navigation

by balajinallathambi on Mon Aug 18, 2003 1:06 pm

Hi all,

Yesterday on Bangalore ( 24th Aug) i attended an seminar on "Nauka Vijnana - navigation in ancient India by Dr M.K Sridhar PROF if Sanskrit, National College, Basvangudi , Bangalore"



The topic is more interesting, since it's cover the most part of our south India especially the Cholos and pallavas. I have some doubt?s in mind i would like to put this as thread.



A glimpse what i heard from the seminar?

Most Part of the traveling is done in the South India particularly thru the malabar region and thru the poompuhar port.

They are large number of ships build on those days, which can accommodate around 300 elephants.
The Ships are classified as how we are having in the present generation (all in Sanskrit words not able to rember all ).
They are large number of exports and imports taken place in the port cities like poompukar.
The imported items are animals (Tiger, Lion etc) and metals (Silver,Gold, Copper...)
The exported items are like Tin, china silk etc ...

It seems that the cholos have went apart to java islands and captured the city. And also in the seminar there is a word about Nariashima pallavar who encouraged trade. As the historic are telling that India never attempted to capture other lands, what about the cholos who had a mighty Naval and captured most part of the North East Asia.

The Questions are below.



1. How the navigation took place in the cholos's period ( what are the methodology they used for example they may need to find the depth of water etc...

2. They would have the things like sword and all (Which i saw in Tanore palace) which is very much needed for the war so what about the mines present during cholos period and infact the workshop for making weapons. Is there is any clue that how cholos handled it.

3. If poompuhar is the capital port city of south India then what are the imports and exports taken place during cholos period.



These are the thread i would like to place.

Hope to have a nice session on this topic.

Thanks

Balaji



PS: Prf M.K Sridhar was mentioning about a Huge bronze bell found in a church in New Zeeland, which dates to 3000-4000 years old, and in that bell they are some tamil wording. He was amazed to see that how they have transported the Bell from TamilNadu to the south part of the world.
