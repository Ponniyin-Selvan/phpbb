Thanjai thaLikuLaththaar - not kulathur.
This was a temple that was at least 300 years older than
Raja rajEsvaram.

It is a possiblity that Raja raja ChOlzar would have built a
newer temple in situ.

There is a deity - the famous Thanjai Vaaraahi - whose statue
is found in the newer temple. This statue is from an earlier
temple.

Unless more intense archeological work is done, nobody can
ascertain it correctly.
Unless they find some long lost literary evidence or a
yet unfound inscription.

It is quite common to see such a thing in PAndya nadu.- shades
of temples from various periods incorporated in a presently
existing temple.

Sometimes you see temples within temples like that.

But then PAndya nadu had a more chequered history. Most of
its important northern temples were destroyed by the Turks.

Have alook at this URL -

http://www.geocities.com/Vienna/Choir/4262/navkanta.htm

We don't know where the statues from this temple come from.

But one thing is certain. They belong to an earlier period.
Perhaps the temple was a separate major one. It might
have had a name. But now it is known as 'PuumAyi amman temple'.
PuumAyi is VaishNavi - one of the seven MAtrikas.

The Tanjai Vaaraahi also belongs to this class of
deities/statues.

And also more or less the same period - or much earlier.
