by thirumalaikv on Mon Apr 16, 2007 11:21 pm

My initial historic novel reading started off with Sandilyan and then Kalki
and others.

From: my perspective

1. he used to give equal weight to both Hero and the Opponent (not a
villain).
2. His works have more "Sirungara Rasam" as he himself quoted on one of his
novels introduction. Sometime between real characters!.
3. More fictional characters, and from my reading experience most of the
heroes and heroines.
4. Episodes will end with dramatic twists and turns. As the other member
pointed out, the hero's strategy will be invincible and sometime will be
very dramatic.

by ymr26 on Tue Apr 17, 2007 8:00 pm

Hi,
I read Rajah Thilakam before even I touched Kalki's works. After
reading PS I read a lot of Kalki's short novels and just now I
finished Kadal Pura and have started Jegasirpiyan's Alavaialzhagan.
I agree with the following points w.r.t Sandilyan.
1. The hero in Sandilyan novels are more than life like.
2. They know it all.
3. At times I dont understand why two people in the same situation
cannot think in the same lines as the hero. BIT illogical to me since
this happens everytime.
4. The hero always falls in love with two women.
5. In the end he marries both of them.
6. The women he potrays are very brave ones but are self-depreciating
when they think w.r.t the hero. Of the two one is dominant and the
other submissive.

I have few bones of contention. Sandilyan didnt emphasize much on the
third part of Kadal Pura. He did a good description of palur port and
things like that in first part. I was not satisfied with the
description of the war to win over Srivijayam. I felt he took us very
quickly about the turn of events.

I agree that he cannot satisfy every one's imagination. But stil....my
opinions....

Jegasirpiyan's novels are short and quite descriptive at the same
time. I have read Makarayaalzh mangai. It was fast paced and good
narration. One feels in the end "Yeh Dil Maange More"

Regards,
Ragothaman
