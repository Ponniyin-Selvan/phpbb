Dear Gokul,

'Madura Vijayam' and allied topics have been dealt with
in detail in Agathiyar.
Kumara Kampanna Udaiyar has also been treated several
times.
It all in Tamil.
KumaraKampanna was the son of Bukka Raya I. Harihara I
and Bukka I along with their three other brothers were the sons
of Sangama.
Harihara and Bukka were the founders. Harihara became the
first ruler in 1336 followed by Bukka. Kampanna's descendants did not
rule Tamilnadu. Bukka's youngest son came to the Vijayanagar throne.
Tamilnadu was ruled by feudal war lords called 'amara nayaks'.
Later on they were supervised by feudal lords called 'Mandalesvara's.
Some of them were more supreme and were known as
'Maha Manadalesvara's.
Later still, the empire was divided into Nayakdoms. Each was
ruled by hereditary nayaks who were directly under the emperor.
These nayakdoms were Madurai, Tanjavur, and Senji. Senji and Tanjavur
were captured by the Marathas. Madurai nayakdom lasted until 1734.
The last emperor of Vijayanagar died as mad fugitive in the Mysore
hills in 1676.
From: 1336 - 1676, 4 houses ruled over Vijayanagar - Sangama,
Saaluva, Tuluva, and Aaravidu. But Aravidu did not rule as supreme
emperors. They lost their capital and imperial territories to the
sultanates of Golconda and Bijapur. Their nayaks became more powerful
and the emperors became their dependants.
Madurai nayakdom was ruled by the house of Visvanatha Nayak.
Visvanatha, Tirumalai, and Mangamma were the greatest rulers among
that house.
Manggamma was the wife of Chokkanatha Nayaka of Madurai.
She ruled as regent during the minorship of her son and grandson.
She did not rule over all of Tamilnadu - just the old districts of Madurai,
Ramnad, Thirunelveli, Kumari, parts of Kovai and Salem and Tiruchi.
That would be about a little more than half of the present Tamilnadu.

Regards

JayBee
