Dear Gokul,

I was talking over phone to a member of American Archeological
Society - specialising in south east asia - particularly on Tamil kings.

He stunned me with a perspective :

In Thiruvalangadu Copper plates referring Aditha Karikalan's demise,
it is mentioned that he hastend up to join the Heaven. Then the
Earth was Surrounded by the darkness of Kali. Then those bearing the
burden of the Earth were happy that a King - avatar of Vishnu has come
down to take this burden..

The Scholar pointed out whether Uthama's Rule was referred as Darkness
surrounding the Earth ?

KAN Sastry appears to have followed this line of thinking..

Likewise 2000 years back, the prominent Sivalayas in south would have
been possibly Rameswaram (Jothir) - Chidambaram - Srisailam.

Therefore it would have been the desire of ardent siva- devotee Kings
to visit Mount Kailash and those Kings in the surrounding kingdoms
would not have objected to such expeditions atleast.. Even today we
are able to get Chinese concurrence for Amarnath yatra.

Karikalan - Neduncheralathan - Nedunchezhiyan would have undertaken
such expeditions and whenever they were in Himalayan range, would have
created the symbols of TIGER - FISH AND BOW - mark of Tamil Kings
expedition in the range...

Possibly there would have been a pact not to attack each other when
the Other King undertake such expedition.

When King Alexander can travel and reach Sindh from his Province, I do
not see why we should doubt our Kings' Nothern expeiditions - atleast
as far as they are viewed as Relegious in nature.

Rajendra's Ganges expedition is totally a military expedition with
ample evidences...

Nanda put it more appropriately..

We ourselves can collect sufficient data and make atleast skeleton
draft.. then take approvals of Scholars..

No doubt Madhan is right in mentioning sufficient materials not avaible..

but we are more right in telling that there have been enough materials
collected after 1935 - 40 - post Pandarathar / Sastry Era - which have
not found the deserving attention .. which prompted IAS Iraiyanbu to
put the question in the first place..

And there have been differences in the data related to Moghuls as well..



regards/ sps
