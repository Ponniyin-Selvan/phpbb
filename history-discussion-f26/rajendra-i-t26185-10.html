It formed the centre of a huge Agaram -
grant to Brahmins who qualified themselves with the
knowledge and recital of the four VEdas. This Agaram
was called 'Niruba Sekara ChaturVEda Manggalam'.
An Agaram is otherwise known as Brahma DEsam.
They always have a title - ChaturVEda Manggalam.

The temple was rebuilt during the time of Kulothunga
Chola I.
This temple is of great historic value because of
several rare and unique information that can be gleaned
from it.
During the time of RajaRaja Chola, the Pandya country
was annexed to the Chola Empire. During Rajendra Chola I's
rule, the Pandyas were expelled and made into persona non grata -
without any recognition - and simply made into non-entities.
Their identity was made non-existant.
A new dynasty was formed.
The sons of Rajendra Chola were conferred the title of
ChOla-PAndya and ruled as the kings in the PAndya country.
They went by names like 'Sundara ChOla-PAndya' etc.
Every town, village were renamed after the ChOlas.
Even irrigation tanks and canals were thus renamed.
The Pandya country was renamed 'RAjaRAja PAndya NAdu'.
Countries were divided into provinces called 'vaLa nAdu's.
These were subdivided into 'nAdu's and 'kURRam's. The
next sub-subdivision was 'Ur' and 'GrAmam'.
The province of 'KEraLa Singga VaLa NAdu' became
'RAjEndra ChOla VaLa NAdu'. 'Niruba SEkara ChadurVEda
Manggalam' became 'ChOla MArthANda Chatur VEda Manggalam'.
One canal was renamed into 'KarikAl ChOla VAykkAl'. And
an irrigation tank came to be called 'Sri Kundhavai
PErEri'.
This process is known as 'ChOlianisation'.

There was a colony of merchants called 'Thisai Ayirathu
AinURRuvar'. They had a chief town called AruviyUr'. The
merchants of AruviyUr were liberal patrons for this temple.
I was able to discover AruviyUr because of the information
that I collected from this temple.

*I also discovered a class of warriors called 'VALIlAr'-
????????. There is a reference to them in an inscription of the
12th year of Vikrama ChOla. I traced their descendants who
are still around after 800 years. *

KulOthunga ChOla I does not belong to the direct
lineage of the VijayAlaya line of ChOlas to which RAja RAja
or RAjEndra belonged. He was actually a prince of the
Eastern Chalukya(Telugu) dyansty. But due to repeated
intermarriage with the Imperial ChOlas, he had more ChOla
blood in him than Chalukya.

But there had been upheavals when and after he came
to power. For one thing, the Pandya country became loosened
from his grip. Inscriptions belonging to the same time -
even engraved by the same sthapathi, are found. Some
would bear the name of KulOthungga. But there are others which
bear the name of one SriVallabha PAndya. That is to say,
the PAndyas had been reinstated.
But all the same, KulOthungga maintained many
cantonments of the army right upto the Cape Kumari.

We also find something sinister taking place during
his reign. His feudatories and war-lords meet in the temple
and take oaths of allegience with each other. Breaking of the
oath was denounced with the severest imprecations and curses.
I have come across four such pacts in that temple.

The other peculiarity is that, although it is
dedicated to ThAnthOnRIswarar - Svayam PrakAshar or Svayambhu
Linggam, there is an installed SivaLinggam. What happened
to the original Svayambhu? May be I am the only one alive
who knows.

Another strange thing is that, all the contemporary
temples in that territory were destroyed and sacked by
the Turks. But this temple was only partially destroyed.
Somehow the Turks seem to have abandoned the sacking
somewhere along the line. There is a very interesting and
strange story behind it.

The Nandhi is huge, The Dvarapalakas are huge;
but the SivaLinggam is very small.

There are also two hidden chambers, one of which was
opened by me - much to my future regret.
Many a times, I would go to the temple to copy the
inscriptions. The GurukkaL would make some Horlicks and
leave it with me; and then he would lock the temple from
outside during the 'uchchi kAlam'.
I would be alone. At certain particular times,
I would feel a tingling sensation on the nape of my neck.
I would feel something; something creepy. The hair would
stand on its end. There will be some loud wind blowing;
the trees will be shaking. That will be around mid-day.
Most often, I used to hear voices and sounds of
people walking around. Sometimes, I had a sensation that
somebody or somebodies were watching me closely from
behind.
Who or what were they?
I don't know.
Never found out.
Never could find out.
I never turned back to look.
I am not supposed to look back.
So I never did.
I even found a long hidden valuable inscription by
a freak occurance.
Even two years ago, I found a freize of GajaSamhara
Murthi. It is the smallest that can be found. It was also
found unexpectedly.
The ChAmundi statue is so full of life that it can
enchant you. So is the Amman 'Dharma Samvardhini' or 'ARam
VaLartha NAyagi'.
Even the jade Sivalinggam for which I do the daily puja,
called ThAnthOnRIswarar, came to me under strange circumstances.

But this temple which has withstood the onslaughts and
vicissitudes, and ravages of time, is now succumbing to vandalism
of the first order. It is taking place under the pretext of
renovation. Many beautiful edifices have been broken up or
smothered with concrete.
These present day vandals are worse than Malik kafur
or Kusrau Khan or Thuglaq.
