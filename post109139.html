Dear Madhu,

Yes, indeed the topic is diverted. In fact even SPS sir asked to
changed the subject line to suitably reflect the topic being
discussed. Thanks for the reminder.

But I would like to say something about the discussions.

1. Your opinion is absolutely valued. I am a member of various
groups. But I have seen that this groups is much ahead in respecting
everyone's opinions, though there can be disagreements. That doesn't
mean buying the opinion of others, though in disagreement. It just
means that every one is welcome to present their views.

2. It is always natural for new threads being started out of the
existing one. But as our SPS sir indicated, we should have changed
the Subject. We did not and sorry for that. Just that some one picks
one point interesting and convenient for them and starts to discuss.
Atleast the topic was changed to "Hinduism Bad Karma and Good Karma".
This itself is a pointer that this slip was done unconsiously though
reminders were there to correct the course.

All,
Let us take this as a constructive-criticism and correct our
mistakes.

Madhu,
We are thankful to you for bringing this to notice.

3. Regarding the participation of other members out of a strength of
more than 1500, nothing can be done about the non-participants. There
could be many reasons for non-participation, starting from Shyness to
Ignorance to many other reason. We can only say that the group does
not restrict any one from participating, as long as the decorum is
maintained. Disagreements to any degree are allowed to be voiced
here, provided the aesthetics are maintained, without any foul
language and name calling. Just for an example, I find that you have
participated for the first time now. If you are a member for long
time here, you have broken the nutshell or if you are a new entrant,
then you are bang right on. Let us respect others for what they are.
But there is always a time when others will come out in the open and
contribute. Until then unfortunately it will be only those 4 or 5
repeating persons who will contribute. I, myself, was a silent
spectator for atleast 4 to 6 months before I felt comfortable to join
the discussions.

4. Within those few people who contribute frequently, there are well
learned people. I really mean it. I dont want to specifically start
naming them for the fear of missing out somebody. There are
distinguished media persons to well known archaeologist, to great
writers, to very young archeological amateurs, to wonderful people
who have done great researches on the Tamizh language and many more.
Naturally there can be fear for some to come out in the open to
contribute, if they had known the background of the persons in this
group.

So please remember that all are welcome to contribute and as said
earlier, there are no restrictions on anyone, except when they use
foul language.

Just thought I will share my views here. Please do not take it
offensively. If my words have hurt you, I sincerely apologize in
advance for the same.

Regards,
Venkatesh
