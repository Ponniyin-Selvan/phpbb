--- Inponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, "Sivapathasekaran"
wrote:

We are glad to inform you that a Pilot CD was released by an arm
named INDIAN SCIENCE MONITOR (TKV RAJAN) just before recent
elections by Vaiko.

Now, in continuation, another CD is being released TODAY by TKV
Rajan's Indian Science Monitor at 1845 hrs in Bharadhiya Vidhya
Bhavan, Mylapore, (near Rasi Silk - Kapali Temple.
=====================================================
The meet is well reported in today's Dinamalar (22.6.2006) Chennai
Edition with a photograph.

Apart from regular discussions on Adichanallur etc., there are
certain interesting high points :

1. Thanks to Gokul, Delhi Tamil Sangam is well aware of
Ponniyinselvan e-group - Mr. Mukundan and Mr. Penneswaran
represented - and Rajavelu (ASI) did mention the details of our
Group in the dias;

2. For culture based Seminars etc., Delhi Tamil Sangam, which runs
a School of about 8000 Tamil students is willing to look at
proposals;

3. Similarly another CAMBRIDGE school from Darmapuri is ready to
look at such proposals.

4. Mr. Sree Borgia (Sterling Holidays MD) showed interest in
Ancient Tamil Study and is in the process of creating a
Silapathikaram Environment in Madurai (Kannagi - Nedunchezhian
Darbar - Dual with Sun God etc)!

5. Supreme Court (Rtd) Justice Siva Natarajan - brought out the
high points of Tamil sofistication in handwriting reading (navalur -
sundarar), cross examinations etc., and concluded that very good
LAW Enforcement system existed in TN much against the popular
belief that it is only Europe which showed us the way on such
matters;

6. Dinamalar Sri Krishnamurthy's video of Sangam Kaasu collections,
TKV Rajan's interview with Scholars and photos of Adhichnallur -
(some of them figure them in the links provided by Gokul) - created
enormous enthusiasm among participants and those in the dias.

There is so much work done already to establish the richness of our
ancestry and traditions - this need to be spread out - particularly
in schools and school books;

There is so much yet to be done and support need to come from Non-
Government quaters.

Those who are involved need to be supported and the number
increased and Press carrys major responsibility in these arena.

Stalwarts like Classical Dancer Rathika Surjit - Cambridge School
Darmapuri Correspondent attended the meet which lasted for about 2
hrs from 645 pm in the Bharathiya Vidhya Bhawan, Mylapore.

sps
