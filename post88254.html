ok ... let me add what I know about the current theories on how the
Universe began... First of all..this breaking down of elements into
earth,fire, water, AIR and space is outdated (I think it was also
prevalent among the greeks).

The most commonly accepted theory these days is that of the Big Bang,
which states that ALL matter that currently exists in the universe was
initially present in a singularity.. a singularity is basically a
point of infinite density.. so all of it.. matter, space everything
was within this infinitesimal point of infinite density...

A question that can be asked is then this: So what was "outside" this
point? Stephen Hawking, the physicist, in his book "A Brief History of
Science" says that the question is not valid under the conditions.
(Unfortunately, I don't think there are too many people who can even
think of NOT asking the question.. people like us... and I am sure we
won't understand the answer either"..

Hawking also goes on to say that time did not exist... Time starts
with a reference point, and the reference point for that is the Big
Bang, which is when this infinitely dense dot blew up and expanded to
the Universe as it is today.

There are also a few competing theories as to what happens now:

A) One theory says that the Universe has reached a steady state of expansion

B) Another viewpoint is that the Universe will finally start
contracting on itself till it comes to a Big Crunch, that is all the
matter in the Universe will again start contracting till it becomes a
singularity.. an infinitesimally dense point...

At this point, time again ceases to exist.. On the whole this theory
is the one that is closes to the Hindu view of Epochs happening one
after the other.

C) Yet another theory is that the Universe will keep expanding..

One must remember that these are all theories... and theories only
exist to explain observed phenomenon.. they are not the truth per se..
so we don't know what actually happened

Alos, I am not in favor of digging up old Hindu (or Muslim or
Christian etc) mythology and reinterpreting it in light of new
discoveries and saying: See our sages said this ages ago...

Hope this helps,

Cheers,

Arun
