Re Navigation and Sailing Vessels of Early days:

Source : Text of talk delivered by former Chief Mechanical Engr of
Vizag port & World Bank consultant KRA Narsaiah:

... Crossing the open sea was not even thought (in India) of as the
necessity never arose .. Wooden boats could travel to Sri Lanka which
was considered an extension of Indian Coast..

The ships were known as Kalam, Naavai, Vangam and Kappal..

Kalam : Timber vessel used locally btwn Ports of Coasts;
Navai was ocean going vessel;
Vangam was fitted with Mast and few a flag;
Kappal was a later term probably from Telugu.

An East - West coastal stretch from KODIKARAI to TONDI - apart from
Adiramapatnam, Muthupettai were ideal places for Ship building.

Early Indian sailors had sufficient knowledge of the seasonal winds
and (water) currents .. Coastal navigation was based on shore markers
- general topography and Light-houses as indicated in Sangam literature..

Target ports were suffixed with KURI (target !).. In East coast -
Ambalakuri where ships changed course from South to South East and
reached Srilankan coast. Ships reached AKKARAIPPATTI in East Coast of
Srilanka and set sail towards East across Sea..

QUOTING AUTHOR :

" .. THIS MUST HAVE BEEN THE WAY CHOSEN BY THE CHOZHA FLEET TO REACH
SRI VIJAYA. SINCE THEY SAILED BELOW 10 DEGREE PARALLEL, IT WAS NOT
POSSIBLE FOR THEM TO SITE POLE STAR, WHICH DISAPPARED INTO THE HAZE OF
THE HORIZON .. " (unquote). !

Sun / Star AHIMUTH was calculated based on Viral (Finger) Kanakku..
Raise of Arudhra in early January indicated commencement of sailing
season .. Arudhra & Margaseerishm (Orion Group - Path finders) Rohini
& Kiruttika (Port bow side), Makam (on the Star board) & Tiruvonam
(which raises as Arudhra sets - near Equator !) are other sailor's
stars... From: Cauvery Delta, Jan & Feb were ideal to sail out ..

Viral kanakku (Tamil) Anguli (Sanskrit - 3/4 of an inch) Dhru
(Gujarathi) Isaba (Arab) and Chih in Chinese are all the same.

Narasayya's contemporary experiences were published as KADALODI ....
Highly respected Seaman.
=====================================

anbudan / sps

Distance also measured by Viral kanakku..

8 Yaamam was one day. There were 56 Groups of Stars - with local
names - known to Tamil Sea-men! Field knowledge was passed on to
family members orally.
