PARAKESARI & RAJAKESARI ::

Two versions by Historians : Alternatively the Heir - apparents were Ruling
Chozha Kings were adopting (given) this title :: Parakesari :: Sundara; RK ::
RR; PK : RJC.

But Sundara was PK; Aditha Karikalan was also PK. Uthama was PK. RR was RK.
However, It is named after Lion and nothing to do with beheading -
since "Beheading" portion do precede "Rajakesari" or "Parakesari" titles -
in inscriptions.

GOLDEN AGE :: Messages are flowing on the lines "In 900s AD, when people
thought World was flat ?! .. comon guys let us do ... " and things like that

Please read Eric Von Dunkon (Chariot of Gods etc., ) and similar ones on
ancient Culture & History .. Construction of Pyramids etc., are classic
examples that we are yet to attain the perfection of the Ancient ..
Some one says the Somnathpur Lingam was a Maragatha Lingam without support
from any side, including top and bottom, just was located in the mid air
above by balancing gravity;

Similar mid-AIR Sun God was at Konarak which was indicating the direction of
the Sun through out the day / night.

Sthapathis were aware of GROWING Stones and made Bulls (Nandhi's) out of
them - and though they made small Nandhis thousand years back, they are big
now;

Several Siddhis - like Rasavadham - cloning etc., were practiced in Ancient
days...

Such people who were aware of such great things were GURUs and the Kings had
such Gurus and guided their countrys to Glory.

India was invaded because of its Glory ..

anbudan, sps

We will march towards the Glory .. and bring back Golden age..

Dear Satish,

Don't compare always. Don't counter, that only by comparing we can improve.

it is not advisable to compare greats. There are people who are great even today. Butt he thing is that the greatness would not be accepted when the person is alive. Only when he is no more people realise any person's contribution.

When Bharathiyar was alive, he was called as "Paithiyam". My Grandfather who had seen Bharathiyar in the streets of Thiruvallikeni, used to say so. Now we call him Maha purushan and all those things and the poor fellow's funeral was attended only by a handful of people.

So don't come to a conclusion every now and then , saying today's world is not as good as what it used to be sometime back.

Also whatever Mahaperiyava said may not be true always. It is said that He did not want to meet a widowed mother of a devotee , because it was considered inauspicious to meet a widow( this is hearsay only, not sure how fat it is true)

With Regards

B. Dhiwakar
