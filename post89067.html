Digitization of Mysore Univ. archives

by nsramkishan on Tue Jun 19, 2007 1:16 pm

Hi all,

When I was reading "Business Today", dated June 17, I came across this
info: "Google will be digitizing 80000 manuscripts, inscriptions, and
palm leaves avilable in the Mysore University archives". It further
stated that the archive included the original version of Arthasasthra.
There was no mention about tamil inscriptions, except that there were
100000 such inscriptions and other forms in the archive.

Though the task in hand is enormous, I hope Google will be able to
complete the exercise. Waiting for the day when we all will be able to
see the digital version of the tamil inscriptions, copper plates, and
things like that that we have been discussing in our group.

--Ramakrishnan

Digitization of Mysore Univ. archives

by sampath_srinivasaraghavan on Tue Jun 19, 2007 7:51 pm

This issue was raised in the yahoo group "Hindu Civilization" in the
first week of june this year. There were many objections to this plan
from various sections - academics, archaeologists and lay people. I
believe the plan was shelved by Mysore University.
The letter below was sent by Prof. Prasad of Mysore University.
Sampath

From: Prof. J. Shashidhara Prasad
Date: Jun 3, 2007 11:17 AM
Subject: Re: Fwd: Theft of Palm-leaf manuscripts
To: kalyan97

Dear Dr.Kalyanaraman,

Thank you for your e-mail. University of Mysore has not signed any
agreement at all. There was a proposal but University of Mysore, during
the preliminary discussion had raised several points pertaining to copy
rights, trade mark and patentable material. In fact I had to refuse the
offer by an American agency which had come forward for similar support
based on the same points of patentable material. I am a working
scientist by profession and I am aware of the values of our heritage
more than manuscriptologists. I am exploring the possibility of taking
up the work with the support of National Manuscript Mission of Ministry
of Culture. Even then we can not put them on to public domain.

I can understand the anxiety of people working in this area. University
of Mysore will not get into any agreement with any organization without
giving proper thought for all the important issues.

Profesor Prasad
