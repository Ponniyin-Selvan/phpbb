by sswetha_2000 on Wed Jul 20, 2005 9:23 pm

vanakkam,

enna ithu thideernu ivvalavu amaithiya irukku? A lull considering in
the hectic activities of the past week.

Coming to the point,

1. Uththama Chozhan rules the country for more than a decade. The
last two years of his rule, he shares the throne with AMV.

2. AMV and VD undertook naval expeditions and roamed around the
country during Uththama's rule. This is probably a strategy to
mingle with the people and gauge the country.

3. Ravidasan was one of the important ministers in Uththama's
council. How long he held that post, was he a minister when AMV was
the co-regent, these are anybody's guess.

4. Two years after he became the king, RRC held Ravidasan and
associates responsible for AK's death. He punished them by exiling
their families (almost the entire population of Udaiyurkudi
village).

5. How RRC ascended the throne, we still don't know. Did Uththama
die or was he dethroned? Uththama had a male heir who served as a
Temple Audit Official - probably one of the 'Enperayam Council' or
a 'Perunthanathu Athikari' - whether he laid a claim to the throne
or not, we don't know.

6. There is a line of thought that Uththama along with his mother
Sembiyanmadevi conspired to get the throne and they were assisted by
Ravidasan, who was a friend-cum-guide (not abaththudavi) of Veera
Pandiyan.

7. Nandhini killing AK doesn't come into the picture as she is a
fictitious character.

8. Sendhan Amudhan and Poongkuzhali are also imaginary characters.

9. Anirudhdhar seems to have vanished(!) during Uththama's period.
He however adorned RRC's court.


Aftermath of PS has lots of scope. Kalki mudivuraiyil koduthirukkum
ovvoru pointukkum oru kathai ezhuthalam.

'Nandipurathu Nayaki' is widely considered to be the sequel to PS. A
good book, but definitely not in the genre of PS.
