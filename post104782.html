why are they fanatical?

by sillybuffer on Thu Apr 14, 2005 9:47 am

Dear All,

I guess I should put mu tuppence worth in. I agree that what Ramadoss and co
are doing is taking things to an extreme. However, I also feel that the
level of spoke Tamizh has deteriorated remarkably, especially among the
English educated folks. This needs to be corrected. I am so ashamed when I
hear a Bengali or someone from UP speak their respective languages, with
nary a word of English thrown in. But watch a couple of tamilians speak, and
it is rather appalling. Just for kicks, check out some programs on Sun TV
(let's say the news) and watch a couple of Tamilians give their views. And
for contrast watch a couple of Northern people speak Hindi and you can see
the stark difference. What amazes me is this. People feel ashamed to make
even simple grammatical errors while speaking English. But they appear to
have absolutely no problems making all sorts of errors while speaking
Tamizh. Why is this the case?


As my own small effort towards correcting this tendency, I have started a
yahoo group called Tamizhvalarchchi where I send out a word-a-day (Dinamoru
Tamizhchchol). You can access it at
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/tamizhvalarchchi.


Yes, we ought not to take it to extremes, but we ought to be able to speak
our mothertongues well.

Cheers,

Arun

pittsburgh. The priest (i believe a non-tamil) was chanting "pallaandu
pallaandu pallayirathaandu.. ". It was nice to hear that. don't know
that priest knew the meaning, but i was able to comprehend.. If you
communicate in a language you know you can immediately connect.. With
thousands of religious songs in tamil, It would be a common-sense
thing to use tamil in prayers.

Even for a non-religious person like me, hearing devotional songs that
I understand makes sense.. I believe you are passionate about
religion. Common sense dictates that you should be in the forefront to
lead this to cultivate interest in the people..

by sillybuffer on Thu Apr 14, 2005 9:52 am

As an addendum.. someone mentioned Carnatic Music.. For me personally, I
tend to listen more to instrumental Carnatic rather than vocal (unless it is
Nithyashree Mahadevan). Then again, I realized that one of the reasons for
that might be that all the Thyagaraj Keertanais are in Telugu and I can't
really understand those. However, I also realized that I enjoyed Bharathiyar
compositions immensely, and then it dawned on me that maybe its the
language.. I like listening to what I understand. In a way, instrumental
music takes the language variable out of the equation.. but sometimes the
beauty of the words is lost. So yeah, hearing something in a language one
understands CAN make a difference.

Cheers,

Arun
