Reference:"Politics of plunder" by George W Spencer 1976.

DEAR SAMPTH,

THESE KIND OF CRITICISMS ARE GOING ON FOR A LONG TIME. I AM NOT A
HISTORIAN. BUT PATRIOT. I SEEK THE GUIDANCE OF THE LEARNED TO
ILLUMINATE THE DARKNESS IN THE MINDS OF THE SO CALLED WESTERN CRITICS:

Prof Y Subbarayalau, MA, M Lit, Ph D (Medieval South India 600 - 1350),
Prof Archlgy, Tamil Univ, Thanjai - Visiting Prof Tokyo Univ, Research
Scholar Madras Univ, Member Indo - Srilankan Projs, Member: Indian
History Congress, South Indian History Congress, Tamilnadu History
Congress, Epgl Soc of India, Tamilnadu Archlgl society. with 48
publications, 5 books, including the Place Ur in Eco & Social History
of Tamilnadu 750 - 1350 AD., contributes :

QUOTE ::

" CHOZHA STATE :: It need not be emphasised that Chozha state was
secondary state to Pallavas.

Chozha Rule Divided into 3 periods :
850 - 985 :: Vijayalaya - RR (Pre Imperial)
986 - 1100 :: RR to Virarajendra Kulothinga.
1100 - 1250 :: Post Imperial

The first phase was existence of small Kingdom, claiming soverign
rights over restricted territory. Local bodies and communities enjoyed
traditional powers. The Imperial phase was marked with powerful
monarchy, GROWTH OF PRIVATE PROPERTY AND WELL SATISFIED SOCIETY. HE
WAS NOT ONLY GREAT WARRIOR BUT ALSO REAL ADMINISTRATOR ASSISTED BY
ORGANISED BODY OF OFFICIALS AND ARMY. TERRITORIAL REORGANISATION LIKE
CREATION OF VALANADU, MANDALAM - ELABORATE BUREAUCRACY, EXTENSIVE LAND
SURVEY, DETAILED RECORDING OF LAND RIGHTS FOR REVENUE PURPOSES,
GOVERNMENT CONTROL ON BRAHMADEYAS & TEMPLES, MOBILISATION OF BIG ARMY
FOR OFFENSIVE AND DEFENSIVE, MAINTAINING INTERNAL ORDER. Tho' No direct
evidence that the State controlled Irrigation system, it reached the
optimum in Kaveri delta during the 10th Century. The Local communities -
including Vellala - Brahmins. Officials were mobile. THE CENTRALISED
POWER STRUCTURE OF THE IMPERIAL PHASE, WHICH WAS DEPENDING ON THE
ABILITY OF THE RULING KING, COULD NOT CONTINUED UNDER CHANGED
CIRCUMSTANCES .. paved way for emergence of various locality Chiefs and
multiplicity of tax collecting agencies. Consequently crises developed
between Big Landlords and cultivators, which attributed to the ultimate
demise of Monarchy. "UNQUOTE.
** In support there are 148 citations !

VERY CLEAR THAT SPENCER IS HALF-BAKED. A POWERFUL KING WAS ABLE TO
CONTROL. IT IS THE LESS POWERFUL PAVED WAY FOR DECENTALISATION ! MOCK
AT THIS FELLOW SPENCER.


LAND MEASUREMENT ACCURACY : Among the Land Extents of ABOUT 40
VILLAGES, 10% refer precision to fraction less than 40.5 Square Inch
Kil-Kil-Ma and 90% to factions above that, They have classified lands
and graded "Tharam" to 10 and taxes levied in accordance;

Land were properly suveyed and Classified (Taram) in 1001. Yield level
was about 100 Kalam / Veli, the Best ever produced in South on river
beds. 20 - 50 k/v on well irrigation; below dry crops.

The concessions amounted to 1/5th share Panca vaaram during first two
periods; to 60% during Second two periods. Main Revenue was from
Kaveri Delta. Concentration of Royal and official activities. The
concessions amounted to 15% only on average.

On a rough estimate, there were 1300 villages in Naduvilnaadu (Trichy /
Thanjr / SA) of which 1000 were paying full taxes.
Estimated production about 29000 Tonnes from Delta alone 10 Centuries
back!

In Kolar 1/3rd to Govt, 1/5th from Dry crops!

Do you mean to say that the Chozhas fought a war to get 1/5th of dry
crops moving 900000 soldiers!? Rediculous.

He has tabulated 58 case studies from 945 AD to 1174 on Land Revenue
aloneshowing average of 75 kalam / veli in 945, 120 kalam / veli in
1003, 122 k/v in Chidambaram in 1036, 54 k/v in 1122, 70 k/v Srirangam
in 1202 AD.

This gives us an idea of Prosperity of Inlands and Wars were not
necessiated to suppliment income / subdue unrest ..

They were purely for Political reasons ... ..

There was another theory that the Chozhas were Marxists ..
YS thwarted this theory also..

Dear SPS,

I think spencer's views are supported in ponnyin selvan.

Spencer talks about declining revenues of cholas.
kalki talks about the same.paluvettarayar's major complaint is that
the treasury is empty.

Spencer talks about inter rivalry between sitrarasars.
Kalki says the same thing.Some local kings like paluvettarayar were
even controlling chola kingdom according to kalki.

My guess is during sundara chola regime,local kings' influence grew
considerably.Maybe in uthama cholas regime the situation
worsened.Maybe Rajaraja and Rajendra reversed this trend later on.

regards
sampath
