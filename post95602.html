NANDHINI'S FATHER

by vksankaran on Wed May 19, 2004 4:46 pm

Dear Friends,
I think this is a very delayed response for this debate. I don't
think any body will be offended of my views.

If you notice carefully, Kalki had given more emphasis on things of
which he was confident of and knew that this is what had ACTUALLY
happened. Things that were not clear, Kalki hadn't given much
emphasis. There are places in the novel, where Kalki himself
indirectly admits somethings as his addditions. Like, the words he
puts after Kudanthai Josiyar telling about the birth of a Son to
Vaanathi, on a Thiruvaathirai day. At the end of the paragraph,
Kalki puts these words, "Josiyar therinthE sonnarO, ookithu sonnarO
yaam aRiyOm".

Well, coming bback to the original issue, in so many places Nandhini
herself tells that Veerapandiyan was her LOVER and not her father.
Even before the killing of Adithya Karikalan, she talks to the ghost
of veerapandiyan and address him as AnbE. So veerapandiyan is not
Nandhini's father.

And Kalki has devoted at least 2 full chapter in introducing
Karuthiruman and telling his story. His introduction is the one that
makes a twist in the tale. (Would Kalki, the genious who had
produced such a gem would add a character at the end to create
confusion among the readers.) His parents named him as Karutha
Thirumaal, so he is a vaishnavite. This might be the reason, why
Aazhvaarkadiyan was so moved towards Nandhini and wanted to make her
like an AaNdal. karuthiruman is the guy who took Mandakini to bodha
theevu and might have forced her to become pregnant (Putting it in
straight terms raped). Since this might have been unknown to Kalki
himself and he might not have wanted to add any "kalankam" to the
lively characters, he might not have added these things. Also he had
already created a god like imagge for Mandakini and by this time
Karuthiruman came into the picture, she was dead. So he didn't want
the readers to learn that she was forced to be pregnant.
But he let it known to the readers thru one of his characters.
Otherwise, he wouldn't have written Vandiyathevan telling Kunthavai,
as to what karuthiruman had told Aazhvaarkadiyan when he was
escaping along with Madhuranthakan.

This is my just my assumption and not conclusion.
So please don't get offended if this contradicts your views and
sentiments.


Rgds,
Vijay
