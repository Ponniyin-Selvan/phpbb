Hi Malathi & Satish

The original is Ramayana itself. That was live, all others were
comments based on the live relay.

Valmiki got the first rights to the relay. Kamban, Tulsidas etc had
second rights at other times, again direct telecasts only.

Original is Rama's itself. Copy right belongs to Rama and not to
Valmiki. Valmiki had the greatest privilege of getting the grant from
Lord himself.

Ratnakar becomes Valmiki who infact 'documents' the ramayana.

I will be taking of the grace which Lord gave to Alwars and others if
I say that they got the Ramayana based out of their studies from
Valmiki's words.

Beeshma recites Sahasranama on battle field, does it mean he is the
owner of Sahasranama ? Ofcourse, only Beeshma was up in the level of
getting the relay of Sahasranama.

If you observe, he not only sees Lord in 1000 forms, he also hears
(and relays) conversations of Lord Shiva and devas etc. The
Sahasranama apparently, is live relay, if we tune ourselves to that
level - we can also see/hear it...
Ramayana and Mahabaratha did happen and.... did not happen also...
purely depends on the intellectual level of the reader. Perfect
example of saying deivam endral athu deivam. I believe they happened
and are happening every time, the time here is the question of eons...

FYI:My Guru Arunagiri has written a lot of Ramayana scenes which I
dont remember reading in some of the Ramayana versions. His visions
are obviously not from any books.

again.... intha vaaram kuzappam vaaram

-- R

literally and getting overtly sentimental over whether or not it is
fact. First of all those of us who are keen on history should not be
very upset when it is not in line with our sentiments (including PS
itself). There are many parts of the RAmayana that are completely
unpalatable - we talked about the horse sacrifice once before. Paula

ordeal and the Uttara Ramayana clearly appear to be interpolations

Mahabharat very much.

Dear Satish,

--- Inponniyinselvan@yahoogroups.com, "SatishKumar Arunachalam"
wrote:
Deepak,

You yourself said it is an interpretation. Interpretation are not
original and hence its duplicate. I am great fan of Kamba Ramayanam,
though I have not read it. Just a glimpse of it made me to start
loving tamil literature. So I have great regards for that.
Interpretation is a wrong word in my opinion. Surely Kamban had
followed the original by Valmiki while making certain modificiations.
This, as Deepak said, however does not take away the historicity of
Ramayana, from Kamban. It is just that we Tamizhs did not classify it
as history, but as ilakkiyam. Whatever name we give it, Kamban's work
is atleast 90% faithful to the original. The remaining 10% falls into
the category that Malathis says, "creepers". This is a very
inevitable one.

But onething for sure is that Ramayana has made a very deep impact in
many parts of South and South East Asia, that every one
started "localizing" it for their pleasures, just like a Remake
Movie. The pathetic part is some of those localisations had resulted
in complete deviation from the original, like the ones, where Sita
and Rama are said to be siblings or Sita is said to Ravana's daughter
etc. But all these variations, if analysed properly (I haven't done
it) will definitely bring out a common holding point faithful to the
original (hi)story line. In this case of Ramayana, this could be the
win of Dharmic forces over Adharmic forces.

The reason I said this is, the word "duplicate", to my opinion,
seemed to be too raw and was giving a very bad picture about Kamban.
As you know the usage of the word Duplicate is done to point out to a
very cheap replica of original. Surely Kamban's treatise is not in
this category. Just that I did not like the word "Duplicate" for all
the greatness of Kamban's work


But we should accept that, Kambar, took the main story line and
modified many a things, like characterization of many characters
(Actually I should say polish). Kambars character might look much
better that valmiki's, because valmiki gave things as is, where as
Kambar polished it to make it more suitable for tamil literature.
This is absolutely correct. Yes, Kamban has indeed tuned it in line
with the "pazhantamizhar marabu". This was a BIT different from the
ones followed in Northern India. So as rightly pointed by you, Kamban
wrote it to suit the views based on demographical variations.

For example, Ravana carrying Sita episode. Valimiki presents this as
Ravana, holding Sita's hands while pulling away. (Example of what
Satish told regarding Valmiki presenting it as it is) While Kamban,
portrays is saying that Ravana just took the entire piece of land in
which Sita and Ravana was standing. This was well in line with
Tamizhar Marabu where in a woman is not supposed to be touched by
anyone other than her husband.

Every such variations based on local customs and traditions is very
much present in Ramayana.

Regards,
Venkatesh
